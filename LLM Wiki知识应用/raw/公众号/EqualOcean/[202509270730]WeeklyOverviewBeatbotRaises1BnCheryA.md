** 1\. Beatbot, selected for EqualOcean 2025 GoGlobal Emerging Brand Top 50
list, Raises RMB 1 Billion in Latest Funding;  **

【Event】  Igh-end pool robot brand Beatbot (星迈创新) has recently completed a new
financing round of RMB 1 billion (USD 137 million), led solely by Long-Z
Investment (美团龙珠). Existing investors also increased their stakes. According
to the company, the funds will focus on R&D and product iteration,
accelerating the expansion of global sales channels and brand influence,
attracting top global talent, and exploring strategic layouts in new
application scenarios.

For years, the pool robot market has been dominated by a few international
manufacturers, with limited innovation, homogeneous functions, and slow
progress in intelligence, failing to meet demand for efficient cleaning. This
market gap has provided Beatbot with an opportunity. As China’s first pool
robot brand to surpass an average unit price of USD 2,000, Beatbot focuses on
the premium user segment, advancing product upgrades across four dimensions:
intelligence, mobility, cleaning capacity, and user experience.

Meanwhile, on September 19, 2025, Beatbot was selected for EqualOcean’s 2025
GoGlobal Emerging Brands Top 50 list. This recognition highlights the
company’s rapid growth and globalization progress, earning dual validation
from capital markets and the industry.

  

** 2\. China's Ministry of Commerce and Eight Other Departments Roll Out 13
Measures to Boost Service Exports;  **

【Event】  On September 24, Chinese authorities announced 13 measures to boost
services exports and promote high-quality trade in services. According to the
Ministry of Commerce, these steps aim to accelerate services trade, expand
opening-up, and foster new growth momentum. Measures include leveraging
government funds to support digital services, high-end design, R&D, supply
chains, and green services, while guiding funds will attract private
investment. The plan also improves efficiency in services export tax rebates,
expands export credit insurance, and optimizes visa policies for foreign
enterprises, researchers, and high-level talent, as well as facilitating
cross-border service trade payments by encouraging banks to improve settlement
efficiency for compliant companies.

**  
**

** 3\. Backed by IDG Capital, Chery Automobile Debuts on Hong Kong Stock
Exchange With Nearly HKD 200 Billion Valuation;  **

【Event】  On September 25, Chery Automobile (奇瑞汽车, 9973.HK) was officially
listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange at an issue price of HKD 30.75 per
share. The stock opened more than 11% higher, giving the company a market
capitalization close to HKD 200 billion, making it the largest car-making IPO
in Hong Kong this year. Founded in 1997, Chery has ranked as China’s No. 1
independent passenger car exporter for 22 consecutive years. As of August
2025, its products have been sold in over 100 countries and regions worldwide,
with cumulative sales exceeding 13 million vehicles. Recent investments from
IDG Capital have supported its global expansion, equity optimization, and new
business development.

  

** 4\. Backed by OpenAI, 1X Technologies Aims for Up to USD 1 Billion Funding,
Valuation May Top USD 10 Billion;  **

【Event】  According to a Bloomberg report, robot company 1X Technologies —
backed by OpenAI — is seeking up to USD 1 billion in new funding, targeting a
valuation of at least USD 10 billion, surging more than twelvefold from its
USD 820 million valuation in January.Founded in 2014, 1X initially focused on
humanoid robot development. After OpenAI’s investment in 2023, it quickly rose
to prominence and attracted backers including Tiger Global and Samsung NEXT.
This year, the company launched its home robot Neo Gamma, and collaborated
with NVIDIA. It gained widespread attention for the viral moment where a robot
“delivered a jacket” to Jensen Huang. Currently, 1X plans to conduct tests in
hundreds to thousands of homes, and has relocated its headquarters to Palo
Alto, California.

  

** 5\. Chinese Autonomous Driving Firms Enter Singapore With Local Partners;
**

【Event】  Reuters reported on September 20 that two Chinese autonomous driving
companies will launch driverless shuttle services in Singapore with local
partners. Ride-hailing platform Grab said it has been selected by authorities
to operate two routes in Punggol from early 2026, using 5- and 8-seater models
developed by WeRide (文远知行). Vehicles will first run tests to ensure safety.
Pony.ai (小马智行) announced a tie-up with ComfortDelGro to run a 12-km route in
Punggol, with commercial service expected within months of regulatory
approval, according to Singapore’s Land Transport Authority. Both firms
already operate autonomous fleets overseas. WeRide secured approval in
Shanghai this July, while Pony.ai runs services in Beijing, Shanghai,
Guangzhou and Shenzhen, and is expanding abroad with Toyota backing. Pony.ai
raised USD 260 million in its Nasdaq listing this November, aiming for a fleet
of 1,000 vehicles by year-end.

** 6\. Pop Mart Enters Mexico With Official Online Store, Highlighting Labubu;
**

【Event】  Chinese designer toy brand Pop Mart (泡泡玛特) has launched its official
online store in Mexico, featuring the popular Labubu (拉布布) series with weekly
product updates every Thursday at 8 p.m. According to Marketing4ecommerce, Pop
Mart toys have gained strong momentum in Mexico and globally over the past
year, reshaping e-commerce trends. The new store will expand product variety
and sales channels, offering local consumers exclusive and limited-edition
items. Besides Labubu, shoppers can purchase blind boxes, figurines, and
oversized dolls from other IPs such as Twinkle Twinkle (星星人), Crybaby (哭娃),
and Pucky (毕奇). Prices range from MXN 319 to MXN 3,000, with some limited
editions reaching MXN 8,000.
