Oil Well Drilling Process: A Step-by-Step Overview  
  
The drilling of an oil well involves multiple phases of precise operations.
Here we outline key stages based on a standard casing program:

Phase 1: Surface Hole Preparation  
Before drilling begins, a rig move and rig up are completed. The crew picks up
5" drill pipe (DP)for preliminary operations. A pre-spud meeting ensures all
personnel understand safety protocols. The 34" hole is drilled using a 34"
BHA(Bottom Hole Assembly), followed by hole cleaning with Hi-Vis pills. After
installing 30" conductor casing, cementing operations (CMT) secure the casing.
Workers then cut the conductor and prepare for the next phase.

Phase 2: Intermediate Sections  
For the 28" hole, a wiper trip verifies hole stability before running 24"
casing. Similar procedures apply to subsequent sections (22"→18-5/8" casing;
16"→13-3/8" casing). Critical steps include hydrostatic testing of casings,
sealing annulus welds, and installing safety equipment like the 13-5/8" BOP
(Blowout Preventer), which undergoes rigorous pressure tests.

Phase 3: Production Zone & Completion  
The final 12-1/4" and 8-1/2" holes target hydrocarbon reservoirs. Advanced
tools like GR-MLWD-ALD(Gamma Ray Measurement-While-Drilling with Azimuthal
Gamma/Resistivity) guide directional drilling. After displacing drilling
fluids to AOM(Active Oil Mud), the ESP completion (Electrical Submersible
Pump) is installed. The well is secured with a X-Mas tree after thorough
function tests.
