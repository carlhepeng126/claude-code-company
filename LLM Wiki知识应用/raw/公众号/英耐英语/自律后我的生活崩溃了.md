** 提到“自律”，总会自然而然地想到辛苦，想到严格遵循日复一日的时间表，时时刻刻保持着战斗状态，向着目标冲刺。

  

很多人知道自律的好处，但生活就像坐过山车一样，坚持了一个月，第二个月又放弃了，努力学习了几天，又开始自暴自弃了，因为自律实在太辛苦……  
** 但这是真正的自律吗？  ** 以下的视频将深深触动你……  

![](https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/mmbiz_gif/2iaIDwqRtG55iax8kkhf1ygAXTdHVX8y7SDxzwwfoxGyhdFCe3GaXKxuVrXLwGZCUIwCMNE6u4iaskMN2XOADaGSw/640?wx_fmt=gif)

  

  
Over the last few years, Dani unsuccessfully tried dieting, working out, and
studying consistently. And the reason she failed is because she believes in
one of the greatest myths of this generation–self-discipline. She says to
herself, "I need to find a good diet plan, workout, and study schedule and
just stick to it.I need to be consistent and whip myself into shape.  "
在过去几年中，丹妮尝试了控制饮食健身和坚持学习，但都没有成功，而她失败的原因是因为，她相信当代最受追捧的谬论之一——自律。她对自己说，"我需要找到一个好的饮食锻炼还有学习的计划，并坚持下去，我需要不断地鞭策自己”。  
But this belief hasn't worked out well for Dani. Her life is a constant roller
coaster ride. She diets for a few weeks before relapsing back into unhealthy
foods. She works out for a month and then quits the next month. She studies
hard for a few days and then binges Netflix for the next few. She's always
going up and down on this roller coaster, never finding true, lasting change.
但这样的信念对丹妮来说，并没有什么用。她的生活就像坐过山车一样，她控制饮食几周后，又重新开始吃不健康的食物，她锻炼了一个月，然后在第二个月就放弃了，她努力学习了几天，然后在接下来的几天里狂看网飞，她总是在像坐过山车一样上上下下，从未找到真正的、持久的改变。  
Dani doesn't understand that self-discipline will never lead to success. Self-
discipline means dividing oneself into two halves, the sculptor and the clay,
them older and the molded, with one-half trying to control the other. This
division creates inner conflict, and the creation of inner conflict can never
lead to inner peace, and no matter how someone defines success for themselves,
at some level, it always comes back to a form of inner peace or the end of
inner conflict.
丹妮不明白，自律永远无法让人成功。自律意味着将自己分为两部分：既是雕塑家又是泥土，既是铸工又是模具，用你的一半试图控制另一半，这种分裂会造成内心的冲突，而内心冲突的产生会导致内心无法平静，而无论一个人如何定义自己的成功，在某种程度上来说，成功终归是某种形式的内心平静或内心冲突的结束。  
So self-discipline can never lead to success,and in fact, it leads to the
exact opposite–self-imprisonment.When looked at plainly, self-discipline is a
ridiculous idea. Most things can't change themselves–they need something
external to do it. For example, a glass of water can't turn itself red, but
with the addition of dye, it can change. When Dani uses self-discipline, she
divides herself into two halves, trying to get one-half to act on the other.
因此，自律永远无法带来成功。事实上，它导致的结果恰恰相反：自我禁锢。直截了当的来看，自律其实是一个很荒谬的想法，大多数事物无法改变自身，它们需要外部的东西来做到这一点。例如，一杯水不可能自己变成红色，但当有颜料加入，就可以发生变化，当丹妮试图靠自律来约束自己时，她其实是将自己分为了两半，试图让一半的自己来管控另一半。  

![](https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/mmbiz_png/2iaIDwqRtG55cICKF2Mob0mB09XkuNlpf4K8VGyibQNwNDYwJfK3iclefPmn6aP59H8Iel9Wkfz7leF61T4SrgX4A/640?wx_fmt=png)

  
But this is like pouring one glass of water into two separate glasses, and
then pouring them back into the same one, hoping something new will happen.
Self-discipline is a waste of time, because it never leads to lasting change.
True change comes from exposure to novelty,and it's much more like a chemical
reaction. Dani's friend Sam is captain of her college basketball team and an
honours student. And one day, Dani asked her how she stayed in such great
shape and did so well in school.She asked:"What's your secret?  "
但这就像把一杯水分开倒入两个杯子，然后再倒回同一个杯子里，期望着会有什么变化发生。自律是浪费时间，因为它无法导致持久的改变，真正的改变来自于对新事物的接触，这更像是一种化学反应。丹妮的朋友萨姆是她大学篮球队的队长，同时也是一名很优秀的学生。有一天，丹妮问她，她是如何保持这么好的身材，同时在学业上也表现出色，“你有什么秘诀？”她问道。  
Sam stared at her blankly.  "  Secret? There's no secret. I like staying
active and studying, that's all.  "  Dani was disappointed. She just doesn't
want to tell me, she thought. So Dani began watching Sam's actions and
imitating them. If Sam ran in the morning, Dani began running in the morning.
If Sam ate eggs for breakfast, Dani began eating eggs. If Sam studied for four
hours a day, Dani began studying for four hours a day.
萨姆茫然地看着她“秘诀？根本没什么秘诀。我只是喜欢运动和学习”，丹妮感到很失望，她想，她只是不愿意告诉我。所以丹妮开始观察萨姆的行为，并进行模仿，萨姆早上跑步，丹妮也开始早上跑步，如果萨姆早餐吃鸡蛋，那丹妮也会开始在早餐时吃鸡蛋，萨姆每天学习四个小时，丹妮也开始每天学习四个小时。  
But all of these behaviours were inauthentic for Dani: she wasn't a morning
person, she hated eggs, and her life was too busy for four continuous hours of
studying, so when she did that, it always meant she had to cancel something
else that was important to her. Eventually Dani returned to her old habits.
But Dani's wasn't the only one who tried copying Sam. Everyone admired Sam's
achievements, and so many people tried imitating her actions, thinking they
could be her. Ironically, what made Sam Sam was that she didn't think about
anyone else's actions.
但所有这些行为对丹妮来说都并非发自内心：她不是个早起的人。她讨厌鸡蛋，她的生活忙碌，很难连续学习四个小时，所以在她模仿萨姆时，总是意味着她不得不取消其他一些对她很重要的事情。最终，丹妮回归到了她的旧习惯，但丹妮并非唯一一个想要学习萨姆的人。大家都很佩服萨姆的成就，很多人都想模仿她的行动，想着他们也可以做到像她那样。讽刺的是，萨姆之所以能成为萨姆，就是因为她并不在乎他人的行为。  
Everyone tried to be Sam, but Sam didn't try to be anyone but herself.
Everyone else tried forcing their behaviour in a certain direction, but Sam's
behaviour was completely unforced. She had incredible self-knowledge which she
obtained at a young age. Sam noticed that Dani was copying her and failing,
and so one day, she decided to tell her a story.  "  When I was only two, my
mother passed away, leaving my father to raise me all alone. And he spent as
much time with me as he could, helping me learn a lot about myself.
每个人都想成为萨姆，但萨姆只想做自己，其他人都想强制让自己的行为向着某个方向转变，但萨姆的行为完全是自发性的，她有着非常清楚的自我认知，这是她从小培养的。萨姆注意到，丹妮在模仿她，而且失败了。于是有一天，她决定告诉她一个故事“在我只有两岁时，我的母亲去世了，留我的父亲独自抚养我长大，他尽可能多地陪伴我，帮助我了解自己。  
![](https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/mmbiz_png/2iaIDwqRtG55cICKF2Mob0mB09XkuNlpftBx1tPky8yUcmWKQvNGMTQiaw1zHD2Q1Le6PBPnzIaIVH8OY4OTmkJg/640?wx_fmt=png)  
He exposed me to a lot of sports as a child, and I quickly learned that I
liked basketball. So he played with me every weekend. And each day, for
breakfast, he made me all kinds of different foods, and I found out that I
liked eggs. So he made me different kinds of eggs each morning, scrambled,
hard boiled, poached. He never forced me to study any specific subject and
never said that one's more important than another.
他让我在小时候接触了很多运动，我很快就知道我喜欢篮球，于是他每个周末都陪我一起玩，每天的早餐他都会给我做不同的食物，我发现我喜欢鸡蛋，所以他每天早上给我做不同种类的鸡蛋，炒的、水煮的、荷包蛋。他从来没有强迫我学习任何特定的科目，也没有说过哪个比哪个更重要。  
So I ended up following my interest in college: physics. And because I love
physics so much, I study a lot and somehow get good grades. I guess what I'm
trying to say is, there's no secret to the things I do. I really, genuinely
like doing them. And I guess I had the good luck of having a father who loved
me enough to help me feel safe exploring the world, and that exploration
allowed me to find the things that worked for me."
所以最终我得以在大学里追随我的兴趣——物理学。因为我非常热爱物理学，我花很多时间去学习，取得了很好的成绩。我想说的只是，我所做的事情没有什么秘诀，我发自内心的喜欢做这些事情，我想我很幸运有一个很爱我的父亲，愿意鼓励我探索这个世界，而这样的探索让我能够找到适合我的东西。
”  
After hearing Sam's story, Dani decided she needed to approach her life
differently, without self-discipline. Dani began a new journey of exploration,
trying new foods from the grocery store each day, testing out different forms
of exercise, and looking up different majors to study. Eventually, she found
alternatives that she could maintain without forcing them upon herself, and in
the end, she ended up losing weight and making the honour roll.
在听了萨姆的故事后，丹妮决定她要以不同的方式去生活，不再依靠自律，丹妮开始了新的探索之旅，每天从商店买不同的食物尝试，尝试不同形式的运动，并找来不同的专业知识学习。最终，她找到了不需要强迫自己就可以持续下去的方案。最后，她终于减肥成功，成绩也变得优秀。  
When one finds what actually works, like Sam and Dani eventually did, there's
no self-discipline. There's no inner conflict. The action is something one
genuinely wants to do because it works for them.And true change doesn't come
by trying to control oneself, but through exploration and encounters with
something new. True change is a chemical reaction. When we use self-
discipline, it means we're walking someone else's path, and walking some
else's path will only lead to inner conflict. The only way is our own way, and
that's why self-discipline is a waste of time.
当一个人找到适合自己的东西，就像萨姆和丹妮最终做的那样，根本不需要自律，也不会有内心的冲突，这些行为都是一个人真正想做的。因为这些适合他们，真正的改变并不是通过试图控制自己来实现的，而是通过探索和遇到新的东西，真正的改变应该是化学反应，当我们试图通过自律来约束自己时，这意味着我们在走别人的路，而走别人的路，只会导致内心的冲突和矛盾。真正行之有效的，只有走自己的路，这就是为什么我们说自律只是在浪费时间。
