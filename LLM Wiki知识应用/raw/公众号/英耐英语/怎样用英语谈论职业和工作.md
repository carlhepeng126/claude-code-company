“工作”，使得我们可以实现自我价值，并且创造利益以满足生活需求。

  

不过你知道如何用英文介绍工作情况吗，不同的职业用英文该怎么说呢？

  

“What is your job?” 这个问题结构是对的，但听起来不礼貌也不自然。今天来分享几个常用的表达。

  

** Ways to ask  **

我们可以调整一下措辞，让这个问题听起来更自然。  当你在社交场合遇到某人，你想知道他们做什么工作，最常见的问题是：  
** What do you do?  ** 或者：  ** What do you do for a living?  **  
例：  A: What do you do?  B: I  '  m a data engineer.  
A: What do you do for a living?  B: I'm a musician.  
另外两种友好的询问方式是：  ** What kind of work do you do?  ** ** What line of work are
you in?  **  
** "  ** ** What is your profession?  ** ** "  **
这个说法一般在正式场合使用，比如工作面试，在日常交流中不会用到。  

** Following up  **  

在对方回答之后，可以看情况再问一两个问题深入了解。看一个例子：  
  
A: What do you do for a living?  B: I  '  m a data engineer.  A: Nice!  **How
long have you been doing that?** 你干这一行多久了。  B: For about five years now.  A:
**Where do you work?** 你在哪里工作？  B: At the city  '  s software park.  

** Not to do  **  

另一件要知道的事是，我们不会主动去问一个人的工资，在工作中更是高度保密的信息。除非有人和你分享这些信息，或者向你咨询薪资建议。

**Professions & Occupations **  
**Accountant** \- a person that works with the money and accounts of a
company.  **Actor /Actress** \- a person that acts in a play or a movie.
**Architect** \- a person that designs building and houses.  **Astronomer** \-
a person who studies the stars and the universe.  **Author** \- They write
books or novels.  **Baker** \- They make bread and cakes and normally work in
a bakery.  **Bricklayer** \- a person that helps to build houses by using
bricks.  **Bus driver** \- a person that drives buses.  **Butcher** \- a
person that works with meat. They cut the meat and sell it in their shops.
**Carpenter** \- a person that makes things from wood including houses and
furniture.  **Chef/Cook** \- a person that prepared food for others, often in
a restaurant or café.  **Cleaner** \- a person that cleans/tidies an area or
place (such as in an office)  **Dentist** \- a person that can fix problems
you have with your teeth.  **Designer** \- a person who has the job of
designing things.  **Doctor** \- a person you go to see when you are ill or
have some type of health problem.  **Dustman/Refuse collector** \- a person
that collects trash/rubbish from bins in the street.  **Electrician** \- a
person that works with electric circuits.  **Engineer** \- a person who
develops solutions to technical problems. They sometimes design, build, or
maintain engines, machines, structures or public works.  **Factory worker** \-
a person that works in a factory.  ** Farmer  ** \- a person that works on a
farm, usually with animals.  **Fireman/Fire fighter** \- a person that puts
out fires.  **Fisherman** \- a person that catches fish.  **Florist** \- a
person that works with flowers.  **Gardener** \- a person that keeps gardens
clean and tidy. They take care of the plants in the garden.  **Hairdresser**
\- they cut your hair or give it a new style.  **Journalist** \- a person that
makes new reports in writing or through television.  **Judge** \- a qualified
person that decides cases in a law court.  **Lawyer** \- a person that defends
people in court and gives legal advice.  **Lecturer** \- a person that gives
lectures, usually in a university.  **Librarian** \- a person that works in a
library.  **Lifeguard** \- a person that saves lives and protects swimmers
from accidents (usually at a beach or swimming pool).  **Mechanic** \- a
person that repairs machines, especially car motors.  **Model** \- a (usually
attractive) person that works in fashion, modeling clothes and accessories.
**Newsreader** \- a person that reads the news, normally on broadcast or
television.  **Nurse** \- a person trained to help a doctor look after the
sick or injured person.  **Optician** \- a person that checks your eyes and
try and correct problems with your sight.  **Painter** \- a person that paints
pictures or a worker who is employed to cover objects with paint (usually the
interior and exterior of buildings).  **Pharmacist** \- a qualified person
that works with and dispenses medicine.  **Photographer** \- a person that
takes photos.  **Pilot** \- a person who flies a plane.  **Plumber** \- a
person that repairs water systems or pipes of your house.  **Politician** \- a
person who works in politics.  **Policeman/Policewoman** \- a member of the
police force. They (try and) prevent crimes.  **Postman** \- a person that
delivers mails to your house.  ** Real estate agent  ** \- a person that makes
money from selling land for development.  **Receptionist** \- a person that is
at the reception (entrance) of a company, whose main duty is to answer the
telephone and receive visitors.  **Scientist** \- a person that works in the
science industry. They do many experiments.  **Secretary** \- a person
employed in an office who types letters, keeps records etc.  **Shop
assistant** \- a person that works in a shop or store selling products.
**Soldier** \- a person who works for the army.  **Tailor** \- a person that
makes clothes for others, mainly producing exclusive items of clothing.
**Taxi driver** \- a person who drives a taxi.  **Teacher** \- a person that
passes knowledge to students, usually at school.  **Translator** \- a person
that translates from one language to another.  **Traffic warden** \- a person
that patrols areas to check that people do not park in the wrong place.
**Travel agent** \- a person that organises and sells holiday plans and
flights for others.  **Veterinary doctor (Vet)** \- a qualified person that
looks after sick animals.  **Waiter/Waitress** \- a person that works in a
food outlet, looking after customers and serving food.  **Window cleaner** \-
a person that cleans windows, normally the windows of tall buildings.  ** **
