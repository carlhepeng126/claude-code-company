** 文  ** ｜何会泽（东欧及独联体事业部）

  

![](https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/mmbiz_jpg/x3uBG25kUEo0YYv1NgjF0xoknRXK8c9OsSuhKKulXCn0VocKlmjLpY2yEbvVsjdsRRoWlGGGK7DLueamPY2Ovg/640?wx_fmt=jpeg)

  
In 2022, you worked on our project site in Belarus, and you have bought a
ticket to China in January to celebrate the Spring Festival with your family.
Two weeks before departure, when you were just thinking about what to buy for
your family, when suddenly you received a text message: Due to the control of
the epidemic,  ** your flight was cancelled  ** . Your heart sank, so you
changed for available date, still hopefully, - May. As May was approaching,
there seems to be a faint hope when you saw the mounting number of increased
cases in local hospitals. You were aware that your flight in May would be also
destined to screw up. Later, you got yourself a seat on a flight in July, but
you are sure that even if you were “stranded”, you would already be used to it
as your life routines.

  

I believe this is exactly what has happened to many of my coworkers in the
past few years working on overseas projects. Actually, they’ve experienced  **
more daunting challenges or difficulties than this  ** . But they have never
bothered with it, still remaining  **optimistic, passionate and giving full
dedication to their works and work commitment** .

  

On our Belarus agro-industrial project, our team took  **immediate action and
formulated contingency plan** since the outbreak of the pandemic. Colleagues
in all positions got involved in the plan. Although they are all ordinary
people and never boast of their contributions, I think it would be impossible
for the project to achieve today's success without their dedication and sense
of responsibility.

  

In early 2020, when the pandemic broke out uncontrollably in Belarus, my co-
worker, Li Shuhui, a young colleague on the project, became  ** a member of
the logistics support working party  ** for COVID-19 prevention and supplies.
Her first task was to  ** buy masks  ** , but because local people were
crazily storing up masks and sterilizer, masks were out of stock in most shops
in Belarus. She  **tried hundreds of pharmacy, both offline and online** .
After constant attempt, she finally found a local medical supplier who was
willing to accept her order. she said it was  **such a relief to see masks
delivered on our construction site** . Even to today, the epidemic seems to
have been settled, she still keeps in touch with the supplier, because she
feels it is her duty to always be ready for further epidemic emergencies.

  

There are many colleagues like her on our project. They are on the front line
of fighting the pandemic. Not only did the employees do their best, but the
leaders also  ** played a leading and exemplary role  ** in this fight. For
our project manager Dai Hongxian, maintaining the morale among employees has
become his top priority in this crucial moment. In October last year, there
were a few workers who were infected in the subcontracting team. During the
National Day Holidays, Mr. Dai visited the quarantine site in person to
comfort and cheer them up, which  **renders these workers in high spirit and
hopeful of quick recovery** . He also has held many meetings to inform the
overall situation and integral preventative measures for further contingency,
so that most employees can feel reassured and relieved; as he said, “  ** The
epidemic is not that scary, optimism is the best immunity.  ** ” It is
precisely this kind of confidence and belief that allows our project to move
forward smoothly in face of the raging epidemic.

  

Looking back on the vigorous anti-epidemic defense battle on our project, I
recalled the scenes from the most famous masterpiece by  ** Tolstoy "War and
Peace"  ** . In face of the raging epidemic, we are both fighting on material
and spiritual battlefield. Isn't this a war of our time? And  ** the victory
of this war belongs to people who hold firm beliefs and act firmly  ** . From
"War and Peace" to our Agro-industrial Complex Project, I seem to have seen
the connection between the past and the present by the same belief in
perseverance, and unity.  **Victory came from Tolstoy's pen, and victory also
belongs to our overseas team and to every CITIC person!** Thank you for
listening.

colleagues.

  

I was planning to take a break in the end of 2021, but the flight was cut off
and I had to ** change the tickets for 5 times  ** . But this I already told
(in my speech).

  

But I was moved by the confidence of my colleagues. That means they have been
faced with the same problem but they always ** make jokes about themselves and
about the others  ** , and I think, we should face this problem with optimism.

to me  ** there. Thank you very much!
