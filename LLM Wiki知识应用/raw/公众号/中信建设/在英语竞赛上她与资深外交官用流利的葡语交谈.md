**_Team CITIC_ **

**_The Audacity of Dream_ **

  

On this stage, many had shared their stories and dream, the sparkle in their
heart becomes a torch, passing on generations. Their spirit inspired me and
brought me here today.

  

![](https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/mmbiz_jpg/x3uBG25kUErvsUeLI9cvZZII2icUKuKPGtEIfajzwlMib7cLtAdzO6KZqkTWd2aqSy6CqlEwzbSalEHia6FlrUUlQ/640?wx_fmt=jpeg)

  

This is a photo taken on the 17th of July, 2019. It was ceremony of the
signing of Contract for 35,000 houses in Mozambique.

  

It’s a moment of dream come true, it is amoment of dream come true, for the
team that worked day and night for years to make it happen. It is an important
milestone for the sustainable development of our overseas market.

  

Years ago, our focus was in the ongoing projects in Angola, aside our
management already started planning for the next twenty years, because what we
seek is not the success of one or two projects, our goal is Built to Last.

  

Before 2014, things were well. Then the oil price went down, and the
Government was having difficulty paying bills, because tit was a catastrophic
stroke for the Angolan oil-led Economy. SONANGOL on the left, stopped being
our owner, and a small private developer stepped in. You can find their
offices in the bush. Not only did the new entity unable to make payments, they
were very difficult when coming to the taking over of projects. Not only were
they unable to make payments. Also, social safety deteriorated badly, because
abductions, robberies happened on via expresso, the only road we had to take
several times a day to go downtown. So, risks wenthigh, but yields no. Winter
is coming.

  

Having Angola as our base for several years, given the unfavorable
circumstances, we had to look for possibilities elsewhere, meanwhile, we
trained our employees skills that they might beneeding in the future.

  

To reassure our Clients of their doubts, we invited them to Angola to see for
their own eyes. Our camp in Kilamba is 40km from downtown, so our team had to
get up around 3 in the morning to fetch the delegation from the airport. But
we were hell-bent. So. After this visit,the, the Minister, getting to know our
team and projects, the Minister was impressed and promised to invite the
president, the Head of State of Mozambique, to Beijing. During the visit of
the President, he said he wanted acopy of the project that we did in Angola in
Mozambique. So, we earned their trust. After months of negotiation, the
Mozambican part was finally satisfied with our quotation and proposals. My
colleague told me, the final version of contract had 3000 pages and the
Representative of the Owner had to sign formore than an hour, but they said it
was worth it. They said they were looking forward to work with a powerful
team.

  

Powerful indeed. Because, for five years, all members of the division we
strived and thrived. We know better because more challenges are yet to come,
but we are not afraid, we will keep the spirit up. We are team CITIC; we have
the audacity of dream. Look out, here we come.

Q:  This project is very important for everybody. In the project you were
talking about, what’s your personal contributions.

  

A: Thank you very much for the question. In the early stage of Mozambique
project, we had a pile of Portuguese documents to be translated into Chinese.
And also this bilingual translation, because even we could talk in English
when we worked, the written documents had to be in Portuguese, as the contract
requested. So, well in our division, each translator or each interpreter works
in a different department, for example logistics, human resources, finance,
they have different specialties. But when it comes to something so urgent, so
important, everybody has to be trained in the same room of contractual
conditions, we can work together for two or three days, a week or longer if
necessary. For a translation like this, because sometimes if we subcontract
such a task to a foreign service provider, for example an interpretation
company, they might not understand exactly of what we were doing and get the
result we want, so I participated in that progress, thank you very much.

Q: Mr. di asked me what I like the most about Angola and also what I think
CITIC can do with what we already have in Angola.

  

A: In Angola, I was giving an example, that our owner or our clients，they are
not just colleagues, sometimes they are actually our friends. Because we are
so far away from our country land, from our friends and families, when we have
difficulties, eventhough we do not count on that they could help us, but when
we called they helped, it was very moving for me. And it is a proof, that
apart from the business relationship, we are having a strong relation,
personal relationship with theses people. They trust us, trust us as a person,
trust us as a company, so in the future this is our core, one of our core
competitiveness in our business because we already have these friends, who’s
going to help us, and we are going to help them, thank you very much.

Q: You have been working in Africa for many years, so I’d like to know from
your point of view, what’s the core competitiveness of CITIC CONSTRUCTION in
Africa?  

  

A: Thank you very much for your question. Well. Taken as an example of our
success in Angola, I think we should take a look at six aspects. The first one
is national strategy, before, we have Going out, now we have the Belt and Road
Initiative, it means that the country’s support and resources are much more
available tous. The second is the influence of the brand of CITIC GROUP and
CITICCONSTRUCTION. And the third is our successful business mode, which is the
Fleet of Alliance, which means we are not going out alone, we have the support
andresources of our subcontractors, which will be with us all along the way in
allof the points of our projects. I thinks that’s the third one, and the forth
isour focus on the construction of our culture of our colleagues, because we
value the importance of human recourses and we give great platform, such as
today, for our employees to show  their talents and to find their
owndirections, on this great platform. And the next one, I think it’s
important, social responsibility, or corporate responsibility.
