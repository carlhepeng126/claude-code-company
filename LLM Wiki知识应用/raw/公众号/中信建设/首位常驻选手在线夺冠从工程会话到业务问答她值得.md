20➡6

NEGOTIATIONS ON THE  

PROJECT CONCESSION AGREEMENT

  
** Tan Ruiqing:  ** CATIC is a leading international engineering contractor.
Recently, CATIC has won the bid for East-West Highway Concession in Angola,
and now they are having a meeting with the Ministry of Infrastructure
Development of Angola, to negotiate the concession agreement.  

  

** Participants:  **

** International Contracting Company — CATIC:  
**

Mr. Gao – Hello everyone. I am Gao Yang. In this conversation, I will be Mr.
Gao, Vice President of company CATIC （高扬）

Mr. Liu – Hello, everyone, this is Liu Junjie. In this conversation, I will be
Mr. Liu, the director of Engineering Department of CATIC （刘俊杰）

Ms. Tan - Hello everyone, my name is Tan Ruiqing. In this conversation, I will
be Ms. Tan, the director of Commercial Department of CATIC （谭睿晴）

** Ministry of Infrastructure Development of Angola:  
**

Mr. António - Vice Minister: Hello everyone, I am Zhao Buyi. In this
conversation, I will be Mr. António, Vice Minister of Infrastructure Ministry
Angola (赵老师)

Ms. Lídia- Director of Road Department: Hello, my name is Chen Zhuo. In this
conversation I am Ms. Lídia, the director of Road Department（陈卓）

  

** Mr. Gao:  ** Good morning, honorable vice minister. As your request we are
here to discuss the project Concession Agreement.  

  

** Mr. António:  ** Good to see you all. Have you read the exposure draft of
the Concession Agreement prepared by our ministry?

  

** Ms. Tan:  ** Yes, of course. It is well structured in the internationally
prevalent template of concession agreements. It deals with all the core issues
such as concession period, obligations of both sides, the levy and collection
of fees, force majeure, etc.

  

** Ms. Lídia:  ** Do you have any amendments or comments on our draft
concession agreement, which you would like us to consider?

  

** Mr. Liu:  ** Ms. Lídia, we do have four suggestions. Firstly, we think some
important information should be compiled into Concession Agreement as
attachments, including project site locations, site delivery schedule,
technical requirements, financing structure, etc.

  

** Ms. Lídia:  ** You are right, Mr. Liu. We didn’t have enough time to
prepare the attachments. The ministry hopes that your company could help to
draft these attachments later on.

  

** Mr. Liu:  ** No problem, we will draft a set of attachments for your review
within ten days.

  

** Ms. Lídia:  ** That would be great.

  

** Mr. Liu:  ** Secondly, we suggest simply merging 5 years’ construction
period and 25 years’ O&M period into 30 years’ concession period.

  

** Mr. António:  ** I understand and agree with your intention. I believe that
the inclusion of construction period in the concession period will incentivize
early completion of the highway, and thus generate greater toll revenues.

  

** Mr. Gao:  ** And if there is any delay in the government handing over the
possession of the land required, we suggest the concession period should be
extended accordingly.

  

** Mr. António:  ** I agree, Mr. Gao. Go ahead, Mr. Liu.

  

** Mr. Liu:  ** The third suggestion is that the Concession Agreement should
set out the compensation mechanism for low traffic flow rate.

  

** Mr. António:  ** Compensation?

  

** Ms. Tan:  ** Oh, vice minister, may I remind you that the Minister has
agreed to compensate our company when the traffic flow rate is below the
break-even point.

  

** Ms. Lídia:  ** I remember that, but our ministry proposes the compensation
is provided in the form of concession period extension instead of cash.

  

** Mr. Gao:  ** Yes, the ministry agrees an extension of the concession period
in the event of a lower than expected traffic growth.

  

** Mr.Tan:  ** And conversely, we will agree that the concession period shall
be reduced if the traffic growth exceeds the expected level.

  

** Ms. Lídia:  ** That compensation mechanism seems equitable.

  

** Mr. António:  ** If no one has anything else to add, then I think we'll
wrap this up.

  

** Mr. Gao:  ** Honorable Vice Minister and Ms. Lídia, thank you for taking
time out of your busy schedules to have this meeting with us today.

  

** Mr. António:  ** See you all next week.

  

** All:  ** Bye.

  

6➡3

ENGLISH TALK SHOW  

  

** Topic: The social responsibilities that companies must fulfil when working
overseas.  **  

  

Companies should care about profits making, but at the same time, they should
also place emphasis on the fulfilling of social responsibilities, since it
significantly affect the fate of the company in long term.

  

Especially when doing business overseas, the fulfilling of social
responsibility affects not only the company’s own image, but also the national
image it represents.

  

So, when working overseas, companies should consider carefully the impact
their activities may bring to the society, and should respect local
traditions, observe the host country’s laws and regulations, protect the local
environment, create more local jobs, and help local communities.

  

That’s all, thank you.
