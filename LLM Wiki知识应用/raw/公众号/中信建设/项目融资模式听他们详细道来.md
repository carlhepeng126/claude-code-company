** Maggie:  **  

  

Ladies and gentlemen, we are from Finance department. This is Eric and Alice,
I’m Maggie. Today we are going to proudly present our golden idea, Project
finance. Before we formally get started, let’s have a look at the video of
world-famous project financed construction.

  

![](https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/mmbiz_jpg/x3uBG25kUErtbibTg9ibejz9A3oBJSSZuN62hKB6HlZuWALaibiaibJkSiaVSNUEjH6CAcD4HxdiaFeLZ6SPvwz1cpEibg/640?wx_fmt=jpeg)

  

As a contractor, undertaking these marvelous projects is our life-pursuing
dream. With Project Finance as a resilient financing method, we could explore
the market and seize more opportunities for our company.

  

In fact, we’ve used lots of traditional finance methods for our projects which
have brought us great profit for our company. Most of the financing we carried
out is Sovereign guarantee, with that, we’ve successfully raised more than
$800 million dollars for Iraq Missan power plant project. We also performed
Commercial Export Buyer's Credit, and financed 188.7 million pounds for the
British Royal Albert Docks (RAD) project.

  

However, we found some limitations when using traditional finance.

  

Firstly, we can see a decrease from sovereign guarantee with the data from
Sinosure in 2020, the target of sovereign guarantee credit has decreased
almost 50% compared with the data in previous years. So it’s much difficult
for us to get sovereign guarantee projects.

  

Secondly, with the negative effect of macroeconomy, clients find it’s hard to
meet bank credit requirements just by themselves.

  

Thirdly, traditional finance relies on the client’s balance sheet. The
repayment obligations will generate huge risks to the clients.

  

These issues mentioned above downsized our target market. To soften these
issues, we came up our golden idea – project finance.

  

So what’s project finance, Alice will give you more details.

  

![](https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/mmbiz_jpg/x3uBG25kUErtbibTg9ibejz9A3oBJSSZuN1yUmrRGq90UMbz0SQvTIvzBRVPia1LgJsQNmQtP2ayV4bcemmXQibaxw/640?wx_fmt=jpeg)

  

** Alice:  **  

  

What is Project finance? It is the funding of long-term infrastructure
projects based upon the projected cash flows of the project itself.

  

Let’s take the power plant project as an example, the client will create a SPV
company based upon the power plant. The expected cash outflow of the power
plant is consisted of the fuel cost, the operation and maintenance cost. And
the cash inflow comes from the electricity consumption of the off-takers.
Usually, Long-term contracts with suppliers and off-takers have already been
involved in the project. Through the positive net cash flow, the SPV Company
will be borrowing from the banks.

  

On our side, as a contractor, we can present the banks with necessary
calculations to show that the expected Cash Flow could cover the loan
repayment. And then we can start the project financing procedure.

  

In this way, we can see that Project finance can permit clients to extend
their debt capacity and at the same time, it can bring us more advantages than
the traditional financing methods.

  

First, Project Finance has less requirements to national credits, because it
is supported by the projected cash flows of the SPV company instead of
sovereign guarantee.

  

Second, Project Finance has limited effect on the client’s financial reports,
because the liabilities from the financing process are showed on the SPV
company’s balance sheet.

  

And the third, project finance is a risk sharing mode, it can reduce the risk
of each entity that’s involved and it doesn’t have to be taken by one entity
alone.

  

The advantages of Project Finance can exactly remove the limitations of our
traditional financing methods and most importantly, by using Project Finance,
we can enlarge the scale of our target clients and expand our market.

  

What about the future of Project Finance? Eric will tell you more.

  

** Eric:  **  

  

We can also help the client with financing process. We have maintained good
relationships with Sinosure, Export-Import Bank, China Development Bank as
well as many commercial banks, more importantly, we are familiar with the
financing process and risk preference of different financial institutions,
which will greatly accelerate the approval process. Last but not least, we
have rich financing experience in construction sector, and we are confident
about project finance.

Despite all recent results and developments, project finance has proved to be

** Q&A  **

  

** Q:  ** Thanks for the opportunity. Your presentation really brings home the
nitty-gritty of project finance. I still have some questions. You mentioned
that project finance is like the more prevailing approach of financing
compared to sovereign guarantee right now. But to me, project financing has to
rely on its projected cash flow as the only source to service its debt. When
you say projected, it means it’s not certain. So to me, it has some risks to
that. And also, project financing is through a SPV, which is bankruptcy
remote. It means banks granting loans with limited recourse to the
originator’s assets. So how do you assess the key risks involved in project
finance? And how do you mitigate these risks? Thank you.

  

** A:  ** thanks for your question. That is really a good one. I think the
main risk of project finance is the credit risk. As we mentioned in our
presentation, there are several entities that’s involved in the project, and
they are closely relevant to the project. The long term agreement should be
longer than the term of bank repayment and the loans and the interests to make
sure that the net cash flow is positive enough to cover everything. To solve
this risk we can do a lot of research before we collaborate with some off-
takers and suppliers. I’m sure by using project finance we can enlarge the
scale of our target client and expand our market.
