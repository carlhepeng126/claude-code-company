My dear family,

  

I miss you.  

  

Today marks my 430th day at the construction site of the Maisan Combined-Cycle
Power Plant Project in Iraq. As the new year begins, I would like to write
this letter to speak my mind and share with you my innermost thoughts.  

  

Dear Mom and Dad, how’s everything? I hope all is well. I have become used to
the moment I find your take-care messages pouring in my phone, whenever there
is news about terrorist attacks in Iraq. Against all odds, such as the harsh
climates, the volatile security situation and the ongoing pandemic, CITIC
Construction and the project team have faced up to those challenges and always
put safety production and COVID-19 precautions first. With a strong commitment
to safeguarding the health and safety of the employees, they have put in place
a number of safety protocols, and prepared sufficient daily necessities at the
workplace. Reassurance is built on trust. Mom and Dad, we are all safe and
sound in Maisan, because we have the company rooting for its overseas staff
members. Please don’t worry about me.  

  

My beloved daughter, I really enjoyed reading your diary. Congratulations on
joining the Young Pioneer of China. I am so proud of you! You once said that
this was your dream. Same as you, Mommy has a dream, too, which is to have the
project completed successfully someday soon. As a participant and builder of
the Maisan project, I feel honored to witness and be part of each remarkable
breakthrough. However, as your mom, I feel terribly sorry about missing out on
your growing-up moments. I’ll never forget the last time you got a fever. When
you said you wished for nothing but Mommy’s company, my sense of guilt and
shame were so overwhelming, which is beyond expression. Dedication is built on
a sense of mission. You may still be too young to understand, but as you grow
up, you will realize that “One for all” is a commitment, rather than an empty
slogan. I hope this letter can inspire you to dream big, stay true to your
goal, and walk on your chosen path.  

  

Dear husband, my faithful supporter, thank you for being a working father and
trying your best to balance work and parenting. I know how hard it would be
for you who doesn’t have the gene for cooking and housework. I am so blessed
to have your unconditional support and understanding, which empowers me to
fulfill my duties without distractions. With the concerted efforts of the
whole project team, the past year has been a fruitful one, as we achieved the
project implementation and successfully inaugurated the power plant. Now the
project has moved into the final stage. At this juncture, I am more determined
than ever to stay on, as other co-workers do. Perseverance is built on
understanding. I still recall what you said when sending me off at the
airport, “Just take that leap. I’ve got your back.” Thank you a thousand times
for being my pillar of strength all along.  

  

Dear loved ones, although we are 6000 kilometers and time-wise five hours
apart, we’re always appreciating the same moon under the same sky. I want to
send you a photo of the moon in Maisan. May it follow the silk road, and bring
my thoughts to you.  

  

The story of mine is merely an embodiment of numerous staff members at CITIC
Construction, who are genuine selfless contributors and heroes, and deserve
our respect and admiration. By dedicating this presentation, I would like to
pay my sincere tribute to them and their family members.

  

Thank you！

Thank you for your questions first. And also thank you for your encouragement.
I think this question...I have been thinking about this question before. I
think it is really hard for everyone when they got an assignment request to be
assigned overseas, and it is hard to communicate with their families to say “I
am going to work overseas, and please understand my decision and give me
support. For my case, I think the way I did is just exactly like what I’m
doing right now – I wrote a family letter regularly, because I think a family
letter can be kept and treasured as a special memory. I know with the help of
the internet, nowadays it is more convenient and faster to communicate through
social media or email, but I think a letter has its own powers –it connects us
together and it forms a special bond, especially on special occasions like the
New Year and birthdays. And my family, they were very supporting me when I
made the decision to work in the Middle East. Because they knew that, I am
determined, I want to align myself with the organization’s development, and I
want to strive for a better career. And this is the way how I can train myself
into a better person professionally. Thank you.
