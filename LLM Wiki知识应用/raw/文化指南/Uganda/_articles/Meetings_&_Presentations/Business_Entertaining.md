# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Hospitality in Uganda is a way of establishing credibility and showing care. When a Ugandan colleague or counterpart invites you to share a meal, they are offering a form of social safety and grounding. Hosting well, even modestly, reflects manners and respect. Accepting the invitation and showing genuine appreciation for what is offered builds trust more reliably than any polished presentation. Food itself holds meaning. Dishes like matooke (steamed plantain) and luwombo (stew steamed in banana leaves) are considered proper meals in many central regions, while other parts of the country have their own staples and pride foods. In urban Kampala, street food like the rolex (a rolled chapati with egg and vegetables) is widely enjoyed. Treat food offerings as a gesture of connection, not just sustenance. Acting overly cautious or dismissive of local dishes can read as distance or superiority.

Entertaining formats that allow for calm, unrushed conversation work best. A shared meal at a restaurant or a relaxed café creates space for relationships to develop without pressure. Kampala has a wide restaurant scene, from Ugandan to Indian, Chinese, Italian, and beyond, and many top hotels are considered prestigious settings for business dinners. Lunch is typically the main meal of the day, while dinners tend to be lighter and earlier, as many people have long commutes and family commitments. Reciprocating an invitation, whether during your visit or later, is expected and appreciated.

Be mindful that norms around alcohol, evening venues, and mixed-gender socializing vary across faith communities and personal values. Some Ugandans drink socially in modern urban settings, while others abstain for religious or personal reasons. Following the host's lead and choosing settings that reduce social pressure allows everyone to participate comfortably. Humor can be an effective bridge, but it should feel warm and inclusive rather than competitive or pointed. The goal is shared ease, not standing out as the most entertaining person in the room.
