# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

Bermuda's business community is small, tightly connected, and shaped by long-standing relationships. Most professionals know each other directly or through a narrow web of shared contacts. This means reputation travels fast, and an introduction from someone already trusted can open doors more quickly than a cold approach. If you're new to the island, reach out through professional networks, industry associations, or mutual connections who can vouch for your credibility. A warm introduction frames the conversation from the start and signals that you understand how business gets done locally.

Email is the standard way to request a meeting, and appointments are usually scheduled a week or two in advance. Bermudians value punctuality and efficiency, so confirm the time and place clearly and arrive on time. That said, the island's small size and interconnected professional culture mean informal touchpoints matter too. You might find that a brief conversation at a networking event or introduction over lunch smooths the way for a later formal meeting. Business here moves through a blend of structure and familiarity.

Visitors from cultures where formal hierarchy shapes access may find Bermuda's professional culture surprisingly direct. Senior leaders are often reachable without layers of gatekeeping, and a well-timed email can lead to a meeting without lengthy protocol. At the same time, Bermudians may find colleagues from more hierarchical settings overly cautious or slow to engage, when a straightforward request would have been welcome.

The island's size also means that business and social circles overlap. Someone you meet casually may later become a key contact, so approachability and consistency across settings both count.
