# Business Meeting Culture

## Meetings & Presentations

Meetings in Bermuda are typically arranged with notice, and invitations should go to senior leaders when decisions are expected. Authority sits at the top in most local organizations, so including the right level from the start helps avoid later delays. That said, meetings often begin informally. The first few minutes are used to connect personally before moving into the agenda, and this opening conversation is more than courtesy. It allows people to assess character and establish rapport, which Bermudians see as part of the foundation for doing business well.

Once discussion begins, expect a polite but direct tone. Bermudians state what they need clearly and prefer the same in return. Overpromising or adjusting your position repeatedly will undermine trust quickly. Consistency matters more than enthusiasm. In earlier meetings, formality is higher and people are careful with their words, especially in the presence of senior figures. As relationships develop, the tone may soften, and humor or personal references may appear more naturally.

Decision-making rarely happens in the room. Pressing for immediate answers or imposing tight deadlines can feel pushy and may slow things down rather than speed them up. A colleague from a culture where decisions are made quickly in meetings may find this frustrating, while a Bermudian counterpart might view rapid commitments as reckless or insufficiently considered. Follow-up is where agreements take shape, and written confirmation of next steps is both expected and respected.
