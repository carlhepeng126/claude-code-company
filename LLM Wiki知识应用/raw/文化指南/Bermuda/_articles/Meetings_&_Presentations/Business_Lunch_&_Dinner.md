# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Bermuda tend to be understated rather than elaborate. While the island has high-end restaurants, particularly in Hamilton and around resort areas, the preference is often for venues that feel approachable rather than showy. A colleague who defaults to formal, multi-course dining as a way to build relationships may find the tone here more casual than expected. Conversely, a Bermudian host might read an overly structured or ceremonial approach to a meal as somewhat stiff, preferring something that allows conversation to flow naturally.

Lunch is more common than dinner for business discussions, and the pace is typically relaxed. Meals are seen as an opportunity to get a sense of someone's character, not just to close deals. Rushing through courses or steering the conversation too quickly toward contract terms can come across as transactional. If you're invited to a business meal, expect some time spent on general topics before work is addressed directly.

Alcohol may be offered, but it's not assumed. Bermuda has a strong tradition of temperance rooted in its religious heritage, and many professionals don't drink. If you're hosting, it's worth checking preferences in advance rather than ordering wine for the table. Offering a choice signals respect without making assumptions about personal or professional norms.
