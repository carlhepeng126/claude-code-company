# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Cameroon are rarely just about the food. They're an extension of the relationship-building that underpins professional trust, and the time taken reflects how seriously that trust is regarded. Meals tend to unfold slowly, with multiple courses and long gaps between them. Rushing through courses or signaling impatience can suggest that the relationship itself holds little value. A colleague accustomed to concise lunch meetings may find the pace frustrating, while a Cameroonian counterpart may read hurried dining as a lack of genuine interest in the partnership.

The food itself reflects Cameroon's regional diversity. In Anglophone areas, you might encounter dishes like *ndolé* (a stew made with bitter leaves and groundnuts) or *achu* (pounded cocoyam served with a spiced soup). In Francophone regions, grilled fish, plantains, and *poulet DG* (a celebratory chicken dish with vegetables and plantains) are common. Sharing from communal plates is typical in some settings, particularly outside major cities, and reflects a wider cultural emphasis on collective participation rather than individual consumption.

Alcohol may be offered, particularly beer or palm wine, but it's wise to follow your host's lead. Not everyone drinks, and assumptions based on context alone can misfire. If dietary restrictions apply, mention them ahead of time. Vegetarian options exist but may not be prominent on standard menus, and last-minute requests can create unnecessary difficulty for your host.

Expect the meal to extend well beyond the table. Conversation continues over coffee or tea, and leaving immediately after dessert can feel abrupt. The time invested in sitting together is part of how commitment is demonstrated and evaluated.
