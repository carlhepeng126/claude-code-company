# Negotiating

## Working Together

Negotiations in Cameroon are rarely quick or linear. A decision may appear close, then drift into further consultation. An agreement reached in one session may be revisited when new voices enter the room or when a senior figure who was not present earlier weighs in. This reflects both the hierarchical nature of decision-making and the need to build consensus across a wider circle than may be visible at the table. If you present final terms early, you leave no room to respond when the conversation reopens or when expectations shift. Keep something in reserve.

The process often moves between relationship-building and substance. A meeting scheduled to finalize terms might begin with extended personal exchange, or veer into topics unrelated to the contract. This is how trust is tested and deepened, and how each side confirms that the other will honor commitments beyond what is written down. A colleague accustomed to separating rapport from negotiation may read this as inefficiency. From a Cameroonian perspective, a counterpart who pushes too quickly toward signing may seem transactional or unreliable over the long term.

Pace can also be strategic. Silence, delay, or a request to consult others may reflect genuine internal process, or it may signal that your offer has not yet convinced. Because bargaining is expected and concessions are part of demonstrating flexibility, leaving room to adjust your position allows the negotiation to feel collaborative rather than imposed.
