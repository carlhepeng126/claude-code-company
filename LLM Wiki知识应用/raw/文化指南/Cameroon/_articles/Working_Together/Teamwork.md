# Teamwork

## Working Together

Teamwork in Cameroon takes its shape from relationships formed over time and hierarchies respected in practice. Collaboration is most effective when senior colleagues have had a chance to guide and model expectations, often through one-on-one conversations or informal observation rather than open group discussion. This approach reflects the value placed on preserving dignity and allowing people to learn without exposure. A manager accustomed to direct, public feedback may find that team members withdraw or become cautious after criticism delivered in front of others, while that same manager's behaviour in Cameroon may be seen as undermining both the individual and the collective trust the team depends on.

Teams function well when authority is clear and when the person leading shows personal concern for each member. Leadership here includes understanding pressures outside the office and making space for those realities to be acknowledged. A colleague from a culture where work and personal life are more clearly separated might read this as over-involvement, while a Cameroonian team member working in that environment may feel the relationship lacks warmth or reciprocity.

Praise directed at the team rather than singling out one person helps maintain cohesion and avoids resentment. When recognition is needed for an individual, it is best done privately. Similarly, any correction should be handled away from the group. Public acknowledgment of error, even when delivered gently, can damage trust and make future collaboration harder to sustain.
