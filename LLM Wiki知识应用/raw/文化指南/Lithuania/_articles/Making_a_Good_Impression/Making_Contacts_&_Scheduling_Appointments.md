# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

Lithuanian professional relationships build on consistency and proven reliability rather than immediate warmth. A culture shaped by tight-knit networks and a preference for emotional self-control means trust typically arrives through repeated contact, not quick charm. Early reserve is a way of reading people over time. When making first contact, keep your tone calm and specific. Avoid hype or heavy self-promotion, which can undermine credibility. Quiet competence tends to be read more favorably than high enthusiasm, especially before a relationship has been established. This means that following through on what you say, even in small details, will do more to build trust than an energetic pitch.

A personal introduction from someone already known and trusted can accelerate the process considerably. Family connections, school ties, hometown links, or long-standing friendships serve as reliable channels because they imply accountability. If you have a mutual contact, ask them to make the introduction. This kind of warm referral carries real value and can open doors that cold outreach rarely reaches. Without an introduction, expect to invest more time in demonstrating steadiness before conversations gain traction.

When scheduling meetings, plan ahead but stay flexible. Reconfirm appointments a day or two in advance, and avoid packing your calendar tightly. Summer weather can prompt last-minute changes, as many Lithuanians will prioritize time at their country houses during a rare heatwave. Expect public holidays and generous vacation periods to affect availability. Cafés are common venues for business conversations, particularly once initial formality has eased. In and around Vilnius, be aware that language and identity cues can be more layered than elsewhere, especially where Polish-Lithuanian coexistence shapes everyday life. Sensitivity to these subtleties helps first contact land well.
