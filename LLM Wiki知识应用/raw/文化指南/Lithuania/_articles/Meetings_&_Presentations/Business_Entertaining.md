# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Lithuanian hospitality tends to be understated in expression but generous in substance. Care shows through doing things properly—good food, steady hosting, and making guests comfortable without drawing attention to the effort. A colleague accustomed to cultures where warmth is expressed through constant verbal reassurance or visible enthusiasm might misread the quietness as distance. In turn, a Lithuanian host may find that style overwhelming or superficial, preferring to let the quality of the experience speak for itself.

Entertaining often builds trust through shared experience rather than conversation alone. Invitations to a *pirtis* (traditional Lithuanian sauna) or time at a countryside summer house signal real closeness. The *pirtis* is ritualised and meaningful, involving plants, honey, and guided practices that go well beyond relaxation. Following the host's lead in these settings shows respect for the depth of the gesture. Similarly, social time tied to nature—lakes, forests, bonfires at midsummer during *Joninės*—is part of how relationships deepen over time.

Toasts offer a bridge between formality and warmth. *Į sveikatą* (to health) is simple and communal, creating shared ritual without forcing intimacy. Much relationship-building happens in coffee shops rather than formal dinners, so an invitation for coffee should be taken as seriously as a meal. If you decline an invitation, propose an alternative. Lithuanians may interpret refusal without explanation as rejection and may not extend another offer.
