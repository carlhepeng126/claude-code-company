# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Iceland often happens outside the obvious corporate spaces. Public pools and hot tubs function as unofficial boardrooms where people from every level of society sit together exchanging news and opinions. You may well encounter a CEO or minister in a thermal pool before you do in a hotel lobby. Being comfortable in that setting shows you understand local life, not just the formal meeting circuit. Walking meetings are equally natural: suggesting a conversation by the harbor or around a lake is more culturally aligned than reaching for an expensive dinner reservation, especially in the early stages of a relationship.

Venue choice reflects values. Icelanders typically prefer small, personally chosen places over showy establishments. A local bistro or fish restaurant by the docks signals genuine inclusion, while overly lavish entertaining reads as performing success rather than building partnership. Colleagues from cultures where formal dining marks status may find this modesty puzzling or underwhelming, while Icelanders may read elaborate hosting as a lack of substance or an attempt to impress rather than connect.

Dinner is more common than lunch, given the long working hours most people keep. Business discussion at the table is acceptable, but your host will set the tone. If spouses attend, conversation tends to stay social. Alcohol is expensive and available only through state-run stores or licensed venues, but drinking culture is relaxed, especially on weekends when nightlife runs late.

Weather and simplicity are tests of cultural fit. Willingness to stand in wind and drizzle on a walk, or share an unpretentious meal, signals you can work here rather than just visit.
