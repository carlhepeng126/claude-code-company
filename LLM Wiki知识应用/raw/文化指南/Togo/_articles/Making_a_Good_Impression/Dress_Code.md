# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

Professional dress in Togo reflects social standing, respect for institutions, and the value placed on appearing composed in public life. In formal business settings, especially in government offices, banking, or established private companies, men typically wear suits with ties and polished shoes, while women wear tailored dresses, skirts with blouses, or business suits. The standard leans conservative and carefully assembled. Appearing well-dressed shows you take the meeting seriously and signals your professionalism before you speak.

Expectations shift by sector and context. In younger industries like tech or media, business casual is becoming more common, though still deliberate rather than relaxed. Jeans and trainers remain too casual for first meetings or formal engagements, even in less traditional workplaces. Heat and humidity influence fabric choices, but formality is rarely sacrificed for comfort in professional settings. If you arrive underdressed, your counterpart may read it as a lack of regard for the occasion or for their status.

There is also internal variation. In Lomé and other urban centers, dress codes can be more aligned with international business norms. In rural areas or smaller towns, traditional clothing may be worn more frequently, and modesty in dress is expected regardless of sector. Women, especially, are expected to dress modestly, with skirts often preferred over trousers in more conservative environments.

When hosting or attending evening events, the level of formality rises. Dinners or receptions call for polished, considered attire. Matching the effort your host puts into their appearance helps establish mutual respect and eases the tone of the interaction. Dressing down in these settings can create distance that is hard to recover.
