# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Togo offer a chance to build trust and understanding away from the formality of the office. Invitations to lunch or dinner usually come once some rapport has been established, and they tend to signal genuine interest in the relationship. The meal itself is often relaxed, and business may be mentioned only briefly or not at all. The host will usually decide when, or whether, work is discussed. Raising it too early can feel transactional, as though the relationship itself were secondary to the outcome.

Restaurants are the most common setting, especially in Lomé, where a range of Togolese and international options are available. Local dishes may be chosen to offer a visitor a taste of the country, or the host may select a neutral venue depending on the guest. Either way, the choice reflects consideration.

Alcohol is present in some settings but less widely than in many other countries. It may be served in international or secular business circles, but many Togolese professionals abstain for religious or personal reasons. Ordering or offering alcohol without first gauging the tone of the table can create discomfort. It's usually better to wait and follow the host's lead rather than assume availability or acceptance.

Reciprocating an invitation, whether during the visit or on a later occasion, is appreciated and seen as a sign of respect for the relationship being built.
