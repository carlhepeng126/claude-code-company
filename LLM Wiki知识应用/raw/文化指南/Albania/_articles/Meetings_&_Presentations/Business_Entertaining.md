# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Albania are a common way to strengthen relationships once initial contact has been established. Lunch or dinner offers a chance to move beyond the formality of the office and see how someone engages more naturally. The setting is usually a restaurant, and in larger cities like Tirana, the choice might range from traditional Albanian fare to international options depending on the guest and the occasion.

Alcohol may be present but should never be assumed. Albania has a mixed approach. Many people drink socially, particularly in urban and secular circles, but others abstain for religious or personal reasons. Ordering wine or raki without first reading the table, or offering it to someone who doesn't drink, creates unnecessary discomfort. Wait for the host to set the tone, or ask discreetly if you're uncertain.

The meal itself tends to be relational rather than transactional. Conversation flows across topics and business may come up briefly, or not at all. Pushing too quickly toward deals or decisions can feel abrupt. The host usually decides if and when work talk begins. The real purpose of the gathering is often to observe how people carry themselves outside formal settings and to build the personal trust that underpins professional confidence.

Reciprocating the invitation, either during your stay or on a return visit, is both expected and valued. It shows you understand the exchange as mutual, not merely hospitable.
