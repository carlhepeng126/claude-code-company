# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

In Albania, making business contacts begins with establishing a sense of familiarity and trust before formal business discussions take place. Decades of political and economic transition have shaped a workplace culture where personal assurance often comes before professional commitment. People want to know who you are and whether you can be relied on, and that means investing time in the relationship first. Initial contact may happen by phone or email, but a face-to-face meeting is where credibility is built. Conversations may touch on mutual acquaintances, family background, or local news before turning to work. This is how people assess character and decide whether to move forward.

A personal introduction makes a significant difference. If a trusted colleague or business partner can vouch for you, it shortens the time needed to establish rapport and opens doors more quickly. In practice, this might mean someone arranging an informal meeting over coffee or including you in a small social gathering before any formal appointment is set. Networking in Albania is rooted in shared connections and shared time, not simply exchanging business cards.

Generational and regional differences also shape how contacts are made. Younger professionals, particularly those working in Tirana or in internationally connected sectors, may be comfortable scheduling meetings online and moving to business topics more directly. Outside the capital, introductions are more likely to come through intermediaries, and the pace of relationship-building tends to be slower. In smaller towns and rural areas, trust is built more gradually, and business waits until the relationship feels ready.

When scheduling appointments, plan ahead but keep your schedule flexible. Meetings are best arranged a week or two in advance and reconfirmed closer to the time. Avoid booking back-to-back appointments. Meetings may start late, run longer than scheduled, or be joined by colleagues not originally invited. Priorities can shift during the conversation, and adapting to these changes shows respect for the relationship and the process.
