# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Costa Rica often happen in sodas, small local eateries where people gather over casados — fixed plates of rice, beans, salad, plantain, and meat or fish. These spaces feel democratic and unpretentious, rooted in the country's agricultural past when workers needed filling, affordable meals. Choosing to meet in a soda rather than defaulting to an upscale restaurant shows you're comfortable with everyday Costa Rica and not holding yourself apart. It also reinforces the relationship-first logic that shapes Costa Rican business culture: the quality of the conversation and the ease between people matters more than the setting.

Meals typically close with coffee, and this is more than a formality. The moment after the food is cleared becomes a natural opening for more candid conversation or final clarifications. Skipping coffee or signaling you need to leave immediately after the meal can feel abrupt. For a colleague from a culture where efficiency and time-consciousness are prized, this lingering may seem slow. For a Costa Rican counterpart, a visitor who checks the time or rushes the close may come across as transactional or disengaged.

In tourist-heavy areas, international cuisine is widely available, but locals appreciate when foreign colleagues show genuine interest in Costa Rican food. Ask for recommendations and let your counterparts guide you. Allowing them to "host" you gastronomically builds rapport and demonstrates respect for the culture beyond the business agenda.
