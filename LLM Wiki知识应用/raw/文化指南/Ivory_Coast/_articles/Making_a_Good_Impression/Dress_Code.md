# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

Appearance in Ivory Coast is tied directly to how seriously you will be taken. How you present yourself is seen as an extension of your personality and professional capability. Dress well and colleagues interpret this as evidence you get the job done. Arrive looking careless or overly casual and you will lose credibility quickly, particularly in more traditional sectors. In business settings, men wear suits made from tropical-weight fabric with a tie, and women dress smartly in tailored outfits. Accessories matter just as much. A quality watch, a well-made bag, and polished shoes will all be noticed.

Many Ivoirians are Muslim, so modest, well-fitted clothing is expected. At the same time, fashion is valued. Ivoirian women often blend French and American styles with traditional African fabrics, jewelry, and stones, a look sometimes described as Afro chic. This mix of elegance and cultural identity shows up across professional environments.

Formality increases for special occasions such as the arrival of a dignitary or the opening of an exhibition. People dress noticeably sharper when the setting calls for it, and your ability to read and match that expectation will strengthen the impression you make. In rural areas, traditional clothing is more common. Women wear pagnes, brightly patterned skirts and tops that often carry meaning related to tribe, lineage, or political affiliation. Men wear shorts or wraps day to day, with elaborate robes reserved for ceremonial events.

If your work takes you beyond the cities, bring practical clothing including rainwear, sturdy shoes, and long sleeves for evenings when insects are common. Choose breathable fabrics suited to high humidity.
