# Decision-Making

## Working Together

Decisions in Venezuela often move through two overlapping channels: the formal organizational chart and the web of trusted relationships. Official roles matter, but people frequently look for confirmation that someone will truly stand behind a decision before they commit. Asking "who will actually support this" is usually more revealing than reviewing a signature list. This dual pathway reflects a cultural instinct to anchor decisions in reliability, not just authority. When working across teams, identify both formal decision-makers and the informal influencers whose backing gives a plan traction. Respectfully confirm who holds final authority, and allocate time for relationship-building before expecting swift conclusions.

Unpredictability has made irreversible commitments feel riskier than they might elsewhere. Staged decisions, review moments, and safe checkpoints often feel more responsible than locking in a single path early. This preference for flexibility should not be read as reluctance. It reflects practical judgment developed in conditions where circumstances can shift quickly and options need to stay open. When proposing a course of action, offer two or three routes with clear trade-offs rather than a single recommendation. Design timelines that allow for adjustment, and frame caution as confidence-building rather than delay.

In sectors linked to oil or state enterprises, decisions can hold extra symbolic weight. PDVSA and oil sovereignty are woven into Venezuela's story of national dignity and survival, and choices in or near those institutions may be read through a lens that goes beyond operational efficiency. Expect decisions in these contexts to take longer and involve wider consultation. Even routine steps like scheduling meetings, coordinating travel, or arranging signings can be shaped by safety and logistical realities. Let local colleagues set the rhythm and format for in-person work, and avoid pushing routines that feel exposed or impractical. After a decision is reached, document next steps clearly so the plan can survive the inevitable changes ahead.
