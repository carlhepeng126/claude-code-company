# Business Etiquette

## Making a Good Impression

Business etiquette in Guyana is shaped by warmth, informality, and the everyday overlaps of a small society where professional and personal circles regularly cross. First meetings often begin with firm handshakes, introductions that include your role and sometimes your organization, and a brief exchange of pleasantries. These opening moments set the tone. Jumping straight into business without greeting people individually or acknowledging the room can come across as abrupt or impersonal.

Guyanese colleagues tend to value approachability over formality. Titles are used selectively, often reserved for senior figures in government, academia, or established family businesses, but first names become the norm quickly once rapport begins to build. In sectors like natural resources or finance, especially in interactions involving international partners, the atmosphere may lean slightly more formal, but even there, rigidity is rare. A colleague from a culture where hierarchy is visibly reinforced through titles and deference might find this informality unclear at first, and may struggle to gauge where authority sits. In turn, a Guyanese professional encountering stricter protocol elsewhere may read it as unnecessarily distant or status-conscious.

Dress tends to be smart but adapted to the heat. Men often wear long-sleeved shirts without jackets, and women opt for blouses and skirts or tailored trousers. In Georgetown's ministries or law offices, the standard may be more conservative, while in mining or agriculture, practicality takes precedence. Showing up too casually for a first meeting, or overdressed in a suit when no one else is, both stand out.

Punctuality is respected but rarely enforced with precision. Meetings may start ten or fifteen minutes late without explanation, and this is generally understood rather than remarked upon. If you arrive exactly on time, you may be the first in the room. Building in a buffer and staying flexible will keep interactions smooth.
