# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

Guyana's professional dress codes reflect the country's tropical climate, colonial British heritage, and growing emphasis on national identity. In formal business settings, particularly in Georgetown's government offices, legal chambers, and established private firms, men wear long-sleeved shirts with ties and dress trousers, while women choose tailored dresses, skirts, or trousers with blouses. Full suits appear in very formal contexts, though jackets are often removed once indoors given the heat. What stands out is the consistent attention to looking well-groomed and professionally turned out, even when the formality itself relaxes slightly in practice.

The climate shapes daily choices more than many visitors expect. Natural fabrics that breathe work better than synthetics, and lighter colors are common, though dark tones still dominate in banking and law. Air conditioning in offices can be uneven, so layering helps. Visiting professionals who arrive in heavy wool suits or synthetic blends often find themselves uncomfortable by midmorning. Paying attention to fabric weight shows practical judgment, not informality.

Sector and generation create visible variation. In newer sectors like tech startups or creative agencies, particularly among younger professionals, business casual has taken hold. Collared shirts without ties and smart casual dresses are standard. Even here, though, the overall impression tends toward deliberate and put-together rather than relaxed. Showing up to a first meeting in a Georgetown law firm dressed as you would to a Californian tech office risks being read as unprepared or dismissive of the context.

In recent years, national dress has gained visibility in professional settings. The Guyanese flag colors, traditional Amerindian or African-inspired prints, and locally made garments appear more often at events or on significant national occasions. Colleagues may wear these with pride, and noticing or asking about them can open genuine conversation.
