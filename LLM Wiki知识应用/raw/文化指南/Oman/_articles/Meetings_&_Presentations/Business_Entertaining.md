# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Oman is relationship-building by design. An invitation does not mean a deal is close, and declining one does not mean you have lost favour. Hospitality here is woven into how people get to know each other, how they establish context, and how they decide whether they want to work together over time. Invitations are extended regardless of commercial outcome, and the evening itself is often more about observation and connection than negotiation.

Venue choice is deliberate. A five-star hotel lounge, a fish restaurant in Muttrah, a farm majlis in Batinah, or a frankincense-scented villa in Dhofar each communicate something different about status, privacy, tradition, and whether family is being brought into the picture. If your hosts move away from the standard hotel circuit, they are signalling a deepening relationship. Let them lead on setting and format, and show enthusiasm if they offer a cultural or landscape experience, whether that is visiting a fort, a mountain picnic, or an evening walk along the corniche. These are not add-ons to the real business. They are the real business.

Alcohol is available in some hotels and expat clubs, but many Omani clients do not drink or prefer not to be seen drinking. Public, alcohol-centred hospitality can make conservative partners uncomfortable. Keep it optional and low-profile, and never use it as the primary bonding mechanism. Dinner is more common than lunch, and spouses are rarely part of the invitation. If you are invited to a private home, accept. It is a significant gesture of trust.

When you reciprocate, choose settings that feel calm, modest, and accessible. Good food, privacy where needed, and space for families or elders if they are included will be noticed and appreciated.
