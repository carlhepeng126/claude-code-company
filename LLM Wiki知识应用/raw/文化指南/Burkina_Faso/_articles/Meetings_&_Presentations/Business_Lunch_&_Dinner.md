# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Burkina Faso are primarily relationship-building occasions rather than venues for negotiation or decision-making. The focus tends to be on conversation, shared experience, and demonstrating respect through the time given to others. Meals can be lengthy, reflecting the broader cultural preference for building trust gradually through personal connection. Rushing through courses or steering conversation too quickly toward business outcomes can signal disinterest in the relationship itself, which may undermine professional credibility over time.

Meals are often communal in structure. Dishes may be placed at the center of the table for everyone to share, and it's common to offer tastes of your own plate to others. This sharing reflects deeper social values around collective participation and generosity. Even in more formal restaurant settings, the style of eating reinforces the relational nature of the occasion. Expect the pace to be unhurried, with time between courses offering space for conversation to develop naturally.

Alcohol is generally not served outside international hotels, reflecting the country's Islamic traditions. Drinks are more likely to include fruit juices, sweetened beverages, or tea. If you have dietary restrictions, it's advisable to communicate them in advance. Vegetarian options may be available but are not always prominent on menus, and advance notice allows your host to plan accordingly without putting them in an uncomfortable position during the meal itself.
