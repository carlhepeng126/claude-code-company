# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Burkina Faso builds trust through shared time rather than formal hospitality. A meal with a colleague or potential partner allows space for observation and conversation beyond the constraints of the office. Invitations usually come after some rapport has been established, and accepting them reflects respect for the relationship being built.

Meals tend to be simple and grounded in what's available locally. Restaurants in Ouagadougou and Bobo-Dioulasso offer a mix of Burkinabè dishes and West African staples, though choices are fewer than in some regional capitals. Hosts often select settings they know well, where the atmosphere is relaxed and conversation can flow. The focus is less on impressing through venue and more on creating ease.

Business may come up briefly or not at all. The gathering itself strengthens the connection and allows each person to see how the other thinks and interacts. Steering conversation toward contracts or timelines too early can feel rushed or transactional. Let your host guide the pace. If work is discussed, it's usually toward the end of the meal and framed lightly.

Reciprocating an invitation during your visit, or on a return trip, is appreciated and signals that you value the relationship as more than a one-off transaction.
