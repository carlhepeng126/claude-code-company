# Negotiating

## Working Together

Negotiations in Andorra begin with relationship-building, not terms. The earliest conversations are designed to assess whether the partnership will hold over time and whether trust can develop naturally between the parties. This reflects the logic of a small country where personal reputation and repeated interaction define professional life. Counterparts want to know who you are before discussing what you offer. If you move straight to pricing or timelines before that foundation is in place, the response may be polite but noncommittal.

The process rarely moves in a straight line. You may reach agreement on one point, then find the conversation returning to it days later or veering toward a different issue entirely. This back-and-forth allows both sides to confirm that nothing has been overlooked and that the terms still feel fair. It also tests flexibility. A colleague from a culture where decisions are made quickly may read this as indecision or delay, while an Andorran counterpart may see a rush to closure as a lack of thoroughness or care for the relationship.

Pace is both cultural and tactical. Slowing down may reflect a need to consult quietly with a senior figure or trusted advisor, or it may signal that your counterpart is waiting for a better offer. For this reason, avoid presenting your strongest terms at the outset. Leave room for concessions that allow the other side to feel the outcome was shaped together, not dictated.
