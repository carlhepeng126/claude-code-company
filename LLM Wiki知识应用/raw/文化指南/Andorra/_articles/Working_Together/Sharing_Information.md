# Sharing Information

## Working Together

Information in Andorra often moves through personal channels rather than formal ones. In a country where everyone knows everyone, or at least knows someone who does, trust is built over time through repeated interactions and shared connections. This makes information sharing selective but reliable. People tend to share openly with those they know personally, and more cautiously with outsiders until a relationship has been established. 

The small size of the professional community means reputation travels quickly. Discretion is valued, especially in matters that could affect someone's standing or business relationships. A colleague from a culture where information is readily shared across organizational boundaries might interpret this caution as gatekeeping or reluctance to collaborate. From the Andorran side, that same openness might feel premature or even careless, given how quickly details can circulate in a close-knit environment.

Building trust accelerates information flow. Showing up consistently, following through on commitments, and respecting confidentiality all signal that you can be relied upon. Once that trust exists, communication becomes more direct and spontaneous. Personal introductions help considerably. A recommendation from someone already trusted can open doors that cold outreach cannot, because it transfers credibility through an existing relationship rather than requiring you to establish it from scratch.
