# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Andorra tend to be practical rather than ceremonial. Lunch may follow a morning meeting or precede an afternoon session, and while the setting is more relaxed than a boardroom, the conversation often stays work-focused. Andorrans appreciate efficiency, and meals are usually straightforward. You're unlikely to encounter elaborate multi-course affairs or extended preambles before substantive topics are raised. That said, the meal itself shouldn't feel rushed. Taking time to eat properly and engage in some personal conversation shows respect for the relationship, even if the agenda remains clear.

Alcohol is not a focal point of business dining. Wine or beer may be offered, particularly at dinner, but it's never expected and declining causes no offense. Coffee, often served at the end of the meal, provides a natural moment to wrap up or confirm next steps. If you're proposing the venue, choose somewhere quiet enough for clear conversation. Andorrans value discretion, and a noisy restaurant can make substantive discussion difficult.

Dietary restrictions are generally accommodated without fuss, but it helps to mention them when arranging the meal rather than at the table. Andorra's food culture reflects its mountain setting and proximity to Catalonia and France, so menus often feature hearty regional dishes. If you're invited to dinner, assume it will start later than in some northern European contexts, typically around 8:30 or 9:00 pm, and plan your schedule accordingly.
