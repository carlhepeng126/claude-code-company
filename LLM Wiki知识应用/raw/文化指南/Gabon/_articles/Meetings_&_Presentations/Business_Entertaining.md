# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Gabon offer a setting where professional relationships deepen outside the formality of the office. Invitations to lunch or dinner reflect a genuine interest in building trust and are common once initial contact has been made. These occasions are valued precisely because they allow people to see each other more fully, beyond titles and agendas. The meal itself becomes part of the relationship, rather than a backdrop to negotiation.

Restaurants in Libreville and other urban centers are the usual choice, with hosts selecting local venues that serve Gabonese dishes such as chicken Nyembwe or grilled fish, or opting for French or international cuisine depending on the guest. The setting tends to be relaxed but respectful, and conversation often moves across family, travel, and broader interests before circling back to work, if at all. Business topics are introduced by the host when they feel the moment suits. Raising them too early can feel transactional and can interrupt the rhythm of connection that the meal is meant to establish.

Alcohol may be present but its role varies. Gabon's Catholic majority and French colonial history mean that wine and beer appear regularly in professional settings, but not universally. Some colleagues abstain for personal or religious reasons. Following the host's lead, rather than ordering immediately, avoids missteps and shows attentiveness to the group's tone.

Reciprocation is expected and appreciated. Hosting a meal in return, either during the same visit or on a future one, acknowledges the hospitality extended and reinforces the mutual investment in the relationship.
