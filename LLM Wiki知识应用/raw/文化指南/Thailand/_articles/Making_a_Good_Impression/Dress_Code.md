# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

In Thailand, what you wear reflects how seriously you understand context. Thai public presentation prioritises looking proper and aware of the setting over personal style. This expectation comes from a deeply relationship-oriented culture where fitting into the social environment shows respect for the people around you and the occasion itself. Clothing becomes a visible way to signal that you're not imposing your identity on others, but instead recognising the shared space. If you arrive at a government ministry in casual wear, or at a formal dinner underdressed, the message received is that you didn't think the setting or the people merited the effort.

Formality levels vary by sector and seniority. In traditional industries, government-linked offices, or organisations with older leadership, business dress remains conservative. Men wear long-sleeved shirts and suits despite the heat, and women choose modest blouses with skirts or tailored trousers. Revealing clothing or overly casual attire can be read as disrespect for hierarchy and institutional structure. In younger sectors like tech or creative agencies, especially in Bangkok, business casual has become more common, but even here the baseline is neat and deliberate. Outside Bangkok, expectations may soften slightly, but starting formal is still the safer choice until you've read the room.

Regional and religious contexts also shape what's appropriate. In Thailand's Muslim-majority southern provinces, modesty expectations are higher. When visiting temples anywhere in the country, long skirts or trousers are required, and shoes must be removed. These are not suggestions. A colleague from a culture where personal comfort guides professional dress might interpret Thai formality as unnecessarily rigid. From a Thai perspective, the same colleague's casual approach might suggest a lack of awareness or consideration for the occasion. The gap often lies in whether dress is seen as individual expression or collective respect.
