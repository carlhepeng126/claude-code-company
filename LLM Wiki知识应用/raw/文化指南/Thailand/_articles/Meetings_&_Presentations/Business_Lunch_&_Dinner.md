# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Thailand are primarily about deepening rapport, not advancing agendas. The communal nature of Thai dining reflects this. Dishes are placed at the center of the table and shared among everyone, reinforcing a sense of togetherness rather than individual transaction. This setup mirrors the broader cultural preference for group cohesion and collective rhythm. Pushing business topics too hard during a meal can feel jarring, as though you're treating the relationship as purely instrumental.

Dining etiquette reflects the same restraint and attentiveness that shapes Thai professional life more broadly. Take food in a way that doesn't disrupt the flow or leave others waiting. Let the host set the pace for ordering, serving, and what gets discussed. Finishing everything on your plate shows respect for the meal and the effort behind it, while leaving large amounts uneaten can come across as wasteful or dismissive. If the food is spicier than you're used to, making a light joke about it is fine. Thais often tease visitors about the difference between tourist-friendly dishes and what locals eat, and they won't take offense if you can't handle the heat.

Regional variation shows up clearly at the table. In Muslim-majority areas of the South, alcohol and certain foods may be inappropriate, while Bangkok and other urban centers tend to be more cosmopolitan and flexible. If you're dining outside the capital, check with a local colleague beforehand rather than assuming what worked in one setting will work in another.

Conversation during meals tends to stay light and harmonious. Sharp disagreements or contentious topics are kept off the table, literally. Raising a difficult issue over dinner risks disrupting the social atmosphere and putting people in a position where they feel they must choose between honesty and preserving face. Save those conversations for private, safer settings.
