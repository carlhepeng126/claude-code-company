# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

Approaching someone in a Norwegian workplace means respecting their time as genuinely their own. Calendars and personal schedules are treated as legitimately protected, reflecting a broader norm around not imposing, not cornering, and not demanding immediate intimacy. This shapes how contacts are made. Introductions do not require elaborate personal networks or go-betweens. Norwegians are generally willing to meet people they do not know, and they will judge a proposal on its content and your track record rather than on who brought you to the door. When making first contact, explain clearly who you are, what you are proposing, and why it matters. Frame your credentials without boastfulness, because egalitarian instincts shaped by Janteloven push against overt status display. A straightforward, well-organized email or phone call is usually enough to open the door.

Scheduling works best when you offer genuine options and confirm plans in writing. Predictability functions as social comfort in Norway, part of a wider trust culture where systems and agreements are expected to be dependable. Written confirmation feels less like mistrust and more like a neutral tool that protects everyone's expectations. Book meetings a week or two ahead and reconfirm the day before. Expect early starts and early finishes, many offices close by 4 p.m., and lunches are typically brief. Build in buffer time. Norwegian punctuality is real, and arriving late without good reason can read as disrespect for the other person's schedule. 

Avoid May, late June through early August, and the first week of August if you can. These periods are shaped by seasonal rhythms tied to outdoor life and friluftsliv, the cultural practice of spending time in nature. Summer holidays and cabin trips are not just leisure, they are protected time that reflects a national model where restoration and nature access matter deeply. Planning around these patterns shows cultural awareness, not just logistical courtesy.
