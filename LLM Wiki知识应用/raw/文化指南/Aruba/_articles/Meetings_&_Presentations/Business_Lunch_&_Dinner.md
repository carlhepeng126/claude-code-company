# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Aruba tend to be relaxed and social occasions. The island's professional culture values personal connection, and shared meals offer a natural setting for that to develop. Conversation will often start with family, local happenings, or upcoming events before turning to work. Rushing straight into business topics can feel transactional and may create distance rather than rapport. Colleagues from cultures where meetings follow tighter agendas may find this indirect, while Aruban professionals might read a fast pivot to business as a lack of genuine interest in the relationship.

The dining scene reflects Aruba's cultural makeup. You'll find Dutch-influenced dishes, fresh seafood typical of the Caribbean, and Latin American flavors alongside international cuisine. Restaurants in hotels and tourist areas are accustomed to serving international visitors, and menus are usually available in English, Dutch, and Papiamento. That said, local colleagues will often guide the choice of venue, especially if the meal is on home ground. Accepting their recommendation is a gesture of trust and usually leads to a more comfortable experience for everyone.

Meals unfold at a steady pace. Lingering over drinks or coffee after the main course is common, and this is when conversation often deepens. Cutting the meal short or signaling you need to leave early can undermine the relational work the meal is meant to accomplish. If you have a hard stop, mention it at the start so expectations are clear from the beginning.
