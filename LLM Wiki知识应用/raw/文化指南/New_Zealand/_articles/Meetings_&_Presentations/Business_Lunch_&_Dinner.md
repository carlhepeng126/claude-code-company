# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in New Zealand tend to be informal in tone but purposeful in structure. Lunch meetings are common and usually last between one and two hours, often at a café or casual restaurant near the office. The setting is relaxed, but the agenda remains clear. Dinner is less frequent for routine business and typically reserved for occasions where relationship building matters more than transactional discussion, such as welcoming a new client or marking the end of a project. Even then, the atmosphere stays approachable rather than ceremonial.

Conversation flows naturally between work and personal topics. New Zealanders value directness and tend to get to the point early, so extended small talk before discussing business can feel unnecessary. At the same time, meals are seen as a chance to connect as people, not just professionals. Asking about someone's weekend plans or interests outside work is normal and appreciated. The balance leans toward genuine exchange rather than performance.

Hierarchy is understated. Titles are rarely used, and junior colleagues may be included in business dinners without formality. For visitors from cultures where seniority shapes seating, menu choices, or speaking order, this can feel unexpectedly flat. From a New Zealand perspective, the same behaviours in reverse might read as overly cautious or deferential, potentially creating distance rather than connection.

Splitting the bill is common among peers, though the inviting party often covers the cost if the meal was their initiative. There is little ceremony around payment, and no expectation of elaborate hosting gestures. Offering to pay your share is considered courteous, not awkward.
