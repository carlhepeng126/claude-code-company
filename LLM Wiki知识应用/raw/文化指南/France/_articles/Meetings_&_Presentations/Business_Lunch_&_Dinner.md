# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

In France, a business meal is a structured social ritual that reveals how you think and relate, not just what you want to agree. Several courses, wine for those who drink, and plenty of time are normal, even when the meal has been streamlined. The rhythm follows a recognizable order: apéritif, starter, main, cheese or dessert, coffee. Jumping straight to a main and leaving quickly reads as purely transactional. Time is set aside for pleasure and conversation, and the meal itself is part of how trust and understanding are built. Use it to explore how your French counterparts reason, not to close every commercial point. Decisions often come later, in a follow-up.

The menu holds a certain authority. French diners tend not to customize heavily or request substitutions, treating the kitchen's composition as deliberate. Over-tinkering can come across as fussy or culturally tone-deaf. If you have real dietary needs, explain them calmly and in advance. Otherwise, let the host guide the structure of the meal, and accept what is offered as part of the experience.

Wine matters in many settings, but it's about appreciation, not intoxication. A good host chooses carefully; a good guest drinks thoughtfully and knows they can decline. If you don't drink, say so simply and confidently. Joining fully in the conversation and appreciation of the meal matters more than the wine itself. What will be noticed is whether you showed up ready to engage, not whether you emptied your glass.
