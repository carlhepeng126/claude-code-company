# Being a Host & Guest

## Meetings & Presentations

French hosting is understood as an expression of care and discernment. Whether choosing a restaurant, selecting a wine, or preparing a meal at home, the attention given to the setting and menu reflects how much you value the occasion and the people you are with. This is why French guests will notice whether a venue has been chosen with thought or convenience. A colleague from a culture where business meals are primarily logistical may interpret this focus on taste and ambiance as excessive, while a French host may view a functional approach to dining as a signal that the relationship has not been taken seriously.

A home invitation is less common early on and usually marks a shift from professional to personal trust. If a French counterpart invites you to their home, the relationship is being offered a different kind of footing. Reciprocate the gesture with a small, thoughtful gift such as quality chocolates, a good bottle, or flowers. Extravagant gifts can create discomfort or raise compliance concerns. Arriving exactly on time or a few minutes late for dinner is typical; early arrival can inconvenience a host still preparing.

As a guest, finish what is on your plate and comment appreciatively on the food or wine without overstatement. Business discussion usually comes late in the meal unless your host raises it earlier. A short follow-up message the next day that mentions something specific you appreciated reinforces the relationship and shows you understood the gesture.
