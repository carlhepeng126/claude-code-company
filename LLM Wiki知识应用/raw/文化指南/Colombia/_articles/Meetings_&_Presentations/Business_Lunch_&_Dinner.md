# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Where you eat shapes what the meal means. A corrientazo, the set lunch served in neighbourhood places, puts you close to everyday Colombia. An almuerzo ejecutivo in a mid-range restaurant strikes a middle ground. Cocina de autor in Bogotá or Medellín places the conversation in elite circles. Each choice sends a message about closeness, status, and where you see the relationship going. Match the venue to the power dynamic and the stage of trust. Taking a modest client only to expensive restaurants, or a high-level official only to cheap spots, can feel misaligned or even disrespectful.

Regional dishes like ajiaco, bandeja paisa, or sancocho are more than food. They are identity markers, and people take pleasure in explaining "esto es muy de mi tierra" (this is very much from my land). Asking how a dish is eaten, or whether it comes from the coast or the interior, opens space for your hosts to teach you. That exchange builds warmth and grounds the relationship in something personal.

Conversation follows a rhythm. Meals usually begin with light topics like traffic, football, or good-natured teasing about which city people come from. Serious business talk fits more naturally once everyone has settled and eaten a bit, usually during the main course. After that, conversation often softens again over dessert and coffee. Jumping straight into numbers before people have sat down feels transactional and abrupt.

Who orders first, who selects the wine, who insists on paying, all reveal status instincts and ease around money. In long-term relationships, generosity tends to move toward turn-taking. Fighting over the bill in a way that causes embarrassment can damage trust. Let the rhythm unfold, and be ready to reciprocate when the time comes.
