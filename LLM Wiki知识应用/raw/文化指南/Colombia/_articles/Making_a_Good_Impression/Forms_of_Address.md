# Forms of Address

## Making a Good Impression

Titles in Colombia express more than formality. In a society where hierarchy and status shape daily interactions, addressing someone as *Doctor* or *Doctora* (even when they are not physicians or academics) acknowledges their position and offers them the respect their role commands. The title is used broadly for lawyers, managers, senior sales staff, and politicians. This reflects a class-conscious environment where titles also function as symbolic protection, a way of asserting dignity and expecting decent treatment. When you address someone correctly, you show that you recognize their standing and the effort it took to reach it.

Use *Doctor* or *Doctora* with the surname until the person invites you to do otherwise. Pay attention to how colleagues refer to someone when they are not in the room—*el Doctor Pérez*, *la Doctora Gómez*—and mirror that level of respect face-to-face. Skipping or dismissing a title can feel like a challenge to authority, especially when status is already fragile or contested. Flattening hierarchy too quickly, or joking about formality, risks undermining the relationship before it has formed.

In more traditional or regional settings, *Don* or *Doña* followed by a first name may be used for older or respected figures. The form blends affection with deference, a warmth that does not erase rank. Let the other person guide when to move to first names or the informal *tú*. Follow their lead rather than imposing your own sense of what feels comfortable or efficient.
