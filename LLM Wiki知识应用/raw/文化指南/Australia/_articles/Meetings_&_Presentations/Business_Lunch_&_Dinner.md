# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business lunches in Australia are usually short and functional. Cafes, pubs, Asian eateries, or even salads brought into a meeting room are all common choices. This reflects a broader preference for getting things done without too much ceremony. You can move to business topics fairly quickly, though a few minutes of small talk first is expected. Someone from a culture where long lunches build trust through extended conversation may read this brevity as a lack of interest in the relationship. From the Australian side, a three-hour midweek lunch can feel indulgent or inefficient. If you're hosting, choose a place nearby and confirm any dietary needs in advance. This shows you've thought things through.

Dinners are used more selectively. In cities like Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane, where commutes can be long and evenings are valued, dinner invitations often mark important relationship moments or visits from senior colleagues. Choosing a good local restaurant, especially one that reflects Australia's diverse food culture, is a subtle way to show care. Southeast Asian, Middle Eastern, Italian, and Modern Australian venues are all widely appreciated. Vegetarian, vegan, halal, and gluten-free diets are understood and openly discussed. A host who fails to ask about dietary needs or picks a venue that can't accommodate them will come across as careless, not casual.

If you're hosted, offer to reciprocate later. Sharing the hosting role helps keep the relationship balanced over time.
