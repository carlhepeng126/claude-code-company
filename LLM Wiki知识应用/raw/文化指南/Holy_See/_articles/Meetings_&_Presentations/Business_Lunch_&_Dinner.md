# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Formal business meals are rare in the Holy See. The Vatican's workforce is small, often geographically dispersed, and typically made up of clergy, diplomats, and lay professionals with packed schedules and clearly defined roles. Meetings tend to happen in offices, not restaurants. When meals do occur, they are usually reserved for significant diplomatic encounters or high-level visits, not routine business development.

When a business lunch or dinner does take place, it will almost certainly be hosted within Vatican territory or at a private venue, not a public restaurant. The setting reflects the institution's discretion and protocol. Guests are typically seated according to rank, and conversation unfolds with care. Topics veer toward culture, theology, or shared institutional interests rather than immediate business outcomes. The meal itself becomes part of a longer relational arc, rarely the moment where decisions are made.

Alcohol may be offered, particularly wine, which has longstanding liturgical and cultural significance in Catholic tradition. But consumption is modest. Overindulgence would be seen as a lapse in self-control and out of step with the formality of the occasion. If you are hosting a Vatican counterpart outside the Holy See, keep the venue quiet, the tone respectful, and the agenda light. A colleague accustomed to using meals to accelerate decisions or close agreements may find the rhythm slow. For Vatican professionals, the meal is relational groundwork, not a shortcut.
