# Forms of Address

## Making a Good Impression

In Timor-Leste, the way you address someone reflects the blend of Portuguese colonial influence, Catholic tradition, and enduring respect for elders and authority that shapes professional life. Titles are rarely optional. Formality is the default, particularly in government, education, and NGO settings where hierarchy remains clearly defined. Use "Senhor" (Mr.) or "Senhora" (Mrs.) with the family name until explicitly invited to do otherwise. Professional titles matter deeply: "Doutor" (Doctor) and "Engenheiro" (Engineer) are used regardless of the setting, and omitting them can read as a lack of respect. For someone accustomed to first-name introductions and rapid informality, this can feel slow or stiff. For a Timorese colleague meeting someone who skips titles and moves quickly to casual address, the informality can suggest disregard for experience or status.

Younger professionals educated abroad or working in international organizations may adopt first names more readily, but even then, wait for their lead. In rural areas and with older generations, formality persists across all interactions. Starting too casually risks undermining trust before it has a chance to form.

In written communication, formality is even more pronounced. Emails and letters open with "Estimado Senhor" or "Estimada Senhora," and close with "Atentamente" or "Com os melhores cumprimentos" (With best regards). Even in digital exchanges, dropping these conventions too soon can come across as careless rather than efficient.
