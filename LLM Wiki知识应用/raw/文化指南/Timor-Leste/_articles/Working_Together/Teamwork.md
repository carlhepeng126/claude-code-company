# Teamwork

## Working Together

Teamwork in Timor-Leste is shaped by relationships and respect for hierarchy, reflecting the collective orientation that runs through Timorese society. Teams function best when senior members guide and mentor those with less experience, often through example rather than direct instruction. This approach allows junior colleagues to learn without being exposed or losing face, a concern that remains central to group harmony. In a culture where preserving dignity is fundamental, open correction or singling someone out in front of others can damage trust and disrupt the cohesion a team depends on.

The way feedback is given reflects this. Praise directed at the group rather than individuals helps maintain balance and avoids elevating one person above others in a way that might create tension. When something needs to be addressed, it should be done quietly and with care. A manager from a culture where direct, individual feedback is the norm may find this indirect, but for Timorese colleagues, this discretion protects both the person and the relationship that holds the team together.

Expect collaboration to move through informal channels as much as formal ones. Side conversations, stories shared over coffee, and decisions that emerge through quiet consensus are how teams often operate in practice. Managers are expected to take an interest in people's lives beyond the office, building the relational foundation that makes teamwork possible. This means that investing time in connection is not separate from the work itself but enables it.
