# Negotiating

## Working Together

Negotiations in Timor-Leste move through relationship first, then terms. Your counterpart will want to understand who you are and whether the partnership can be relied on beyond a single transaction. This comes from a deeply communal society where trust is earned over time and through demonstrated commitment, not just stated intent. If you lead with price or timeline before the relationship has been established, discussions may stall or feel hollow. Build rapport early by showing genuine interest in the context your counterpart is working within, and by being present for more than one conversation.

Expect the process to circle back. A point you thought was agreed may be revisited, or the conversation may shift to something that seems unrelated before returning to the main issue. This is how many Timorese negotiators make sure all perspectives have been heard and that decisions reflect collective input, not just individual authority. It also allows space to test flexibility without direct confrontation. For a colleague from a culture where agreements are locked in quickly and moving forward means not looking back, this can feel like progress is being undone. From a Timorese perspective, reopening a point is often about refining it, not rejecting it.

Leave room for movement in your offer. Bargaining is expected and understood as a way to reach something that works for both sides. Presenting your final terms at the outset can close down the collaborative exchange that many Timorese counterparts see as part of building a fair outcome.
