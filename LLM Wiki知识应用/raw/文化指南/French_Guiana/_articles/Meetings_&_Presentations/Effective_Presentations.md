# Effective Presentations

## Meetings & Presentations

Presentations in French Guiana often blend French administrative formality with Caribbean social warmth, reflecting the territory's unique position as both a French département and a culturally diverse society. While French is the official language of business and government, the way presentations unfold is shaped by relationships and the need to maintain face across groups with different levels of formal education and linguistic backgrounds. Starting with rapport-building rather than diving straight into data helps establish credibility in a context where trust between mainland French officials, local Creole communities, and immigrant populations can be delicate.

Tone matters more than pace. A conversational, people-centered delivery tends to work better than rapid-fire efficiency. French Guianese audiences may remain quiet during a presentation, not because they lack interest but because interrupting or challenging a speaker publicly risks causing embarrassment. Questions often surface afterward in smaller settings, where hierarchy is less visible and disagreement can be voiced without compromising anyone's standing. If you move straight to the next meeting without allowing time for private follow-up, you may miss the most honest feedback.

Visual materials should be in French, and dense slides with technical jargon can create distance rather than clarity. Keep text minimal and offer to clarify terms as you go, especially in mixed settings where not everyone may be equally comfortable with formal French. If your content includes policy or regulatory detail, allow extra time for translation into plain language and be prepared for people to consult quietly with colleagues during your talk. This reflects collaboration, not disengagement.

Status differences shape who speaks when. A senior figure may say little during the presentation itself but will guide the decision afterward. Pay attention to who defers to whom, and avoid assuming that the most vocal participant holds the most influence.
