# Motivating Others

## Managing Relationships

Mexicans often draw their strongest motivation from what achievement means for family, team and community, rather than purely personal ambition. Success is understood not just as individual advancement but as something that can improve the lives of those close to you or honor the people who supported you along the way. Religion often frames success as service or purpose, and cultural narratives of resilience run deep. When a manager connects a goal to its positive ripple effect, whether that is supporting a household, strengthening a team, or contributing to something larger than the project itself, engagement typically deepens. Urban professionals may also be inspired by opportunities to innovate, develop new skills, or make meaningful contributions beyond routine deliverables, especially when those contributions are acknowledged warmly and in the moment.

Recognition works best when it feels genuine and relational. A public thank you, a story told to senior leadership about someone's contribution, or direct personal praise from a respected manager can be more motivating than a formal performance bonus alone. Money matters, but it is rarely the only driver. Mexicans tend to view work as a means to support a fulfilling personal and family life, not as an end in itself. Leaders who respect personal and family priorities, and who make time for informal connection, strengthen loyalty and discretionary effort far more than those who rely on rigid targets or overtime expectations.

Younger professionals, especially in urban centers and sectors like tech, finance and creative industries, are increasingly influenced by global trends and expect clearer paths for development, merit-based advancement, and autonomy. They still value relationships and community, but they also want opportunities to build their own skills, be recognized for individual contributions, and grow on merit. Leaders who blend relationship-based motivation with visible learning opportunities and mentorship are better positioned to engage this generation than those who rely solely on traditional loyalty to a single leader or company.
