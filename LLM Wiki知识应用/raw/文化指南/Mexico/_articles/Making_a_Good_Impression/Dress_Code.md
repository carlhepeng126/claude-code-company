# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

Professional attire in Mexico draws together European formality, contemporary cosmopolitan style, and practical responses to climate. Urban professionals tend to dress well, with a focus on being polished, neat, and intentional in how they present themselves. This reflects the importance of personal appearance as a form of respect, both for the occasion and for the people you're meeting. Well-groomed appearance and quality clothing send a clear message about how seriously you take the relationship and the work at hand.

In cities, standard business wear is the norm. Men typically wear suits, especially for first meetings, and women choose formal business attire with conservative cuts. Attention to grooming matters just as much: clean shoes, tidy hair, and clothing in good condition all contribute to credibility. Coastal areas and hotter regions may lean toward lighter fabrics, but the overall sense of professionalism remains. In these settings, overly casual clothing can undermine the impression you're trying to build, so even in warmer climates, polish still counts.

Regional differences also shape expectations. More conservative or religious regions, influenced by Catholic values, tend toward greater modesty in professional settings. Progressive urban centers offer more flexibility, but even there, the baseline leans formal rather than casual. Shorts, for instance, belong at the beach or resort, not in business or urban social settings, where they immediately code as tourist rather than professional.

For evening occasions, dress codes generally shift to smart casual, meaning trousers with an open-collar shirt or a sweater, though some venues still expect a jacket. The approach remains deliberately chosen rather than careless, maintaining a sense of elegance even when formality relaxes.
