# Business Etiquette

## Making a Good Impression

Business etiquette in Mexico is shaped by both Indigenous traditions of communal respect and Spanish Catholic layers of courtesy and humility. The result is a professional culture where warmth, politeness, and personal dignity matter as much as competence. Hierarchy and seniority are acknowledged clearly, especially in formal settings, but interactions remain friendly and conversational. Titles such as Doctor or Professor are used to signal respect, particularly when introducing yourself or addressing senior figures. Public confrontation is generally avoided to preserve harmony and protect relationships.

Communication tends to be polite and indirect. Requests are often softened using conditional forms or courteous phrasing. A colleague might ask, "Would it be possible to have this for tomorrow?" rather than stating the need directly. This approach supports face-saving and maintains positive rapport. When disagreement arises, sensitive issues are best handled privately. A blunt critique in front of others can damage trust and close down future collaboration, even if the content is factually accurate.

Personal space in Mexican business settings is typically closer than in many Northern European or North American contexts, often around half an arm's length once familiarity is established. Stepping back noticeably during conversation may come across as cold or distant. Physical gestures such as the abrazo (a brief embrace and back slap between male colleagues) or a light kiss on each cheek between women are common among those who know each other well. Avoid prolonged direct eye contact, which can feel challenging, but do maintain a steady gaze to show engagement.

First impressions are taken seriously. Dressing well and presenting a polished image signals professionalism and attention to detail. Small gestures of appreciation, such as quality pens or local crafts from your home region, are welcomed, but avoid overly personal or lavish gifts. If invited to someone's home, a small token such as chocolates is appropriate. Avoid yellow flowers or marigolds, which are associated with death, and red flowers, which may suggest romance or carry superstitious connotations in some regions.
