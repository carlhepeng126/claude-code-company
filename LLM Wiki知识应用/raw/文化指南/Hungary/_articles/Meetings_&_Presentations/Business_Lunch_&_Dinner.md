# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Hungary still follow the rhythm of a traditional eating culture. Lunch remains the main hot meal of the day for many people, not just a sandwich at the desk. Skipping it or replacing it with only coffee will likely feel odd to your hosts, especially outside Budapest. A midday pause for a substantial meal is understood as necessary refuelling, and that makes it a useful time for relationship-building without the pressure of a late dinner.

The food itself can be heavy by international standards. Expect dishes like *gulyás* (a rich soup-stew of beef, vegetables, and paprika), *pörkölt* (a slow-cooked meat stew), *halászlé* (a spicy fish soup), or schnitzel-style cuts served with creamy sauces, dumplings, or pasta. Rich desserts often follow. Lighter options exist in Budapest, but in smaller towns the menu will lean traditional. Hungarians take quiet pride in their cuisine and will usually want you to try something local. If you're hosting, include at least one Hungarian dish on the menu. It shows you're willing to meet the culture on its own terms.

Toasting happens, but it tends to be sincere rather than showy. With beer, some older Hungarians may still avoid clinking glasses, a fading custom tied to 19th-century Austrian rule. Follow your hosts' lead. Participate in toasts, but don't turn the occasion into a test of endurance. The meal is meant to deepen trust, and that depends on pacing and mutual respect, not volume.
