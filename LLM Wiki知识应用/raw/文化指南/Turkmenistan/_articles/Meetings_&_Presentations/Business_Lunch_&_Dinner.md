# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Turkmenistan are rare and usually reserved for guests who have been in the country for some time or with whom a relationship has already been established. The formality and importance of these occasions reflect the broader Turkmen emphasis on status and hierarchy. If you're invited, it's a signal that the relationship has moved beyond initial courtesies.

When meals do take place, they tend to be structured around traditional dishes. Expect rice-based pilafs, grilled meats, and flatbreads, often served communally. The host will typically take charge of serving, and this reflects the expectation that seniority and care are shown through gesture rather than words alone. Alcohol is generally not served, and it's best not to request it. Tea, often green and offered repeatedly, usually closes the meal.

Conversation during the meal may move slowly and touch on family, health, or shared interests before any return to business topics. This isn't a delay. The meal itself is part of the process of building trust, and attempting to steer too quickly toward transactional matters can feel abrupt or disrespectful. Allow time for the rhythm of the occasion to unfold, and let your host guide when and how the conversation shifts. Patience here is read as respect for the relationship, not just the agenda.
