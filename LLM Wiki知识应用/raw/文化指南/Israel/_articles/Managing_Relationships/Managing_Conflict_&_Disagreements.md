# Managing Conflict & Disagreements

## Managing Relationships

In Israel, disagreement is handled openly and on the spot. The instinct is not to manage around a problem or let tension sit, but to address it head-on. This directness is rooted in a communication style where clarity and speed outweigh diplomacy. Israelis typically separate the argument from the relationship. A fierce debate over strategy or priorities is understood as being about ideas, not people. When the decision is made, the argument ends, and the relationship continues without residue. This allows teams to move quickly and to test thinking in real time, but it also means conflict is visible and can feel intense to those not used to it.

For colleagues from cultures where disagreement is softened or expressed indirectly, Israeli directness can feel confrontational or even personal. What an Israeli experiences as a normal exchange of views may be received as an attack. The reverse also happens. An Israeli working with someone who raises concerns cautiously or circles an issue may read that behavior as evasive or unclear, when it reflects a different logic around preserving face and group cohesion. If you are leading a mixed team, naming these differences early and translating between styles can prevent real misunderstandings from taking root.

Rather than avoid conflict with Israeli colleagues, address it directly and it will usually resolve fast. If something is unclear or feels off, raise it. Israelis expect that, and delaying the conversation is more likely to cause frustration than speaking up.
