# Managing Suppliers & Customers

## Managing Relationships

In Argentina, suppliers and customers are viewed as co-survivors of shared economic turbulence rather than transactional counterparties. Many family firms can recall with precision who paid in dollars during the 2001 crisis, who honoured peso debts through hyperinflation, and who renegotiated fairly when exchange rates collapsed overnight. These stories circulate at asados and are retold across generations, shaping who gets called when things stabilize. Building lasting commercial relationships means recognizing this long memory and treating partners as allies navigating the same volatile terrain, not simply as cost centres or revenue streams.

Suppliers may propose creative billing arrangements to manage the gap between official exchange rates, blue market rates, and ever-shifting import quotas. These suggestions reflect survival logic in an environment where currency controls can wipe out margins overnight, and they should be met with clarity about ethical and legal boundaries rather than dismissal. Working together to find compliant solutions that still protect suppliers from foreign exchange shocks builds the trust that outlasts individual transactions. Many key suppliers are family companies where a vendor change or aggressive price squeeze can register as an affront to the entire clan, affecting relationships well beyond the immediate deal.

Important customers expect to know someone by name who will answer the phone when a system breaks. In a context where shortages, delayed imports, and power cuts are familiar realities, what gets judged is how you behave when you cannot deliver. Hiding, blaming regulators, or going silent damages reputation far more than the original failure. Showing up personally, explaining what happened, and offering tangible gestures like extended payment terms or priority on the next shipment builds the kind of credibility that formal service channels alone cannot create. Reputation in Argentina is earned less by flawless execution and more by how honourably you handle the inevitable breakdowns.
