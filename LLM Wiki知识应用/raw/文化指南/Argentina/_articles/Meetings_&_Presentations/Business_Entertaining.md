# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Argentina is where relationships solidify and trust is tested in real time. Cafés and restaurants function as extended offices, places where friendships, deals and intense debates about everything from politics to football unfold over hours. Your willingness to linger, to let the conversation wander, and to engage beyond the transactional all register as signs of whether you're genuinely interested in the relationship or just working through a checklist.

The parrilla, Argentina's traditional grill restaurant, is the most culturally significant venue. Being invited to one places you inside the circle. The ritual itself is revealing: choosing cuts of meat, debating how asado or vacío should be cooked, sharing provoleta and papas fritas. This performance of conviviality and Argentine identity doubles as social literacy. Knowing your way around the menu, or at least showing curiosity about classic cuts like entraña or choripán, earns respect. Total indifference to the food codes you as detached.

Entertaining is rarely ostentatious. After decades of economic shocks, visible luxury can trigger discomfort or sarcasm. What people value is generosity, atmosphere and lively conversation, often in a packed, noisy neighbourhood spot where the owner greets guests by name. A beloved local restaurant outweighs an impersonal high-end venue that feels cold or signals inequality.

Dinners typically start late, around 10pm, and the expectation is that you'll stay on afterward. The evening itself is the point. Business may come up naturally, but let your Argentine counterpart decide when and how.
