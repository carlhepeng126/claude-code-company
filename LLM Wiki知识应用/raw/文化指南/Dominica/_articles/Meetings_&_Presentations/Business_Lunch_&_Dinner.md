# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Dominica are relatively informal occasions, though they unfold at an unhurried pace that reflects the island's broader cultural rhythm. Conversations tend to begin with personal topics—family, local events, mutual acquaintances—before shifting toward business. This opening phase is not filler. It establishes goodwill and allows people to assess whether they are comfortable working together. Jumping straight into proposals or deliverables can feel transactional and may undermine rapport before it has had a chance to form.

Meals are typically held at local restaurants or hotel dining rooms, where seafood, provisions like dasheen and green fig, and hearty Creole stews are common. Sharing food often happens naturally, with dishes placed centrally or offered across the table. This reflects a broader cultural expectation that food, like time, is something to be enjoyed collectively. Declining an offer to taste something may be read as standoffishness rather than mere preference.

Alcohol may be present but is consumed in moderation. Overindulgence can damage credibility quickly in a small society where reputations travel fast. Similarly, being overly formal or reserved throughout the meal may come across as disengaged. A colleague from a culture where business meals follow a tighter agenda and clearer separation between social and professional talk might interpret the extended conversation as inefficient. From a Dominican perspective, that same brisk focus can seem impersonal and suggest a lack of genuine interest in the relationship itself.

Expect the meal to take longer than scheduled. Rushing the close or checking the time visibly signals impatience and can undo much of the goodwill built during the conversation.
