# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

In India, access to senior decision-makers often flows through networks built on family, caste, community, alumni ties, and long-standing business relationships. A warm introduction from someone already known and trusted can open doors faster than a corporate credential or polished pitch deck. These networks function as social proof. If a trusted intermediary vouches for you, the relationship starts with goodwill already in place. Without that connection, you may find yourself waiting longer or filtered through layers of gatekeepers: personal assistants, relatives, or long-term suppliers who control how and when senior leaders engage. If you are entering a new market or seeking a first meeting, investing time to identify a credible introducer, whether a local partner, shared alumnus, or diaspora contact, will shape how quickly the conversation moves forward.

Scheduling appointments requires both formal and informal channels. A calendar invite alone may not be enough to confirm the meeting. WhatsApp messages and mobile calls are widely used to set times, remind participants, and reconfirm attendance, sometimes on the day itself. This dual-channel approach reflects how relationships are maintained in India: official processes run alongside personal connection. If you rely only on email, you risk the meeting being deprioritized or quietly reshuffled. Following up with a quick message or call shows attentiveness and keeps the appointment visible.

Plan around the festival calendar. Major holidays like Diwali, Eid, Navratri, Durga Puja, Pongal, and Onam vary by region and religion, and their timing shapes availability across the year. During these periods, people may be at their desks but mentally elsewhere, preparing for family gatherings or travel. Election cycles, exam seasons, and regional events can also shift schedules unexpectedly. Proposing dates without checking the calendar risks low attendance or last-minute cancellations. Building in flexibility around these rhythms shows cultural awareness and makes rescheduling easier when it happens.
