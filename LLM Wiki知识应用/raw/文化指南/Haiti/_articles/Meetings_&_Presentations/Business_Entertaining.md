# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Haiti serve a social purpose more than a practical one. They create a setting where people can begin to know each other outside the formal boundaries of the office. Trust develops through these repeated, informal interactions, and until that groundwork exists, moving quickly into substantive agreements can feel premature. A colleague from a culture where decisions are made quickly in formal settings may feel uncertain about the purpose of a meal where no business terms are discussed. From the Haitian side, that same directness can seem transactional, as though the relationship itself holds no value beyond the contract.

Restaurants are the most common venue, particularly in Port-au-Prince, though invitations to someone's home are less frequent in professional contexts. When they do occur, they reflect a meaningful degree of trust. Hosting at home involves preparation and often family presence, and accepting such an invitation acknowledges the gesture.

Conversation during meals is typically relaxed and wide-ranging. Topics may include family, shared interests, or broader social issues. Business may come up briefly, but it is usually the host who introduces it, and often only lightly. Raising commercial details too early or pressing for outcomes during the meal itself can seem as though the guest values the transaction more than the person. If business is to be advanced, it will happen later, once the relationship has been observed and affirmed through these quieter exchanges.
