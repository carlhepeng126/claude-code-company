# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in the Democratic Republic of Congo are rarely only about the meal. They function as a setting to deepen relationships, observe comportment, and confirm trust beyond what can be established in a formal meeting room. Once initial contact has been made, it's common for Congolese colleagues to extend an invitation to lunch or dinner. Accepting signals openness to a more grounded working relationship.

Restaurants in Kinshasa and other major cities are the usual venue, often chosen for their reputation or ambiance. Conversation tends to begin broadly and may remain there. Politics, family, shared acquaintances, and current events often feature more prominently than the project at hand. Business talk may surface lightly or not at all during the first meal together, and it's typically the host who determines if and when it enters the discussion. Raising it too early can read as rushed or transactional, suggesting the relationship itself holds less value than what can be extracted from it.

Alcohol is widely available and often present, but individual preferences vary considerably. Some colleagues drink openly in professional settings, while others abstain for religious, personal, or health reasons. It's generally safer to wait and follow the host's lead rather than order alcohol first or assume it's expected. Misjudging this can create discomfort without explanation.

Reciprocating an invitation, either during the same visit or on return, is seen as courteous and strengthens the sense of mutual regard. The gesture itself matters more than the formality of the setting.
