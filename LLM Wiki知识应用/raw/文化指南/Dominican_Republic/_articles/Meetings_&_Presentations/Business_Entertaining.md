# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in the Dominican Republic can happen in resort restaurants and hotel lounges, but also in colmados (local corner stores with seating), at sancocho gatherings, or at pelota games. Choosing only upscale venues keeps you visible in one social class bubble and limits the relationship. Accepting invitations to more everyday spots reveals a different layer of your counterparts and builds credibility. Dominicans value warmth and hospitality, and where you meet says something about whether you're willing to step outside the professional perimeter.

Expect music, often loud, and TVs showing baseball or playing dembow. These aren't background elements to be tolerated but part of how energy and connection are maintained. Being visibly uncomfortable with noise or asking to turn everything off can create distance. You'll often need to talk over the volume and keep your own energy up rather than waiting for quiet.

Alcohol is common, rum, beer and whisky especially, and refusing a first drink may be noted. That said, people generally understand religious or health reasons. What will undermine respect quickly is getting drunk, especially as a foreigner. The expectation is to stay gracious and lucid. Drink slowly and know your limit. You gain standing by how you handle yourself, not by proving you can match everyone else.

Business may come up naturally during a meal, but the gathering itself often does more work than any agenda. Reciprocating an invitation, whether during your visit or later, is appreciated and expected.
