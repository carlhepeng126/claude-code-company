# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

First contact in the Czech Republic is typically a short, factual email that sets out who you are, why you're relevant, and what you're proposing. This preference for written opening moves reflects a literate, organised professional culture where people expect clarity before committing time. If there's genuine interest, the next step is often informal: "Sejdeme se na kafe/pivo?" (Shall we meet for a coffee or beer?). This is where your credibility is really tested, not through your presentation deck but through whether you come across as a normal, trustworthy person. That informal moment can decide whether polite interest becomes real cooperation.

Scheduling follows regional patterns. In Prague and Brno, expect the same structured approach as any Central European capital: Outlook invites, clearly defined time slots, and dense calendars. Outside the urban centers, particularly in industrial towns and Moravian villages, local reputation and personal introductions matter more. People in these areas may only agree to a meeting once someone they already trust has vouched for you. Aggressive follow-up sequences will not accelerate this process; investing in local references and word-of-mouth will.

Many Czech firms have long-standing relationships with German and Austrian customers, and those relationships are often efficient but emotionally distant and heavily transactional. A new partner needs to show they understand the reality of Czech companies as suppliers embedded in long supply chains, not just as a market to be unlocked. Asking about existing export relationships and the pressures they bring demonstrates that awareness and helps you stand apart.

Trust develops through repeated small touches rather than one good meeting. Czechs look for a pattern: showing up on time, maintaining the same tone, and following through when you say you will. That consistency over multiple touchpoints builds the predictability they need before moving from polite engagement to genuine partnership.
