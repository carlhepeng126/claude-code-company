# Delegating & Supervising

## Managing Relationships

Authority in Ukrainian workplaces is shaped by a Soviet legacy that combined bureaucratic hierarchy with collective practice. Older employees, particularly in traditional sectors, often expect managers to provide clear direction before they act. Taking initiative without explicit approval can feel like overstepping, especially if something goes wrong and blame might fall on the supervisor. Younger professionals in international firms have shifted toward more independent working styles, but even in those settings, respect for hierarchy runs deep. If you delegate broadly and expect people to run with tasks without checking in, you may find progress stalls while team members wait for confirmation they have your backing.

Managers earn authority through visible competence. Education and technical expertise are highly respected, and employees will look to see whether you know the work well enough to guide them credibly. Positional power alone will not be enough. A manager who cannot demonstrate knowledge of the area they oversee may struggle to command respect, regardless of title. If you are new to a role or leading a team outside your technical background, acknowledge what you are learning and involve people in decisions where their expertise exceeds yours.

The instinct to present the correct answer rather than the honest one comes from an era when candor carried real risk. Employees may soften bad news, delay reporting problems, or only raise issues once they have a solution in hand. This does not mean they are avoiding responsibility. It reflects a learned wariness of being seen as the person who caused trouble. As a manager, you will need to actively create safety for honest communication. Ask open questions, follow up on progress without waiting for people to volunteer concerns, and make it clear that you value transparency over reassurance. Public criticism will almost certainly shut people down. Delivered privately and without diminishing someone's dignity, feedback will land far more effectively.
