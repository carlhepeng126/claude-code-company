# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Azerbaijan happens across very different settings, and each one sends a message. A traditional restaurant with live mugham music and kebabs, a sleek café in central Baku, or an invitation to someone's home all reflect different levels of intimacy, status, and intent. Your host's choice of venue often reveals how they see the relationship developing. Let your local counterparts suggest where to meet, since they will intuitively select something that fits the mix of people and the stage of your connection.

Hospitality here performs status and responsibility. A senior host who insists on paying is demonstrating both generosity and success. Pushing too hard to split the bill can create awkwardness rather than ease. If your host insists, accept graciously and reciprocate on another occasion. The exchange itself reinforces the relationship over time, and being rigid about equality in a single moment can undercut that.

At more festive gatherings, expect music, toasts, and perhaps dancing. A skilled host allows everyone to participate without pressuring anyone to drink beyond their comfort or perform beyond their willingness. Joining in visibly, even modestly, shows respect for the occasion and the people gathered. Being engaged counts more than being fluent in every custom.

How you treat waiting staff, drivers, and others outside the core group is watched closely. It functions as a character test, revealing how you behave when hierarchy is unambiguous. Courtesy in those moments tells your host whether you understand respect as something owed across roles, not just exchanged among peers.
