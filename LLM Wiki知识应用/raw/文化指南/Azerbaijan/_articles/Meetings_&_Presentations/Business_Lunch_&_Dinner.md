# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

A business meal in Azerbaijan is one of the main ways people decide whether they want to work with you. Over the course of several hours and many dishes — salads, cold appetizers, hot plates, plov, fruit, sweets, and tea — your Azerbaijani counterparts are watching how you talk about family, money, risk, your own country, and theirs. The conversation is a form of due diligence. They are trying to understand not just whether your proposal is sound, but whether you are someone they can trust over time.

The ability to host a generous meal is a source of pride, and abundance is expected. At large dinners, especially around weddings or significant deals, tables are full, music may be playing, and photographers may be present. This culture of feasting reflects both hospitality and status. Business topics appear and reappear throughout the meal rather than being saved for a single phase or left until the end. Let the rhythm of the conversation guide you. If you host, resist the urge to structure the meal like a presentation. Themes will emerge naturally, and agreements can be captured in writing later.

Pace yourself with both food and drink. The goal is conviviality, and moderation is respected. Be ready for sensitive questions asked quietly over tea — about political perceptions, regional views, or your own country's stance on issues that matter locally. How you handle these moments, with tact and without evasion, builds the kind of trust that contracts alone cannot.
