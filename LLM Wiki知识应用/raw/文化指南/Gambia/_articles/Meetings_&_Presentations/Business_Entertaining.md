# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Gambia are less frequent than in some West African cultures, but when they do happen, they tend to follow an established social meal rather than replace the core work itself. If a relationship has developed warmth and mutual regard, an invitation to lunch or dinner may follow, usually after several meetings. Meals are a way of affirming goodwill and observing how someone behaves in a less formal setting, rather than a venue for negotiation or decision-making.

Restaurants in Banjul or the coastal tourist areas are the most common choice, particularly for visitors. Gambian hosts may select a place serving local dishes such as benachin (a spiced rice dish with fish or meat) or domoda (a groundnut stew), or opt for Lebanese or international cuisine depending on familiarity and preference. The choice reflects consideration of the guest's comfort as much as local pride.

Business is rarely discussed during the meal itself. The focus is usually on family, travel, or shared acquaintances. A colleague from a culture where deals are advanced over dinner may read this as evasion or lost opportunity, while the Gambian host may view a quick return to business topics as transactional or socially clumsy. Raising work matters too early can suggest the relationship itself holds little value beyond the contract.

Reciprocating an invitation, either during the same visit or on return, is noticed and valued. It confirms that the gesture was understood as relational, not merely procedural.
