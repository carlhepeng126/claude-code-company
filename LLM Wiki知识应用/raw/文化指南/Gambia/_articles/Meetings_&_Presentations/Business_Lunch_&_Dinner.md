# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in The Gambia blend relationship-building with local hospitality. Lunch is more common than dinner for professional meetings, often held at hotel restaurants in urban centers like Banjul or Serrekunda, where international and Gambian dishes are available. The pace tends to be relaxed. Rushing through courses or pivoting too quickly to business topics can seem transactional and may undermine the trust you're working to establish. Gambians generally prefer to let conversation flow naturally, moving between personal and professional topics as rapport builds.

Rice is central to most meals, reflecting its role as a dietary staple. Dishes like benachin (a one-pot rice dish cooked with vegetables, fish, or meat) or domoda (a rich peanut stew served over rice) are widely enjoyed. Fish is especially common given the country's coastal location and rivers. If you have dietary restrictions, it's useful to mention them in advance, as vegetarian options may be limited outside of international hotel menus.

Sharing food is a sign of goodwill. Even when dishes are served individually, offering a taste or suggesting someone try a particular dish is a gesture of inclusion. Alcohol is rarely present outside of hotels and tourist-oriented venues, in line with the country's predominantly Muslim culture. Attaya, a strong sweet tea served in small glasses over multiple rounds, often appears later in the meal and offers a natural opportunity to deepen conversation without formality.
