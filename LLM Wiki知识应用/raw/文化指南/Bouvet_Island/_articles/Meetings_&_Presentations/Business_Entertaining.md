# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Bouvet Island is an uninhabited Norwegian dependency in the South Atlantic, covered almost entirely by glaciers. It has no permanent population, no commercial activity, and no infrastructure for meetings or hospitality. Business entertaining does not occur there.

The island is occasionally visited by scientific expeditions, but these are logistical operations rather than professional engagements. There are no restaurants, hotels, or venues of any kind. The climate is harsh, access is difficult, and the terrain offers no practical space for social or professional gatherings.

For organizations working on research projects in the Southern Ocean or Antarctic region, any relationship-building or hospitality connected to Bouvet Island would take place elsewhere—typically in Norway, South Africa, or another country with logistical ties to the area. In those settings, the cultural norms of the host country would apply.

If you are coordinating with teams involved in activity related to Bouvet Island, expect that any entertaining or informal relationship-building will happen far from the island itself, and will follow the practices of wherever that coordination is based.
