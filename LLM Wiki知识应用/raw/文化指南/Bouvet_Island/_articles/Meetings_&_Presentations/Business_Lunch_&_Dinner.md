# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Bouvet Island is an uninhabited Norwegian dependency in the South Atlantic, covered entirely by glaciers and without permanent infrastructure. There are no restaurants, no business districts, and no local population. The island has never supported human settlement and remains one of the most remote and inaccessible places on Earth.

For Norwegian entities conducting research or logistical operations related to Bouvet Island, any business meals would take place on the Norwegian mainland or aboard vessels, following standard Norwegian professional customs. In those settings, meals tend to be straightforward and egalitarian, with little emphasis on hierarchy or formality. Conversation is direct, and the meal itself is rarely used as a primary forum for negotiation or decision-making.

If your work involves Bouvet Island in any administrative or scientific capacity, clarify early where and with whom you'll actually be meeting. The cultural context will reflect the location of that meeting, not the island itself.
