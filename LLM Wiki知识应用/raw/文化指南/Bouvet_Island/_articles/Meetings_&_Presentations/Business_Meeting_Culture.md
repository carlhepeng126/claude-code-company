# Business Meeting Culture

## Meetings & Presentations

Bouvet Island is an uninhabited Norwegian dependency in the South Atlantic Ocean. It has no permanent population, no infrastructure for business, and no meeting culture of any kind. The island is a nature reserve covered in glaciers, accessible only by ship or helicopter in rare weather windows, and visited occasionally by scientists or government expeditions.

There are no offices, no boardrooms, and no professional collaboration taking place on Bouvet Island. Any reference to business meetings in this context would be entirely fictional. The island has no economy, no workforce, and no cultural norms around professional interaction, because no one lives or works there.

If you are preparing for meetings with Norwegian colleagues, or with researchers who have visited the island, you would be working within Norwegian professional culture, not a culture specific to Bouvet Island itself. That distinction matters, because the frame of reference shifts entirely to mainland Norway, where egalitarian decision-making, punctuality, and direct communication tend to shape how meetings unfold.
