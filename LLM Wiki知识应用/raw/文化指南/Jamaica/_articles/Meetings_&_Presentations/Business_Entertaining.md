# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Entertaining in Jamaica is about warmth and ease, not ceremony. The goal is to make people feel comfortable and welcome, to create an atmosphere where conversation flows naturally. This reflects a culture where relationships precede outcomes, and where hospitality is a way of showing respect and building trust. Accept invitations when they're extended, and reciprocate where you can. Declining too often can be read as disinterest or aloofness.

Social time is relationship-building time, but it also functions as character-reading. How you treat others, how you handle humor, whether you're consistent across settings—all of this is being observed, quietly but closely. Jamaica is a small society where reputation travels through networks, and how you show up socially becomes part of your professional credibility. This means business entertaining is rarely about business talk unless the host brings it up, and even then it may surface only lightly. Pushing the conversation toward outcomes too early can seem transactional.

Lunch is the most common setting, though evening gatherings happen too. Meals are often unhurried, and the pace may feel slower than in cultures where efficiency is the guiding rhythm. What matters is the quality of the connection, not the speed of the service. Music, energy, and atmosphere are often woven into the experience—these aren't background details but part of how comfort and belonging are created.

Be aware that tourism-facing venues and more local spaces operate differently. In local settings, tone and demeanor are read more carefully, and there may be stronger expectations around how you carry yourself. Following cues from your host is the clearest guide.
