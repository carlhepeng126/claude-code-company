# Forms of Address

## Making a Good Impression

In Grenada, forms of address tend to be warm but respectful, reflecting a culture where personal connection and deference to position both play a role. Titles such as "Mr.," "Mrs.," or "Miss" are commonly used alongside surnames in professional settings, especially when meeting someone for the first time or addressing a senior colleague or client. Professional titles like "Doctor" or "Engineer" are also recognized and appreciated. Using someone's title and surname shows that you understand the social courtesies that help establish trust and goodwill in early interactions.

As relationships develop, the shift to first names often happens naturally, but it usually follows an invitation rather than an assumption. A colleague from a culture where informality is immediate may move to first names quickly and find the initial formality slow or unnecessary. From a Grenadian perspective, that early informality can feel abrupt or overly familiar, particularly if the other person holds seniority or is not yet well known. Letting the Grenadian counterpart signal the move to first names avoids creating unintended distance or discomfort.

In written communication, maintaining a courteous tone is valued. Opening emails with a polite greeting and using full names or titles until informality is established helps set the right tone. Even as email exchanges become more frequent, a degree of formality in address often persists longer than in face-to-face settings, where warmth and humor can soften the boundaries more easily.
