# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Grenada are mostly informal, but they serve a real purpose. Shared food provides a natural setting to get to know someone beyond what they say in an office, and that knowledge often determines whether collaboration deepens. Invitations to lunch or dinner usually come once a working relationship has started to take shape, and accepting them is seen as a sign of genuine interest in the partnership.

Local restaurants are the most common venue, particularly those serving Grenadian dishes like oil down or callaloo. Food is a point of pride, and choosing a place with authentic flavours reflects a desire to share something meaningful. Conversations over meals tend to be relaxed and wide-ranging. Business may come up lightly, or not at all, and it's almost always the host who introduces it if it does. Raising work topics too early can feel transactional and cut short the opportunity to build trust through easier, more personal conversation.

Grenada is a Christian-majority country with relatively relaxed social norms, so alcohol may be offered at business meals, particularly rum-based drinks. That said, not everyone drinks, and assumptions shouldn't be made. It's best to follow the host's lead, and if you're hosting, it's courteous to offer options and read the table before ordering for yourself. Reciprocating an invitation, either during your visit or the next time you meet, is both appreciated and expected.
