# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

Rwanda is a linguistically unified society where almost everyone speaks Kinyarwanda, and that closeness shapes how professional connections are made. Stories and reputations move quickly, and people often assess you through a social map that already exists, rather than treating each introduction as a blank slate. A warm introduction from someone already embedded in that network can shift how quickly trust begins to form. Cold outreach is possible, but it places you outside the usual channels where people know how to read each other.

Trust here is built through consistency and observable seriousness, not quick promises or casual enthusiasm. In a context shaped by rupture and careful rebuilding, people watch for follow-through. Confirming meeting details, showing up as agreed, and closing loops quietly all register more strongly than energetic first impressions. Early messages work best when they are specific about purpose, context, and what you're asking for, and when they avoid over-familiarity. Warmth grows once reliability has been established.

Community participation carries real weight in how belonging is understood. Practices like umuganda, monthly community work, and the legacy of ubudehe, collective mutual support, reinforce the idea that membership is demonstrated through contribution. People may hold back access or openness until they sense you understand how the social fabric works. This can show up in scheduling as well: gentle follow-up and steady presence often work better than repeated urgency.

Plan appointments well in advance and reconfirm them shortly before, but keep your schedule loose. Meetings may shift, and flexibility signals commitment rather than inconvenience. April, especially the second week from the 7th to the 14th, is a period of intense mourning during the commemoration of the 1994 Genocide against the Tutsi, and many people do not work or attend social events. For the rest of the month, half-day schedules and no celebrations are common. Early July around public holidays is another time when availability narrows.
