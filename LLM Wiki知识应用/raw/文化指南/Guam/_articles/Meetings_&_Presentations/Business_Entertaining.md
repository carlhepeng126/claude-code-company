# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Guam serve a clear purpose: they allow people to get to know each other outside the meeting room. Relationships matter more than contracts at the outset, and sharing food creates the informal space where trust begins. Lunches are more common than dinners, often at local restaurants where Chamorro dishes, American-style grills, or Japanese cuisine might feature. The choice of venue may reflect the host's sense of what will make a guest feel comfortable, but it also reflects Guam's own culinary mix.

Conversations over meals tend to stay away from business unless the host brings it up. For a colleague from a culture where every interaction is an opportunity to move a deal forward, this can feel unclear or inefficient. From the Guam side, steering straight into negotiation over food may feel pushy or transactional. The meal itself builds the relationship, and that relationship shapes what happens next. If a visitor tries to use the occasion to close a point or clarify terms, they may find the response polite but evasive.

Alcohol is widely available and socially accepted, though not always expected. Some hosts will order it, others won't, and either choice is fine. It's better to wait and follow the host's lead rather than assuming the table will drink. Reciprocating an invitation, whether during the same visit or next time, shows respect for the gesture and keeps the relationship balanced.
