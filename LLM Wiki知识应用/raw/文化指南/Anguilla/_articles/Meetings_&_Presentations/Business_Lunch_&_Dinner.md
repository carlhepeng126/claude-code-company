# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Anguilla are rare and not part of the standard rhythm of professional life. Most business is conducted in offices during working hours, and suggesting a lunch or dinner meeting may come across as an unusual or overly formal move. The island's small size means that colleagues and clients often see each other socially outside of work anyway, so there's little perceived need to mix business with a formal meal setting. If a meal does happen, it's more likely to emerge from an existing relationship than as a way to initiate one.

When a business meal does take place, it will probably be casual in tone and setting. Local restaurants and beach bars are more common venues than hotel dining rooms. The focus will be on building rapport rather than advancing specific agenda items. Anguillians value personal connection, and a meal is seen as an extension of that, not a forum for negotiation or decision-making. A colleague from a culture where business lunches are used to close deals or clarify terms may find the conversation stays light and relational, which can feel indirect or unproductive. From an Anguillian perspective, a visiting counterpart who steers conversation toward contracts or timelines too quickly may seem transactional or pushy.

The pace is relaxed. Meals are not rushed, and there's no expectation that the conversation will stay on business throughout. If you're invited, treat it as a sign of goodwill, not a working session.
