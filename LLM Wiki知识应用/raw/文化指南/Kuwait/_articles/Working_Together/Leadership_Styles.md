# Leadership Styles

## Working Together

Many Kuwaiti leaders are expected to operate on three levels at once: as a patron who helps employees navigate state institutions, as a broker who can activate connections through wasta, and as a modern manager who understands strategy, performance targets, and international partnerships. Staff assess them across all three roles. A leader skilled in formal planning but unable to secure approvals through informal channels, or unwilling to support someone's family matter through the right ministry contact, will lose credibility. When working with a Kuwaiti leader, recognize that your partnership needs to strengthen their standing in all three arenas: organizational performance, social reputation, and political viability.

A leader's presence in the diwaniya, the communal gathering space where employees, constituents, and suppliers can meet informally, is part of how they lead. Being accessible there holds as much weight as formal office hours. Someone who avoids diwaniya scrutiny may be read as arrogant or detached. Don't assume your project can bypass these informal channels. Ask how your counterpart would like to introduce your work into their wider networks, and be prepared to explain it in settings that feel more social than strictly professional.

Leaders in ministries or state-owned firms sit at a crossroads of obligations: to the ruling family, to Parliament, to their tribe or extended family, and to professional standards. Their decisions must preserve that balance to avoid political or social backlash. If a decision stalls, it may be constrained by obligations you cannot see. Rather than pressing harder, ask who else needs to be reassured for the work to move forward. Younger or Western-educated leaders may push for process, data, and merit-based decisions, but they still operate within a system where older-style patrons, tribal figures, and parliamentarians can block or overrule them. Support reform-minded leaders without publicly criticizing the networks they still depend on to survive.
