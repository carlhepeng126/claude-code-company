# Forms of Address

## Making a Good Impression

Spanish honorifics like Señor, Señora, and professional titles such as Doctor or Licenciado are commonly paired with surnames in professional contexts, especially in early exchanges. These formal markers reflect respect for seniority and social standing, but they function inside a wider relational logic. Formality in Paraguay is often about signaling whether someone is treated as inside or outside the circle, rather than purely about rank. Respect is conveyed through both titles and tone, which means that sticking rigidly to honorifics without warmth can leave an interaction feeling cold or transactional. Wait to be invited before using first names, and use formal openings like Estimado Señor [Last Name] in writing, closing with Atentamente or Cordialmente.

The country's bilingual reality adds another layer. Spanish forms carry official seriousness, but Guaraní and jopara (the mixed form spoken widely) carry intimacy, humor, and shared identity. Which language register someone uses can signal closeness more powerfully than any title. For visitors, this means that an over-reliance on formal Spanish alone might keep you positioned as an outsider longer than necessary, even if the titles are correct. Guaraní is not a heritage language at the margins. It sits at the core of national identity, with active public concern around its preservation. Colleagues may shift fluidly between registers depending on who is present and how familiar they feel.

The speed at which people move to familiar naming varies by setting. In some urban professional environments, surnames hold until trust is built. In others, quick movement to nicknames or first names signals acceptance. The same naming choice can mean different things depending on whether the context is linked to family networks, rural community, or corporate formality. A colleague from a culture where first names are immediate might read the use of surnames as stiff or hierarchical. From a Paraguayan perspective, that same formality may simply mean the relationship is still being established and the person has not yet been brought inside.
