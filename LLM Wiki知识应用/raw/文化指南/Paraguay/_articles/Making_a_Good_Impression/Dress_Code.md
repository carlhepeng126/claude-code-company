# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

In Paraguay, being well put-together is a form of respect. Appearing polished in professional settings reflects how seriously you take the relationship and the occasion, rooted in a culture where visible care and presentation convey dignity and social seriousness. This is not about conspicuous display. Elegance here often means tailored attention to detail rather than understatement for its own sake. Men typically wear dark, well-fitted suits with ties and polished shoes. Women choose elegant dresses or tailored suits in neutral tones, with restrained accessories. Even on less formal days, smart trousers or skirts with collared shirts are preferred. If you arrive in business casual where Paraguayan colleagues are wearing suits, the contrast can read as a lack of investment in the meeting.

Climate shapes these choices in practical ways. Heat and humidity make light fabrics and breathable tailoring essential, and colleagues understand that comfort is part of maintaining professional dignity through long days. Choosing climate-appropriate fabrics is seen as realistic preparation, not a lowering of standards.

Traditional materials like ñandutí lace, a delicate embroidery with Guaraní heritage, may appear at cultural events or national celebrations. These are wearable markers of identity and collective memory, not everyday office wear, but recognizing them when they appear can open rapport. Religious and festive occasions, such as the Caacupé pilgrimage, bring their own visual language of devotion and participation, recalibrating what formality means in those moments.

When in doubt, dress more formally than you think necessary. The effort signals you understand the occasion and the people you are meeting.
