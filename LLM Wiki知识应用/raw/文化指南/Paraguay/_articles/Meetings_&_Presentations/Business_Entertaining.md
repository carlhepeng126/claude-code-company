# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Paraguay is rarely just a meal. It is where trust is observed, generosity is demonstrated, and social intelligence becomes visible. In settings where professional and personal networks overlap, especially outside Asunción, how you participate in informal gatherings can shape your reputation far beyond the event itself. Invitations to share food or drink offer a chance to build rapport in a space where warmth and respect matter as much as competence.

Tereré, a cold yerba mate infusion, is central to Paraguayan social life and recognized by UNESCO as living heritage. Sharing it is a structured ritual involving turn-taking, herb knowledge (pohã ñana), and an ethic of inclusion. Accepting the gourd when it is passed to you and following the rhythm of the circle shows you understand the cultural logic of participation and belonging. This is not a casual refreshment, it is a space where people observe whether you can join a shared practice with grace.

Meals often begin with personal conversation before any business is discussed, and it is typically the host who decides if or when work topics enter the room. Pushing too early can seem transactional. Traditional foods like chipa carry cultural weight, especially during religious seasons such as Holy Week, and treating them as identity markers rather than just snacks reflects attentiveness. Alcohol may be offered and is a sign of hospitality, but it is wise to follow the host's lead on pacing and toasts.

Reciprocating invitations when appropriate, arriving on time, and dressing well are all noticed. Conversations should remain harmonious, avoiding politics or religion unless the host opens the door. In a context where devotion and collective emotion sit comfortably in public life, respecting these boundaries supports the trust you are working to build.
