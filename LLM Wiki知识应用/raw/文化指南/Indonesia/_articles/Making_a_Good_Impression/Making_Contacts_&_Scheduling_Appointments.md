# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

Getting through the door in Indonesia depends less on a polished email and more on who introduces you. People gravitate toward *orang sendiri* (literally "our own people"), whether that means alumni from the same school or *pesantren*, members of the same ethnic network, or contacts vouched for through shared WhatsApp groups. An introduction from a trusted *Bapak* or *Ibu* opens conversations that a perfect deck will not. Before reaching out cold, ask your local partner, a university alumni contact, or a community or religious figure to connect you. For high-level government or state enterprise meetings, this relational groundwork often decides whether your call gets answered at all.

Approach and tempo shift noticeably between Jakarta and the regions. In the capital, corporate and ministry environments expect formal emails, calendar invitations, and quicker turnaround. In provincial and district settings, relationships still form through local officials, *adat* leaders, religious figures, and family ties, with more time given to courtesy visits and small talk before business gets named. Adjust your preparation accordingly: more documentation and formality in Jakarta, more protocol and face-to-face rapport building outside it.

*Jam karet* (rubber time) is real but selective. People may arrive late to a community event yet be punctual for a ministerial meeting, even as everyone still describes time as flexible. The flexibility applies more to internal or social gatherings than to encounters with senior figures or clients. If timing genuinely matters, say so respectfully and early. Reconfirm appointments a day or two before, and allow buffer between meetings since they often run longer than scheduled.

Once contact is made, WhatsApp becomes the real scheduler. Even when meetings are set by email, last-minute changes, confirmations, and check-ins happen by message. Use it to stay responsive and keep the relationship warm, but only after consent.
