# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Indonesia happens in family-style restaurants, mall cafés, or seafood spots by the sea, and the setting is often loud and lively. Music, overlapping conversations, and a warm buzz are part of the atmosphere. Quiet formality is not the goal. These meals exist to deepen connection and show respect through shared time, not just to exchange information. If you are invited, accept unless you have a very good reason to decline. Turning down an invitation can leave the host feeling exposed, as though their gesture of goodwill has been rejected.

Alcohol is context-dependent. In Muslim-majority settings, it is usually absent or discreet. In Bali, Batak areas, or certain Chinese-Indonesian circles, beer or spirits may appear without hesitation. Reading the religious and ethnic mix of the group before ordering drinks avoids awkwardness. Let your host set the tone, and do not assume that urban or international settings mean alcohol is expected.

Kekeluargaan (a family-like ethos) shapes how relationships develop over meals. It is common for spouses or children to be mentioned, and sometimes present, especially as trust builds. Asking warmly about family, hometowns, or local food stories fits the rhythm of conversation and strengthens rapport. Business may come up, but only if your counterpart raises it first. Pushing the agenda too early can make the gathering feel transactional rather than relational.

Reciprocating an invitation, either during your visit or later, is appreciated. Choosing a venue that reflects the importance of the relationship is more meaningful than choosing somewhere personally familiar. If your counterpart selects the place, follow their lead on formality, pace, and whether business enters the conversation at all.
