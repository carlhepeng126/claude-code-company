# Teamwork

## Working Together

Mozambican teamwork draws strength from traditions of synchrony and shared survival. Chopi timbila orchestras, where multiple xylophones become a single instrument through mutual listening and collective endurance, model a kind of coordination that values how well a group functions together rather than who stands out. This aesthetic of ensemble competence shows up in the workplace as a preference for visible interdependence. When roles are clearly defined and people can see how their work connects, teams tend to perform better. What matters is becoming part of a reliable whole, not proving individual mastery.

Extended family, church, mosque, and neighborhood networks function as practical safety systems in settings where formal infrastructure is often strained. This makes reliability for the group a recognizable form of integrity. Colleagues who show up consistently for each other build trust quickly, because mutual support is understood as more than professional courtesy. It reflects a culturally legible way of being dependable. In teams with members from cultures where individual accountability is emphasized more strongly, this collective reliability may read as diffused responsibility. From the other direction, a colleague working independently without frequent check-ins may seem disconnected from the group's shared rhythm.

In northern regions, particularly among Makhuwa communities, matrilineal kinship structures shape how obligations and continuity are understood. Authority and belonging often trace through maternal lines, which can affect whose commitments hold weight and how team members prioritize family or community responsibilities. Managers working with northern-linked teams may need to account for these patterns when planning schedules or assigning accountability. In displacement-affected contexts, especially following the Cabo Delgado crisis, trust-building may need to be more explicit and sustained. Networks have been torn and reformed repeatedly, so teams may require extra time and visible consistency to rebuild workable collaboration.
