# Effective Presentations

## Meetings & Presentations

Presentations in Mozambique rely heavily on how content is delivered, because oral transmission remains a deeply credible channel. Many Mozambican knowledge systems are still shared through voice, repetition, and apprenticeship rather than text alone, particularly where schooling language and home language differ. This makes presence, pacing, and relational clarity socially meaningful. If you rush through slides or rely on written detail to do the explaining, your message may feel incomplete or disconnected. A colleague from a culture that treats slides as the primary information source may wonder why the presenter is repeating what's already visible, while Mozambican audiences may see that repetition and vocal delivery as the actual proof of understanding and intent.

Visual elements help, especially when they support rather than replace spoken explanation. Keep slides clean and image-driven. Dense text can create confusion, particularly if you're working with an interpreter or presenting to an audience that reads Portuguese more formally than they speak it. Allow time at the start to introduce yourself and establish context through brief, warm stories. This isn't small talk, it's how you become real to the room. Humor can be used, but keep it general and avoid anything that might echo colonial attitudes or religious insensitivity, given that audiences may be Christian or Muslim.

Expect interruptions. People may take calls, step out, or join partway through. This reflects how hierarchy and collective participation work together. Address the room generally, but acknowledge the most senior people with occasional eye contact. Questions often come at the end, so build in time for them. Some colleagues may prefer to ask clarifying or sensitive questions privately afterward to avoid losing face in front of others, and these follow-up conversations often surface the most honest feedback. Treat them as part of the presentation itself, especially if the final decision-maker wasn't in the room.
