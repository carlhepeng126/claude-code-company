# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

Guernsey's professional dress reflects the island's hybrid identity: shaped by close ties to Britain and the formality of its well-established finance sector, but with an informality rooted in its small size and coastal setting. In banking, insurance, and law, business dress remains the norm. Men wear suits with ties, women wear tailored separates or dresses, and the overall standard leans conservative. This formality is expected at client-facing meetings and in larger, established firms, where appearance continues to be treated as a marker of professionalism and reliability.

Outside these sectors, norms are more relaxed. In smaller businesses, tech companies, or creative fields, business casual is common year-round, and ties are often dropped. Office culture tends to be pragmatic rather than rigid. Still, even here, there's rarely a sense of carelessness. People dress neatly and appropriately to the occasion, aware that the island's small professional community makes every interaction visible. Turning up underdressed to a first meeting can create an impression that's hard to undo.

Seasonal variation also plays a role. Summer brings lighter fabrics and a slightly more casual feel, especially in offices away from St Peter Port, but shorts and beachwear remain firmly outside workplace norms. The shift is subtle, and context still drives choice. A meeting with a fund administrator or a legal team in July may still call for a jacket, even if the weather doesn't.

For visitors, the safest approach is to dress one notch more formally than expected, especially early in a relationship. Once rapport is established, others will signal where flexibility exists, but arriving polished shows respect for the local professional culture and for the care Guernsey's business community places on reputation.
