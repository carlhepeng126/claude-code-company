# Forms of Address

## Making a Good Impression

Guernsey's professional culture blends British formality with island-scale familiarity. In initial business meetings, titles and surnames are standard. Use Mr., Mrs., or Miss followed by the surname unless a professional title applies, particularly with senior figures or anyone in finance, legal, or regulatory roles where hierarchy is more pronounced. This deference reflects the island's conservative professional traditions and a respect for established authority that runs through local institutions.

The shift to first names happens quickly once rapport begins to build, often after a first meeting or within early email exchanges. The invitation may be explicit or simply modeled by the other person signing off with their given name. What matters is reading the cue rather than forcing informality too soon. A colleague from a more egalitarian culture might assume first names from the outset, which can feel presumptuous to islanders who see the transition as something earned through early trust, not granted automatically.

Written communication follows the same arc. Initial emails open with formal salutations and full names. As the relationship develops, tone softens and formality recedes, though respect for seniority remains visible in how people sign off and address authority figures. In local government or finance settings, where institutional hierarchy is clearer, formality tends to hold longer than in smaller businesses or creative sectors, where island familiarity can override convention more readily.
