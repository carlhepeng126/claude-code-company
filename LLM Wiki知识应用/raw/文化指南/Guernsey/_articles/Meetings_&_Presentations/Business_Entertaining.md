# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Guernsey exists, but it tends to be low-key and purposeful rather than performative. Meals are more likely once a working relationship has taken root than during early introductions. The setting is usually a quiet local restaurant or hotel, and the tone is restrained. Guernsey's small size and long memories mean that discretion and understatement are valued more than showiness. A modest dinner is seen as a practical continuation of the working relationship, not a chance to impress.

Alcohol may be offered but never pushed. Wine or beer is common, though many people in professional settings drink sparingly or not at all, reflecting Guernsey's historically moderate social norms and its blend of British and Norman cultural influences. If a host orders a drink, it's safe to follow. If they don't, it's better to wait or decline. Pressing someone to drink, or ordering without reading the room, risks misreading the culture's preference for restraint over indulgence.

Conversation stays broad and light. Business may come up briefly, but it's rarely the focus. The meal itself serves to confirm that the working relationship is solid and that people are comfortable with one another. For a colleague from a culture where meals are used to negotiate or advance deals, this can seem passive. For someone from Guernsey, a meal that feels like a pitch meeting can seem rushed and transactional. The gathering is the work, not the prelude to it.
