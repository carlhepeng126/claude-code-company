# Sharing Information

## Working Together

In Bulgarian workplaces, people draw a clear line between what is written down and what is actually happening. The official version that appears in documents and formal communications often exists alongside a messier, more nuanced reality discussed face-to-face or over the phone. This split reflects decades of experience where formal records served bureaucratic purposes while real decisions and risks were communicated through trusted relationships. If you rely only on written updates or formal reports, you may miss the unwritten context that shapes how work actually gets done. Building informal networks and making time for corridor conversations will give you access to the fuller picture.

Bulgarians are cautious about what they put in writing, especially on sensitive topics like internal politics, client issues, or regulatory concerns. A long, detailed email that documents every complication can make colleagues uncomfortable, particularly if it creates a permanent record of something better handled through quiet conversation. What might feel like transparency in one culture can feel like unnecessary exposure in another. Check with local colleagues about what is normal to email versus what should be discussed in person or by phone, and adjust your own communication habits accordingly.

When Bulgarians explain a situation, they often provide rich historical context: who was involved before, which director changed, what happened during the last audit. This backstory is not filler. It carries implicit warnings about relationships, risks, and patterns that might repeat. A colleague from a culture that values concise, forward-looking updates may find this level of detail slow or unfocused. From the Bulgarian side, a brisk summary without context can seem dangerously incomplete, as though the person either does not understand the situation or is unwilling to share what they know. Make time to listen to the full explanation. The detail often contains the information you need to navigate what comes next.
