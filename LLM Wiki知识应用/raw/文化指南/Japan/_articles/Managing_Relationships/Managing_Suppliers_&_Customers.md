# Managing Suppliers & Customers

## Managing Relationships

Japanese buyer-supplier relationships are shaped by decades of interdependence, even where formal keiretsu networks have loosened. Manufacturers, tiered suppliers, trading companies, and logistics firms remain woven together over long timescales, and switching partners means unravelling parts of that fabric. A decision to change supplier is rarely just commercial; it affects forecasts, quality processes, and mutual commitments built up over years. Foreign partners proposing a switch based on cost or technology alone often find the conversation stalls, not because the offer lacks merit, but because continuity and predictability themselves hold value. If you need to propose a change, help your counterpart see how the whole chain can move safely, not just your part of it.

The phrase *okyakusama wa kamisama* ("the customer is god") remains a live expectation in business-to-business settings. Many suppliers accept punishing demands, tight specifications, or emergency orders to protect a major customer relationship, absorbing the cost as a point of pride. When something goes wrong, a recall, late delivery, or visible defect triggers intense internal scrutiny and public self-critique. How a supplier responds in that moment defines whether the relationship survives. The tone of apology and restitution matters as much as the technical fix. If you are co-managing a Japanese supplier or customer, be ready to stand beside them in front of the client when things go wrong, sharing responsibility instead of distancing yourself.

Japanese buyers remain wary of foreign partners who can disappear: companies with no local presence, no Japanese-speaking staff, and no one who will physically show up during a crisis. Trust grows when you anchor yourself in Japan through offices, long-resident staff, or stable distributors. Plan not just for the sale, but for a decade or more of co-existence: shared forecasts, joint improvement programs, and crisis scenarios rehearsed together.
