# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Zimbabwe is built around care and connection, with social invitations seen as meaningful gestures of welcome and respect. Hosting someone, even simply, reflects a moral commitment to making them feel safe and valued. This approach is shaped by resilience through hardship and by cultural traditions like *roora* or *lobola* (a marriage practice that formalizes relationships through family involvement), which reflect a broader logic that relationships are accountable to wider networks. Entertaining is therefore read as a signal of seriousness and respect, not just a calendar event.

The tone and setting can vary considerably depending on faith community and personal background. Pentecostal and Apostolic influence in some circles means that alcohol, nightlife, or certain social settings may be avoided entirely. Two colleagues in the same organization may navigate very different social worlds, so what feels appropriate to one may exclude the other. Checking preferences or offering options that accommodate varying comfort levels helps ensure everyone feels included.

Understated generosity tends to land better than conspicuous display. Years of economic volatility have made many Zimbabweans sensitive to extravagance. Warmth, effort, and thoughtfulness preserve dignity more effectively than lavish spending. In Harare, diaspora-linked tastes may influence venue choices toward more cosmopolitan or modern settings, while Bulawayo and smaller centers often lean toward relationship-paced, less showy hospitality. The key is appropriateness to the people present, not prestige alone.

Reciprocating an invitation, whether during a visit or later, is appreciated and reinforces mutual regard. The host typically pays, so be sensitive if a colleague extends an invitation that may stretch their budget. Offering to pay when you've been invited can come across as dismissive, but structuring future interactions so you host in return shows thoughtfulness and maintains balance.
