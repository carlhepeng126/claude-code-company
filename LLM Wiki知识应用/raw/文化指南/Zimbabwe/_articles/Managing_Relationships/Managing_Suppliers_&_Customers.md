# Managing Suppliers & Customers

## Managing Relationships

In Zimbabwe, supplier and customer relationships rest on credibility that builds over time. Because personal and professional networks overlap through community, church, school, and hometown connections, reputation becomes a social asset that travels quickly. Consistency in what you say and do, especially when conditions shift, builds the trust that underpins commercial relationships. Past experience with hyperinflation has created a lasting cultural habit of skepticism toward promises that stretch into uncertain futures. People trust what they see demonstrated repeatedly—track records, small pilot steps, clear next actions—more than confident projections. If you want to build confidence with a new customer or supplier, show proof through steady delivery, not just well-framed intentions.

Even in formal procurement settings, many Zimbabweans bring familiarity with relational commerce from everyday trading ecosystems and informal markets. This means trust is often judged through the quality of the interaction as much as the terms themselves. How you treat people carries reputational weight. Dismissiveness, public blame, or talking down can violate dignity codes and spread through networks, damaging credibility in ways that go beyond the immediate transaction. When problems arise, address them discreetly, protect dignity, and focus on repair rather than fault. A colleague from a culture where directness is valued for efficiency might see this as avoidance, while a Zimbabwean counterpart might read blunt criticism as disrespect that undermines future cooperation.

Practical improvisation, known locally as *kukiya-kiya* (making a plan under constraint), is culturally normal and often admired when handled with calm competence. A supplier who finds a workaround to meet a deadline without drama demonstrates partnership, not corner-cutting, as long as the solution preserves quality and dignity. Regular face-to-face contact reinforces continuity and signals that the relationship extends beyond a single transaction. Appointing a local representative who can maintain these connections when you are not present ensures relationships remain active and responsive.
