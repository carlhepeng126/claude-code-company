# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Bosnia and Herzegovina follow a slow, relational rhythm. The meal is rarely just a transaction. Conversation builds gradually, often starting with family, health, or shared connections before moving toward business. Rushing this opening phase or steering too quickly to the agenda can feel abrupt and may undermine the trust being built at the table. Colleagues expect the meal to breathe, with enough time to settle into the conversation and each other's company.

Coffee, usually a strong Bosnian-style brew served in small cups, frequently marks the end of the meal. It signals a moment to slow down rather than wrap up quickly. If someone suggests coffee, it often means the formal part of the meal is transitioning, but the conversation may continue for some time. Leaving immediately after coffee can feel rushed or impersonal, particularly if the relationship is still developing.

Alcohol may or may not be present, depending on your colleague's background and the setting. Bosnia and Herzegovina's religious and ethnic diversity means that some colleagues will drink and others will not, and this is rarely discussed in advance. Follow the lead of your host. If alcohol is offered, it's usually wine or rakija, a strong fruit brandy. If it's not, no explanation is needed. Assuming everyone will drink, or treating abstinence as unusual, can create unnecessary awkwardness.

Sharing food is common. Dishes may arrive in the center of the table, and offering something from your plate to others reflects warmth and inclusion. Declining repeatedly, or eating alone without engaging others, can come across as distant.
