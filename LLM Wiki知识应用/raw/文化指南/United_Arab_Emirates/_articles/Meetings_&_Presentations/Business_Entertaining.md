# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in the UAE unfolds across distinct social terrains, and the venue itself quietly tells you where you stand. Corporate dinners and networking often take place in hotel majlis spaces, rooftop lounges, or private hotel rooms designed to echo traditional hospitality without crossing into personal territory. These settings allow for relationship building in a semi-formal way, appropriate for newer contacts or commercial partners. An invitation to a family majlis in a villa or traditional home, by contrast, reflects deep trust and long-term thinking. These are spaces where reputations are built, alliances remembered, and connections move beyond the transactional. The shift from one setting to another is gradual and earned, not requested.

During Ramadan, iftar tents and suhoor gatherings become central stages for corporate life. Companies and government entities host large iftars where senior Emiratis, key clients, and decision-makers gather. Who attends which table, and who sits beside you, reflects the depth and visibility of the relationship. Arriving on time, dressing modestly, and recognizing the gathering as a serious investment rather than a courtesy event will shape how you are remembered. These occasions also introduce you to wider networks, and the connections made in one tent often resurface months later in unrelated contexts.

Gender considerations and venue tone matter. Mixed-gender entertaining is standard in many hotels and free zones, but some Emirati women and men prefer family sections or women-only spaces, particularly when relatives are present. Traditional families may choose family-oriented restaurants over bars or nightlife venues. When hosting, select places that protect your guests' dignity and religious comfort. Putting someone in a setting where alcohol or atmosphere could later reflect badly on them undermines the relationship you are trying to build. The Friday brunch culture illustrates this clearly: early, family-friendly buffets attract Emirati and GCC families, while later, alcohol-heavy brunches are largely expat territory. Be explicit about the tone of any event so people can decide comfortably whether to attend.
