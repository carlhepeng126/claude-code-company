# Being a Host & Guest

## Meetings & Presentations

Hospitality in the UAE is rooted in long-standing social and political forms, most notably the majlis, a reception space where community and business overlap. Within a traditional majlis, order governs nearly everything: who is greeted first (elders before others), how coffee is poured and offered, and where people sit. These are elements of an embedded etiquette system, and being invited into a ruler's or senior family's majlis reflects both social trust and political recognition. Some majlis are open community gatherings; others are tightly curated. As a guest, let your host or a local colleague quietly guide you on when to sit, when to accept refreshment, and when to leave. Stay long enough to show appreciation, but leave when conversation winds down rather than overstaying.

Many Emirati families host separate women's majlis, which can be livelier and more informal than their male equivalents. Foreign businesswomen may be invited once trust is established, and these spaces offer powerful opportunities for relationship-building. Privacy around family life runs deep. Asking questions about home arrangements, taking photos, or posting on social media without permission can damage rapport quickly. Households often have live-in domestic workers, and your behavior toward them is noted. Treat staff with visible courtesy but without interrogation, as your host will assess both your respect and your discretion.

Gift-giving reflects thoughtfulness rather than expense. Dates, oud, local sweets, or something tasteful from your home country are welcome. Items involving alcohol, provocative imagery, or non-halal food are inappropriate. For senior figures, a well-chosen book or object that reflects their known interests or projects conveys attention in a way that something generic and costly does not. When you host Emiratis yourself, recreate elements of the majlis where possible: comfortable seating, good Arabic coffee and dates, and space for unhurried conversation, rather than only a rushed restaurant meeting.
