# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Lunch in Sweden is functional and social at once, typically lasting 45 to 60 minutes. People often eat in workplace canteens or simple nearby restaurants, and they expect to return to work on time. The meal is light, often a sandwich or salad, and the conversation flows easily without needing to pivot formally into business topics. If your Swedish colleague raises work matters over lunch, the door is open. If they don't, forcing the agenda can feel transactional. Choose a venue that's easy to reach and fits the time window rather than planning a drawn-out midday event.

Dietary awareness runs deeper than it once did. Vegetarian and vegan options are common, and increasing diversity means halal, kosher, or allergy-related needs may be present without being announced in advance. Asking about preferences when you book signals respect and thoughtfulness. It also avoids awkwardness later when someone is left with little to choose from.

Alcohol follows the context. A glass of wine or beer with dinner is normal and unremarkable, but multiple rounds at a weekday lunch would stand out. Swedes calibrate their drinking to the formality and timing of the occasion, so keeping weekday lunches alcohol-light or alcohol-free aligns with the rhythm most people expect. Save wine for deliberate evening gatherings if the relationship and setting call for it.
