# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Sweden tends to be modest and informal. Lunches, after-work drinks, or a straightforward dinner are the norm. The emphasis is on ease, not display. Lavish hospitality can feel uncomfortable or even suspicious, reflecting a culture where equality and restraint are valued over extravagance. A cosy bistro will often land better than a gilded dining room. The goal is to create space for conversation and connection, rather than to impress through expense.

Alcohol is common at business meals and social gatherings — beer and wine especially — but there is growing awareness around inclusion and Sweden's history with alcohol-related problems. Getting visibly drunk at a work event is increasingly frowned upon. Don't assume everyone will drink, and don't press the point. Provide good non-alcoholic options and keep your own consumption moderate. This approach reflects both a broader cultural shift toward well-being and a practical awareness of who is and isn't comfortable drinking.

An invitation to join someone for a walk, a sailing trip, cross-country skiing, or a visit to a summer cottage is a different order of gesture. It signals real trust and a willingness to invest in the relationship. Swedes often blend business and their deep connection to nature, and being invited into that space is meaningful. Show up on time, offer to help, and respect the environment. These outings are not just pleasant diversions — they're relationship capital. Treat them as such.

Spouses may be invited to dinners, though less often to lunches. Conversation at meals is often relaxed and wide-ranging, and business may come up only lightly, or not at all. The gathering itself is the point: a way to understand each other beyond the meeting room. Reciprocating an invitation, whether during your visit or later, is appreciated and remembered.
