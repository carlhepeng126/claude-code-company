# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

Professional appearance in Georgia communicates care and commitment. How you present yourself registers as a sign of how seriously you take the relationship. In most business settings, men wear dark suits with conservative ties, and women opt for tailored suits, dresses, or smart blouses with skirts or trousers. The emphasis sits on being well put together rather than on visible expense or boldness. What you wear tells your counterpart that you've prepared for the meeting, and that preparation is noticed.

The expected level of formality shifts with sector and setting. In state institutions, banking, and traditional industries, dark suits and formal tailoring remain standard. In younger sectors like technology and creative services, business casual is more common and may reflect current trends. Even in these less formal environments, though, people dress with intention. Showing up in something that feels careless or thrown together can undermine credibility before the conversation begins. If you're uncertain about what's expected, aim slightly more formal. You can adjust once you've seen how colleagues dress.

Evening occasions call for more polish. At a good restaurant or cultural event, your host will likely arrive dressed sharply, and matching that effort shows respect for the occasion. A well-fitted jacket or a stylish dress fits naturally into these settings.

If the relationship extends to a visit to an Orthodox church or other religious site, modesty becomes the norm. Keep shoulders covered, avoid shorts, and ensure skirts fall below the knee. These adjustments, though small, show attentiveness to context and will be read as genuine respect.
