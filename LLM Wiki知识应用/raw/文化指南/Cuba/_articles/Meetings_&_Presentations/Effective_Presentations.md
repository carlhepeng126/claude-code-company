# Effective Presentations

## Meetings & Presentations

Presentations in Cuba are opportunities to build trust and demonstrate respect, not just deliver information. Your audience will assess both your content and your approach, watching for signs of genuine collaboration rather than imposition. Avoid anything that feels like a hard sell or sounds scripted for foreign investors. Cubans value sincerity, and a polished but impersonal delivery can land as distant or transactional. Focus instead on building credibility through your track record, relevant examples, and clear interest in long-term partnership.

Storytelling resonates strongly. Cuba's culture has deep roots in oral tradition, and framing ideas through real-world scenarios or case studies makes your message more tangible and memorable. Anchor your points to situations that feel relevant to the people in the room. Emotional connection often matters as much as technical detail, so aim to bring your message to life rather than relying solely on data.

Pacing should be unhurried. If you're working through an interpreter, allow time for each point to be conveyed clearly. Silence after a question or a key statement reflects respect or consideration, not disengagement. Resist the urge to fill every pause. People may also step out to take calls or join partway through. Welcome late arrivals warmly rather than viewing them as disruptions.

Supporting materials should be clear and visual. Dense slides rarely translate well and can create confusion, especially if technical terms are unfamiliar. If you're using specialized language, brief your interpreter in advance to ensure accuracy. Keep your tone collaborative and grounded. A presenter who listens and adapts leaves a stronger impression than one who delivers a flawless deck with little room for dialogue.
