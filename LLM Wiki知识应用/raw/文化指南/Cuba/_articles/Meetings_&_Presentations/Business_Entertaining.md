# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Cuba happens infrequently and reflects both economic reality and the dual economy. Access to restaurants suitable for hosting international guests is limited and often concentrated in tourist areas or venues that operate in convertible currency. Many Cuban professionals simply do not have the financial means to extend invitations in formal dining settings, and when they do, it represents a considerable personal investment. Foreign guests who receive an invitation should recognize the gesture as one of real significance.

When entertaining does take place, it tends to happen outside working hours and moves at an unhurried pace. Meals are seen as opportunities to deepen personal connection, and business talk typically waits until trust has been established. A Cuban colleague may prioritize understanding who you are as a person before engaging with the transactional side of the relationship. Raising business topics too early can feel rushed or instrumental, particularly if the host has gone to some effort to arrange the occasion.

Reciprocating an invitation is appreciated, though it should be handled with care. Offering to host in a venue that operates in hard currency can inadvertently highlight income disparities. A practical alternative is to invite your Cuban counterpart to join you for coffee or a light meal in a setting that feels comfortable for both sides, acknowledging the gesture without creating awkwardness around cost.
