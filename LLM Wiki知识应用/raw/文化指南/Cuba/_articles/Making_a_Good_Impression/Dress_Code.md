# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

Professional appearance in Cuba tends toward formality, especially in business and government settings. Men typically wear long trousers and collared shirts, often with a guayabera, a lightweight embroidered shirt with four pockets that serves as formal business attire in the tropical climate. Women wear dresses, skirts, or tailored trousers with blouses. Cubans take care with grooming and presentation, seeing how someone dresses as a sign of seriousness and respect for the occasion. Arriving well-groomed and appropriately formal shows you take the meeting and the people in it seriously.

The guayabera itself reflects a Cuban adaptation to heat without sacrificing professionalism. Originally worn in rural areas, it became the official formal dress for Cuban men and is still the standard in government offices, state enterprises, and formal business contexts. For international visitors, wearing a guayabera can be well-received, though it should be pressed and well-fitted. A suit jacket is also acceptable, but comfort in the heat is understood.

Dress codes relax slightly in newer sectors like tourism or joint ventures, where business casual may appear, but even here the overall sense of presentation remains. Cubans notice when someone dresses carelessly, and that lack of effort can read as disrespect or a lack of investment in the relationship. Matching the level of formality your Cuban colleagues bring to the table helps set the right tone from the start.
