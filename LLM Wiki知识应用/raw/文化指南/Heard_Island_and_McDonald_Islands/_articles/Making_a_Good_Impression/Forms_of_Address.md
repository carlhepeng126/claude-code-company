# Forms of Address

## Making a Good Impression

Heard Island and McDonald Islands are uninhabited Australian external territories in the southern Indian Ocean, used only for scientific research. There is no resident population, no local workplace culture, and no established social customs around forms of address. The islands function as field research stations visited by rotating teams of scientists and support staff, typically Australian, who work under the protocols of their home institutions and the Australian Antarctic Division.

In this context, forms of address follow the norms of the research teams and national agencies coordinating the work. Australian scientists and personnel generally use first names quickly, reflecting a preference for informality and flat hierarchies common in Australian professional settings. Titles are used sparingly, typically reserved for formal correspondence or when clarity around academic or professional rank is needed. On-site, the emphasis is on practical collaboration in a remote and physically demanding environment, where formality can feel out of place.

For international researchers joining expeditions to the islands, it is worth noting that the default tone is collegial and direct. Waiting for an invitation to use a first name, or maintaining formal address over weeks of close fieldwork, may feel mismatched to the setting and could create unnecessary distance in a small, isolated team.
