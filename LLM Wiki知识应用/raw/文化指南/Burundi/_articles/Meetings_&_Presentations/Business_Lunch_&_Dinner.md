# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Burundi often take place in restaurants or hotels, particularly in Bujumbura, and tend to be quiet, measured occasions. The pace is slow and conversation builds gradually. Meals begin with polite exchanges about family or health, and business topics emerge later, if at all. Rushing into work matters too early can feel transactional and may weaken trust. For colleagues from cultures where lunch is a time to move deals forward, this can feel indirect. From a Burundian perspective, a meal that opens with business talk can seem impersonal or opportunistic.

Food reflects Belgian colonial influence and regional East African traditions. Dishes like grilled fish from Lake Tanganyika or goat brochettes may appear, often served with rice, beans, or fried plantains. Beer, particularly local brands like Primus, is widely consumed and may be offered during business dinners. Accepting a drink, even if you don't finish it, is a gesture of goodwill. If alcohol is not appropriate for you, a soft drink works just as well, provided you join in the social rhythm.

Hospitality is a serious responsibility. If a Burundian colleague invites you to a meal, expect them to cover the bill. Offering to split or insisting on paying can feel like a rejection of the relationship. Let the host guide the timing and tone. Meals rarely end abruptly; the close is signaled by the offer of tea or coffee, not by discussion winding down.
