# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Greenland is informal and rarely driven by a sense of obligation. Meetings tend to end without the expectation of a shared meal, and formal dinners are uncommon in most professional contexts. When meals do happen, they are usually low-key and reflect the practical rhythms of daily life rather than structured relationship-building rituals. If a colleague invites you to join them for lunch or coffee, it's more likely a sign of personal goodwill than a strategic move.

When entertaining does occur, it often takes place in simple settings such as cafés, hotel restaurants, or occasionally at home. In smaller towns, options are limited and gatherings may happen around shared food brought in or prepared together. The tone is relaxed and conversation tends to roam across personal and professional topics without clear boundaries. Business may come up naturally, but it's rarely the purpose of the meal. Pushing toward outcomes or treating the occasion as a networking opportunity can feel out of step with the wider cultural preference for keeping professional life understated.

Alcohol is present in some settings but not universally consumed. Many Greenlanders do not drink, and preferences vary across communities and individuals. Offering or ordering alcohol without reading the tone of the group can create discomfort. Following the host's lead avoids missteps. Reciprocating an invitation, when it feels natural, is appreciated but not expected as a formal courtesy.
