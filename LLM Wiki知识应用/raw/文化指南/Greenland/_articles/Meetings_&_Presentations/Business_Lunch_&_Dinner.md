# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Greenland's business meal culture reflects a wider social norm of informality and equality. Formal business dinners are uncommon, and when colleagues or partners share a meal, the tone is relaxed. Meals are more about building connection than conducting structured business discussions. Conversations may touch on work, but they often move toward family, nature, or shared interests. Pushing an agenda too hard during a meal can feel out of step with the pace and purpose of the gathering.

The food itself is often straightforward. Traditional dishes might include fish, seal, or reindeer, but international cuisine is also common in Nuuk and larger towns. Portions tend to reflect practical eating rather than elaborate presentation. If you're visiting a home, the setting is likely to be modest and unpretentious. Greenlandic hosts do not seek to impress through formality or abundance, and guests who respond with genuine appreciation for what is offered show respect for the culture's understated approach.

The absence of rigid etiquette can be misread. A colleague from a culture where meals follow a clear sequence of courses and cues may find Greenlandic meals less structured and wonder when it's appropriate to begin eating or when the meal is over. From the Greenlandic side, a guest who waits for formal signals or defers excessively to hierarchy may seem unnecessarily stiff. Watching others and following their lead is usually enough.
