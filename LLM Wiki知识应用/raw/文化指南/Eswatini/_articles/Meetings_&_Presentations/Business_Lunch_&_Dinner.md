# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Eswatini tend to follow a relaxed structure, especially when relationships are still forming. The meal itself is often an extension of the relationship-building process rather than a working session with an agenda. Colleagues may suggest lunch or dinner at a hotel restaurant, particularly in Mbabane or Manzini, where international and local dishes are served in a comfortable setting. The conversation usually stays light at first, touching on family, community, or observations about the country before any work-related discussion emerges naturally.

Hierarchy shows up quietly but consistently. Senior figures are typically served first, and their lead in conversation is noticed and respected. If you're hosting, defer to the most senior person present when ordering begins or when the meal is ready to start. Jumping in too quickly, or steering the conversation toward business outcomes early in the meal, can feel transactional and may undermine the relational foundation that Swazi colleagues are still assessing.

Meals move at a gentle pace. There's no expectation to fill every silence or to treat the occasion as a timed event. Colleagues from cultures where meals are brief and efficient may find this rhythm slower than expected, while a Swazi colleague dining abroad might read a hurried meal as a missed opportunity to understand the person behind the role. Allowing the meal to unfold without forcing conclusions or decisions demonstrates respect for the relational work being done.

Alcohol may be offered in hotels and some restaurants, but it's not central to the occasion. If you're uncertain about preferences, it's safer to follow your host's lead rather than assume.
