# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

Finnish professional networks often grow through sustained participation in clubs, professional associations, hobby groups and university cohorts rather than through large networking events or small talk. The person who knows you from a sailing club or student guild may quietly recommend you years later when a tender committee reviews candidates. This pattern reflects a broader cultural preference for trust built through shared activity over time rather than through social performance. If you are entering a specific sector, mapping the relevant association or regional cluster and finding a way into that circle will open more doors than generic outreach.

Cold emails and LinkedIn messages are widely accepted if they are short, concrete and free of unnecessary warmth. State who you are, what you propose, and why it is relevant to the recipient. Overly friendly or sales-driven introductions can trigger suspicion rather than engagement. Finns respect evidence that you have done your homework, so referencing the company's website, annual report or a recent project shows seriousness. A third-party introduction can help clarify who you are, but it is not required to make first contact.

Dropping by unannounced is rare. People expect you to request time slots early, share a clear agenda and stick to the agreed window. Allow at least two weeks to secure an appointment, and confirm it in writing. Rescheduling multiple times damages credibility. Summer brings extended holidays, particularly in June, July and August, and the working day can end as early as mid-afternoon, so plan around those periods and check availability well in advance.

Regional and linguistic circles shape access in certain industries. Swedish-speaking Finn networks remain strong in shipping, coastal manufacturing and some media sectors. Lapland tourism and eastern forest industries have their own local circuits. Knowing which Finland you are stepping into helps you identify the right entry point.
