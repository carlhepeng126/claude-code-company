# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

The dining culture across Bonaire, Sint Eustatius and Saba reflects the islands' Dutch Caribbean identity, blending European formality with Caribbean ease. Business meals tend to be relaxed affairs, often held at beachside restaurants or informal eateries rather than corporate venues. The emphasis is on building personal rapport rather than advancing negotiations over the table, so let conversation unfold naturally before turning to work matters. Rushing into business talk can feel transactional and may undermine the relationship you're trying to establish.

Menus typically feature fresh seafood, Dutch-influenced dishes, and Caribbean staples. On Bonaire, you might encounter locally caught fish served alongside plantains or funchi, a polenta-like side. Saba's small restaurant scene leans more European, while Sint Eustatius offers a mix of Creole and continental options. If hosting, ask about dietary preferences beforehand, as vegetarian or specialized menus may be limited outside larger hotels.

Dress remains smart-casual unless otherwise indicated. The islands' small size and close-knit communities mean formality is rarely expected, but professionalism still matters. Arrive on time, though slight flexibility is common. Paying attention to who picks up the bill is useful, as the person who extends the invitation usually expects to cover it. Offering to split may be politely declined, so acknowledge the gesture with genuine thanks rather than insisting.

If alcohol is served, it's generally acceptable in moderation, but read the room. The tone remains cordial and unhurried, reflecting broader island rhythms where efficiency doesn't mean haste.
