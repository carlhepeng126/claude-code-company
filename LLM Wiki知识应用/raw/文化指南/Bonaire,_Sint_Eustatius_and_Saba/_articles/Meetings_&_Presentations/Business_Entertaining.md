# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining on the islands happens, but it rarely follows the formal dinner-and-deal rhythm found in more corporate environments. Social time with colleagues or clients tends to feel informal and unhurried, often taking place at a beach bar, local restaurant, or over casual drinks after work. The setting may be relaxed, but the purpose is clear: people are assessing whether the relationship feels genuine and whether they enjoy working with you.

Food and drink are shared without much ceremony. Conversation usually stays broad, touching on family, the island, or experiences outside work. Business may come up, but rarely as the focus, and almost never in a way that feels structured or conclusive. Bringing it up too directly can make the gathering feel transactional. The value of the occasion lies more in being present and easy to talk with than in advancing a specific outcome.

Alcohol is widely available and consumed socially, though the tone varies. In some settings it flows freely, in others it may not appear at all. Follow the group's lead rather than assuming either way. What matters more than what is ordered is how you join in, whether that means raising a glass or simply enjoying the moment.

If someone extends an invitation, reciprocating at some point is appreciated. It does not need to be elaborate. A return meal or drink, hosted with the same ease, shows you value the relationship beyond the immediate project.
