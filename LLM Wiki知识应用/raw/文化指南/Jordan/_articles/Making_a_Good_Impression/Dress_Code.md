# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

Business dress in Jordan reflects *iḥtirām*, the culturally central idea of showing respect through comportment and presentation. How you dress communicates your seriousness about the relationship, not just personal taste. A polished, modest baseline reassures colleagues that they are working with someone who understands public dignity and makes considered choices. Flamboyant or overly casual dress can raise questions about judgment and reliability, particularly in traditional sectors or when meeting partners for the first time.

Men typically wear suits with conservative ties, though smart shirts and trousers are common in summer. Women often choose business suits, tailored skirts or trousers, and blouses with sleeves that cover the elbows. Skirts and dresses should fall below the knee. Understated quality reads better than conspicuous expense. The goal is not to stand out but to signal that you take the setting and the people in it seriously.

Amman offers a visually plural environment where you will see everything from jeans and uncovered hair to more conservative dress, but norms tighten noticeably outside the capital, in smaller cities, family settings, and religious or community spaces. The key is situational literacy: demonstrating you can read the context and adjust accordingly. Covering shoulders and knees provides a reliable baseline, but stepping into a mosque or a more traditional setting will call for greater coverage and care.

Western professionals should not attempt to dress in traditional Jordanian attire. A modest, well-tailored version of your own professional wardrobe will always land well and allows you to move comfortably across Jordan's multiple publics.
