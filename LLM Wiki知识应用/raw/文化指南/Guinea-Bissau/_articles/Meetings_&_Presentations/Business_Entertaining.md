# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Guinea-Bissau happens less frequently than in some West African contexts, partly due to economic constraints and limited restaurant infrastructure, especially outside Bissau. When invitations do occur, they reflect a real investment in the relationship. A meal shared at someone's home or a modest local spot can mean considerably more than the setting might suggest to someone unfamiliar with the context.

Hosting typically takes place at home rather than in restaurants, particularly among older or more established business contacts. The meal itself becomes a marker of respect and an expression of hospitality rooted in the country's strong communal traditions. Expect generous portions and repeated offers of food, even in modest circumstances. Declining outright can feel abrupt. Accepting a small amount and expressing appreciation allows the host to fulfill their role without either party feeling uncomfortable.

Business is rarely discussed during the meal. The gathering exists to deepen trust and observe character in a more relaxed setting. A colleague from a culture where deal-making over dinner is standard may find this indirect, while a Bissau-Guinean counterpart might view immediate business talk at the table as transactional or even disrespectful to the occasion. The relationship being built is the transaction.

If alcohol appears, it will likely be beer or imported spirits, and only in secular or mixed-faith settings. Many abstain for religious reasons, so it's better to wait and follow what others do rather than ordering first. Reciprocating hospitality when possible, whether during the visit or later, is noticed and valued.
