# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Botswana are often low-key and flexible. Lunch is the more common choice for work discussions, typically held at a hotel restaurant or a familiar local spot. The setting tends to be relaxed rather than formal, and the meal itself is rarely elaborate. What you order is less significant than the time you take to share it. Rushing through the meal or checking your watch repeatedly suggests you see the meeting as purely transactional, which can undermine the relationship you're there to build.

Conversation usually begins with personal topics before moving to business. Asking about family, weekend plans, or local events is standard. A colleague from a culture where agendas are introduced early may feel the discussion is wandering, while someone from Botswana might read an immediate pivot to business as brusque or impersonal. The opening exchanges serve a purpose: they establish goodwill and signal that the relationship extends beyond the contract.

Hosting is taken seriously. If your Batswana counterpart has invited you, they will likely insist on paying. Offering to split the bill is polite, but pressing the point can cause awkwardness. If you want to reciprocate, suggest hosting the next meal instead. Dietary preferences should be mentioned ahead of time if possible, as beef and chicken dominate most menus and vegetarian options may be limited outside of Gaborone's larger hotels and restaurants.
