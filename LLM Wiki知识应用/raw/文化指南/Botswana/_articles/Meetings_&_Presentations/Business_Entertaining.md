# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Botswana serve a relational purpose more than a formal function. They create room to assess character, observe how someone engages beyond the agenda, and build the kind of trust that supports longer-term collaboration. Invitations are extended more often once initial contact has been positive, and acceptance is read as a sign of genuine interest in the relationship, not just the transaction.

The venue is usually a restaurant, and the host will typically choose the setting. In larger towns like Gaborone or Maun, this might be a hotel restaurant or an established spot known for consistency. The choice reflects care, and guests are expected to engage with warmth rather than treat the occasion as background to a deal. Conversation tends to be broad and unhurried. Topics may include family, travel, sport, or observations about the region, and business talk is rarely introduced early. When it does come up, it's usually the host who steers toward it, and even then the tone stays informal.

Alcohol may or may not be present. Some Batswana drink socially, others abstain, and assumptions based on setting or seniority are unreliable. It's better to wait and follow the host's lead than to order or offer without reading the table first. Reciprocating the invitation, either during the visit or on a return trip, is both expected and appreciated.
