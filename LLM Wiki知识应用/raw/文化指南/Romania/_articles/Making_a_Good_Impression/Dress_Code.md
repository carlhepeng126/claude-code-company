# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

Appearance in Romania reflects how seriously you take the encounter. After decades of scarcity and uniformity under communism, dressing well became a way to reclaim dignity and signal aspiration. That history still shapes expectations today. Showing up polished and put-together tells your Romanian colleagues that the meeting matters to you. It's less about status and more about self-respect and reciprocal regard.

The tone to aim for is balanced and understated. Flash can backfire. Conspicuous branding or overly styled outfits may read as trying too hard or, worse, insincere. Romanian professionals tend to value neatness, quality, and coherence over loudness. A well-fitted suit, clean shoes, and attention to detail will open more doors than expensive labels worn carelessly. In practical terms, this means checking your collar, your cuffs, and your shoes before a first meeting. Those details register.

Urban and rural settings differ noticeably. Bucharest and Cluj lean contemporary and European in style. Smaller towns and rural areas often favour more conservative or classic dress, especially during religious seasons. Romania remains predominantly Orthodox by self-identification, and modesty norms can heighten around religious occasions or in formal institutional settings. If you're meeting counterparts outside the capital or attending a ceremony, err toward formality. Watch what senior colleagues wear and adjust quickly.

Sector also shapes expectations. In government offices, law firms, and traditional industries, dark suits remain standard. In tech, startups, or creative fields, business casual is more common, but even there, people dress deliberately. The effort still shows.
