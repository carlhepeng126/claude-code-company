# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Romania often develop slowly, with warmth and trust shaped around the table rather than through the agenda. Relationship matters here, and the meal serves as more than a venue. Expect conversation to range widely before settling into business, and avoid pressing for decisions early. Your Romanian colleague may be reading how you respond to the moment, not just to the proposal.

Orthodox fasting periods shape the food calendar, even for people who don't actively observe them. Multiple fasting seasons throughout the year mean colleagues may prefer fish or vegetarian options at certain times without explaining why. If you're organizing a meal, quietly offer plant-based dishes alongside meat. This saves anyone from having to justify their choice and shows you've thought beyond the menu. The flip side appears around Easter and Christmas, when feast tables become emotionally thick occasions tied to family, ritual, and abundance. Availability during these seasons will reflect that pull, and acknowledging it reads as awareness rather than intrusion.

Toasts often function as small ceremonies of goodwill, especially in traditional or rural settings. Raising a glass can signal inclusion and shared warmth, not a drinking challenge. If you don't drink alcohol, raise a water glass or take a symbolic sip. The gesture itself does the relational work. As the evening continues, men may be offered *țuică* (a clear fruit brandy) or *pălincă* (even stronger), and sessions can stretch late. Understand what you're comfortable with before accepting multiple rounds.
