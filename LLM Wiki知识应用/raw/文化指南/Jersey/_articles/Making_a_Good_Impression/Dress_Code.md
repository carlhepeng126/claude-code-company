# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

Jersey's professional dress code reflects the formality of its finance-led economy and the influence of British professional culture. In banking, legal services, and traditional finance roles, men wear dark suits with conservative ties, and women choose tailored suits, dresses, or smart blouses with skirts or trousers. The standard is polished and restrained. Anything too bold or casual in these sectors can suggest you're unfamiliar with the seriousness of the work or the expectations of the client base.

Other sectors allow more flexibility. In technology, retail, or creative roles, smart casual is more common, particularly among younger professionals. Even here, though, presentation is noticed. People dress with intention, and appearing too informal in a client-facing setting or first meeting can create the wrong impression. A colleague from a culture where comfort or personal style takes precedence in the office may find Jersey's conservatism unexpectedly rigid, while someone from Jersey working abroad may read looser dress codes as a lack of professionalism rather than a different cultural logic.

Jersey is small and relationship-based, and appearance contributes to how quickly trust is established. Dressing appropriately for the sector and occasion shows awareness of local expectations and respect for the relationship. If you're unsure, err toward formality until the context becomes clearer. What you wear on day one will be remembered longer than in larger, more anonymous environments.
