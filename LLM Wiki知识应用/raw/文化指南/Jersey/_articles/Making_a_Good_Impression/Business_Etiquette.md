# Business Etiquette

## Making a Good Impression

Jersey's professional culture reflects its size and mixed roots. As a small island with close ties to Britain and neighbouring France, business interactions blend British reserve with Channel Island informality. People value politeness and smooth working relationships, but there's little taste for excessive formality or rigid protocol. The setting shapes the tone more than any single rulebook.

Dress codes vary by industry and occasion. Finance professionals tend toward suits, though even in that sector, business casual is becoming more common. Outside financial services, smart casual is often enough, particularly for internal meetings. If you're uncertain, it's worth asking in advance or starting slightly more formal. What you wear will be noticed, but comfort and practicality count as well.

Small gestures like punctuality, a firm handshake, and direct eye contact go a long way. Meetings often begin with light conversation, particularly if people haven't met before. This isn't stalling, it's relationship-building in a culture where professional and personal networks frequently overlap. Jersey's small population means people often know each other, or know someone in common, and that familiarity influences how business gets done. Taking time to establish rapport can make later discussions more straightforward.

Business cards are exchanged but not with ceremony. Offering one at the end of a meeting is standard practice. There's no formal ritual around how you present or receive them, but treating the exchange with basic courtesy matters.
