# Business Etiquette

## Making a Good Impression

Courtesy in Malaysia reflects more than good manners. The concept of *budi bahasa* (refined conduct and speech) treats politeness as a marker of character. Smoothing social edges, showing consideration, and avoiding situations that expose others to embarrassment are understood as ethical acts, part of maintaining harmony in shared space. When a colleague softens a refusal, shifts the timing of feedback, or uses indirect phrasing to navigate disagreement, they are often responding to the cultural weight of *malu* (shame or discomfort), which makes public contradiction or forcing someone into a corner feel inappropriate. If you press for a yes-or-no answer in a meeting, you may receive one, but it might protect dignity rather than reflect the full picture.

Gift giving follows similar logic. Small, modest gifts signal respect and care for the relationship, but anything large can read as an attempt to create obligation or worse, appear as bribery in settings where corruption sensitivity runs high. Chinese colleagues may initially decline a gift as a gesture of politeness, so a gentle second offer is normal. Religious boundaries matter in choice and timing: avoid alcohol or pork-derived items when giving to Muslim colleagues, and skip white, blue, black, or yellow wrapping, which can suggest mourning. Red or pink is safer. At Chinese New Year, giving *hong bao* (cash in a red envelope, ideally new bills in even amounts) to children or service providers is customary and widely expected.

Etiquette shifts depending on setting. Corporate Kuala Lumpur, government-linked organizations, family-run Chinese Malaysian firms, and East Malaysian community contexts each bring different expectations. Reading which norms apply in the moment is itself part of professional fluency. Building alignment through consultation (*musyawarah*) and framing requests as collective effort (*gotong-royong*) can strengthen group buy-in, especially where relational comfort needs to precede action.
