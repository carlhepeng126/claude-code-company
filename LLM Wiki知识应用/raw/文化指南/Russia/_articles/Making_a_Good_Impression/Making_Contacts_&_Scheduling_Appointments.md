# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

Initial contact in Russia benefits greatly from a credible introduction. The cultural distinction between svoi (our own) and chuzhoi (outsider) means trust develops much faster when someone already trusted vouches for you. A mutual contact or shared business partner creates a relational foundation that can take weeks or months to build from scratch. The introduction itself functions as social proof, reassuring your Russian counterpart that you are reliable and worth investing time in. If you lack this bridge, be prepared to demonstrate consistency over time through clear, purposeful communication that explains exactly who you are, why you're reaching out, and what you're proposing.

Reserve in early interactions reflects sincerity and contextual awareness, not hostility or disinterest. Russians often present themselves carefully until they understand who they're dealing with, especially with foreign contacts where wariness about being exploited has historical roots. What might read to a colleague from a culture where openness comes quickly as coolness or lack of interest is simply measured professionalism. From the Russian side, instant warmth or enthusiasm without substance can seem superficial or insincere. Keep your approach respectful and consistent, and don't mistake a guarded tone for lack of engagement. Warmth deepens through dependability, not through attempts to force familiarity too soon.

Russia spans eleven time zones, so scheduling across regions requires explicit discipline. Always label time zones clearly in meeting invitations, use auto-converting calendar tools, and confirm the final appointment time by phone or direct message. Last-minute changes are common, and meetings may run much longer than planned, so avoid tight back-to-back scheduling. If your Russian counterpart is late or reschedules without advance notice, that flexibility reflects the reality of competing priorities, not disrespect. You are still expected to arrive on time.
