# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Russia functions as a trust-building institution. The meal or evening together is where people assess sincerity, character, and what is often called "human reliability," not just shared commercial interests. Russians are hospitable, and the invitation itself communicates intent to move beyond transactional contact. Treat these occasions as relationship time. Attentiveness, curiosity, and respect for the host's effort are what deepen the connection, and this is often where trust forms more reliably than it does in formal meetings.

The tradition of zakuski, an array of starters, reflects a cultural language of generosity. The host demonstrates care and status through variety, plenty, and the rhythm of shared sampling. Abundance is a social message, signaling welcome rather than excess for its own sake. Respond with appreciation and willingness to participate at a level that feels comfortable, but recognize that declining everything can read as rejection of the gesture itself.

Toasting functions as social choreography. Even when alcohol is minimal or absent, the ritualized speech aspect often remains. Toasts express respect, solidarity, gratitude, and shared story in a structured way. Prepare a simple, sincere line of appreciation that is human and specific rather than grandiose. If you do not drink, signal it respectfully early and stay engaged in the ritual. The words and the moment are often as central as the drink.

You may be invited to culturally meaningful settings beyond restaurants, such as the banya, a traditional bathhouse that has historically served as a place for bonding and conversation across classes. Treat such invitations as signs of inclusion and respond with discretion and respect.
