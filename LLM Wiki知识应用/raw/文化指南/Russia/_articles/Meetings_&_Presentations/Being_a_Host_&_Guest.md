# Being a Host & Guest

## Meetings & Presentations

Being invited into a Russian home represents a shift from formal distance to personal connection. The home is where many Russians feel most at ease, and extending this invitation reflects real trust. Hosts typically demonstrate care through generous offerings of food, drink, and attention to comfort. This is especially true in a climate where warmth and shelter hold symbolic weight; the rituals of crossing the threshold—removing shoes, being offered slippers, being served tea—create a sense of protected, intimate space that contrasts with the more guarded exterior many Russians maintain in public or professional settings.

Small gestures of respect go a long way. Bringing flowers, sweets, or something for tea acknowledges the effort your host has made. The odd-number bouquet convention is widely observed and noticed when missed. If there are children, a small toy or sweets is a thoughtful touch. Accepting what you are offered, even if you don't finish it, shows you value the care behind it. Following the lead on domestic rituals—shoes, seating, when to start eating—signals cultural fluency quickly and quietly.

Time itself communicates sincerity. Rushing, tightly managing your schedule, or signaling that you need to leave soon can read as disinterest, even when unintentional. A colleague from a culture where social visits are brief and time-boxed may feel they are being polite by keeping things efficient; a Russian host may read the same behavior as coolness or lack of genuine engagement. Conversely, a Russian guest lingering well into the evening may seem unaware of boundaries to someone expecting a clear end time, when in fact staying reflects respect and enjoyment. If you do need to leave, frame it warmly and appreciatively rather than abruptly.

Younger professionals may host more casually and drink less than older generations, but the underlying expectation remains: showing up fully, staying present, and receiving hospitality with visible warmth are how trust deepens.
