# Motivating Others

## Managing Relationships

Many younger Croats live with a constant question: stay and build something here, or leave for Germany or Ireland where pay is higher and systems feel less frustrating? This tension sits in the background of workplace motivation. Employers who offer fair pay, respectful treatment, and visible career growth make staying feel possible. Those who rely on patriotic appeals while offering little substance simply sharpen the exit calculus.

Recognition works when it comes with something real. After years of hearing grand statements about national pride while watching corruption scandals unfold, people have learned to value tangible outcomes over ceremony. A certificate and a group photo mean less than access to training, a meaningful assignment, or the autonomy to implement an idea. Praise that leads nowhere drains trust. Recognition paired with opportunity builds it.

There is real pride in the idea of matching European standards without leaving home. Projects that visibly improve local systems or bring professional practices up to the level people associate with places like Germany tap into a strong current: the desire to prove that quality work can happen here, on local terms. Framing change as "bringing the good things from outside and making them ours" resonates far more than suggesting Croatia must simply copy what works elsewhere. The difference is whether people feel they are co-creating something better or being told they are not good enough as they are.
