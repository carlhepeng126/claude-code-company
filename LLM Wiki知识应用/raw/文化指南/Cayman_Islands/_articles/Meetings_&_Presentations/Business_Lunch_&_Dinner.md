# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in the Cayman Islands tend to be straightforward and low-key. The setting is often casual, even when the stakes are high, and the tone mirrors the wider professional culture: friendly, pragmatic, and direct. Lunch is more common than dinner for work discussions, partly because the pace of business here leaves evenings open for personal time. When dinner does happen, it's often reserved for relationship-building with established contacts rather than early-stage introductions.

Caymanians generally keep business talk light during the meal itself, especially at the start. There's an expectation that you'll ease into work topics rather than launching straight in. A colleague from a culture where meetings follow tight agendas may read this as inefficiency, while a Caymanian counterpart might view an abrupt pivot to business as lacking warmth. The meal is part of the conversation, and moving too quickly can feel transactional. Let the host set the pace, and watch for cues about when the discussion shifts.

Dress remains smart-casual in most settings, though financial services professionals may keep things slightly more formal. Venues range from upscale waterfront restaurants to quieter spots away from tourist areas. Ordering is typically individual rather than communal, and splitting the bill is common among peers, though the person who extended the invitation will often offer to cover it. If you're hosting, clarify payment discreetly with staff beforehand to avoid awkwardness at the table.
