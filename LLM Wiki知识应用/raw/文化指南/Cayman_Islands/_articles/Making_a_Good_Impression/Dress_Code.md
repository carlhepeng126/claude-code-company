# Dress Code

## Making a Good Impression

Professional dress in the Cayman Islands leans formal and polished, shaped by a workplace culture where financial services and law firms dominate. The islands host one of the world's largest offshore financial centers, and this concentration of international finance has made smart business attire the expected standard, especially in George Town. Men typically wear long-sleeved shirts with ties and formal trousers. Suits are common in client-facing roles and for senior meetings. Women tend toward tailored dresses, skirts, or trousers paired with blouses. The tropical climate is acknowledged, fabrics are lightweight, but the level of formality remains consistent.

This emphasis on polished presentation reflects both the professional expectations of the finance sector and a broader cultural preference for respectability and order. Appearance is read as a measure of professionalism and seriousness. Showing up underdressed to a meeting can suggest a lack of preparation or regard for the occasion, which may affect how your input is received, particularly in formal settings like legal offices or fund administration firms.

Dress expectations ease somewhat outside the financial core. In tourism, hospitality, and retail, business casual is more accepted, though still neat and intentional. Tech startups and creative industries, while smaller in number, allow for more relaxed styling, but even here, carefulness in presentation is valued over casualness. Friday dress codes may relax slightly in some offices, but this varies by firm and is never assumed.

For social or evening business occasions, the formality often ticks up. Dinner with clients or colleagues at an upscale restaurant typically calls for a jacket or cocktail dress. Matching the effort your hosts make shows attentiveness and respect for the relationship being built.
