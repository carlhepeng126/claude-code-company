# Business Etiquette

## Making a Good Impression

Business etiquette in the Cayman Islands reflects the country's position as a global financial center with deep Caribbean roots. Professionalism is taken seriously, but the tone is relaxed and approachable. Formality exists, but it rarely feels stiff. Most business interactions begin on a first-name basis, even at senior levels, and that informality is genuine rather than a performance of friendliness. Colleagues from cultures where titles and surnames establish respect may read this as a lack of hierarchy, but Caymanians often view overly formal address as creating unnecessary distance in what is, by population, a small and interconnected professional community.

Dress codes lean conservative, especially in banking, legal, and government sectors. Business attire is expected, though the climate means lightweight fabrics and short-sleeved shirts are common. In more casual industries or outdoor settings, smart casual is acceptable, but visitors should err toward formality until they understand the office norm. Punctuality is respected, and lateness without notice can come across as careless. That said, meetings may begin with a few minutes of light conversation before turning to business. Rushing straight into an agenda can feel transactional.

Communication is polite and indirect when it comes to disagreement or refusal. Caymanians rarely say no outright in professional settings. Instead, a vague response, a delay, or a shift in topic may signal reluctance. This is rooted in a preference for harmony and avoiding confrontation, particularly in a small jurisdiction where professional and personal networks often overlap. Colleagues from cultures where direct rejection is expected may find this ambiguous, while Caymanians may see bluntness as unnecessarily abrasive. Listening for what is not said, and giving space for people to express concerns privately, often leads to clearer outcomes than pressing for an immediate answer in a group.
