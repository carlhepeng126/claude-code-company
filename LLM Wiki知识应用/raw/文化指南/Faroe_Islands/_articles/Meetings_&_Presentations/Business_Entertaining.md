# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in the Faroe Islands tends to be understated and informal. Meals may happen during a visit or project, but they are not usually a core part of the relationship-building process in the way they might be in more hierarchical or formal business cultures. When invitations do occur, they often reflect genuine personal interest rather than protocol. A Faroese colleague who suggests dinner is likely signaling that they see the relationship as more than transactional.

The setting is typically casual. A local restaurant, often serving Faroese fish or lamb, is more common than fine dining. Alcohol is widely consumed socially, especially beer or spirits, and refusing a drink is generally fine without explanation. Conversation during meals is relaxed and often avoids work topics entirely unless the host brings them up. Pushing business discussion over dinner can come across as overly formal or impatient. The meal itself is the point, a space to get to know each other without agenda.

Reciprocating an invitation is appreciated but not expected in the short term. A colleague from a culture where entertaining follows a stricter exchange protocol may feel uncertain about when or how to respond. In return, a Faroese host may find elaborate gestures of thanks or immediate counter-invitations unnecessarily formal. A simple expression of thanks and a relaxed willingness to meet again is usually enough.
