# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

In Santiago, access often depends on which school or university you attended. A cold approach from someone with no reference point lands very differently from a contact who comes via a known colegio or university network. This reflects the much-debated *pituto* system, where opportunities flow through personal connections. People still ask: who brought you, who trusts you? A warm introduction from a respected professional matters more than your company's brand. When reaching out, ask local colleagues, clients, or diaspora Chileans for introductions through trusted networks rather than relying on pure cold outreach.

Chileans often start polite, measured, and a bit distant. They're testing whether you're serious, respectful, and not *vende humo* (selling smoke, all talk and no substance). The warmth usually comes in later meetings, once they've seen you show up, follow through, and not over-claim. In first meetings, be professional and calm. Don't mistake low initial enthusiasm for rejection. Let trust grow through consistency.

Emails and calendar invites exist, but WhatsApp is the real coordination tool. Last-minute confirmations or changes are common, especially with traffic or protests. Arrange appointments two or three weeks in advance, then reconfirm on WhatsApp the day before or even the morning of the meeting. Use the platform for quick adjustments, and build in flexibility. The reserve you encounter early on often shifts once Chileans have had time to observe how you work.
