# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

A business lunch in Chile follows a familiar urban rhythm, often built around the menu ejecutivo — a set lunch that includes a starter, main course, and sometimes dessert and coffee. People expect a proper meal, not a sandwich at the desk, but they also need to return to work. The structure offers time to connect, but within understood limits.

The order of conversation is deliberate. Family, football, maybe the last earthquake come first. Business usually waits until after the main course. Raising a contract before anyone has touched their plate reads as brusque. This opening phase builds rapport and shows human interest, and Chileans expect it. For colleagues from cultures where business leads the conversation from the start, this may feel like lost time. From a Chilean perspective, jumping straight into terms can suggest the relationship itself matters less than the transaction.

Regional styles shape the meal beyond Santiago. In Antofagasta or Calama, seafood and mining are natural topics. In the south, heavier food and weather dominate. In Valparaíso, expect port life and politics. Each setting has its own tempo and humor around the table. Let local partners choose the venue when possible. It shows openness to their Chile, rather than staying inside expat zones.

Conversation is sociable but measured. Even when the mood is relaxed, deeply personal topics — trauma, raw politics, money troubles — often stay with closer friends. Oversharing quickly can make people pull back. Share something relatable: children, studies, moving cities. Read the room as you go, and let trust deepen at the table's own pace.
