# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Djibouti tends to be informal and centred on relationship building. Meals offer a chance to deepen trust away from the office, and invitations usually follow once initial meetings have gone well. Lunch or dinner may take place in a restaurant, often in the capital, where hosts may choose Somali, Yemeni, or French-influenced cuisine depending on familiarity and the preferences of their guests. The setting reflects Djibouti's role as a crossroads, drawing on multiple culinary traditions shaped by trade, migration, and colonial history.

Conversation during meals is typically wide-ranging and relaxed. Business may be touched on briefly or not at all, and it is usually the host who signals whether and when to raise it. Leading with work talk can feel abrupt in a context where the meal itself affirms the relationship. A colleague from a culture where deals are advanced over dinner may read this as evasive, while a Djiboutian counterpart may view a direct pivot to business as transactional or dismissive of the time being shared.

Alcohol is generally not part of business entertaining. Most Djiboutians are Muslim, and abstaining is the norm in professional settings. Offering or ordering alcohol without clear indication from the host risks causing discomfort. Following the host's lead on what is served and discussed shows respect for both the person and the occasion. Reciprocating an invitation, whether during the same visit or later, is expected and appreciated.
