# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

Djibouti's small size and interconnected society mean that making business contacts depends on who you know and who vouches for you. In a country where many families have links across clans, regions, and neighboring countries, a personal introduction from a trusted intermediary opens doors that cold outreach rarely can. When someone introduces you, they lend you their credibility, and that endorsement shapes how seriously you'll be taken. If you're working with an established local partner or through a chamber of commerce, ask them to make the introduction rather than reaching out alone.

Face-to-face contact builds trust faster than emails or phone calls. Even if initial communication happens remotely, expect that the relationship will be tested in person before any real commitment is made. Conversations often begin with questions about your background, your connections, or recent events in the country. This isn't small talk for its own sake. It establishes who you are and whether the relationship feels reliable.

Scheduling appointments works best when done a week or two in advance, but follow up the day before to confirm. Meetings may start late or be interrupted by phone calls, drop-in visitors, or shifting priorities. Back-to-back bookings leave no room for these realities. Leave space in your schedule, and don't interpret delays as disinterest. Flexibility in timing shows you understand how business actually gets done.
