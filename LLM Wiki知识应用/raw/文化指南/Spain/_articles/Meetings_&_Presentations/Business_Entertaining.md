# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Meals are central to building professional relationships in Spain, and accepting invitations is often where trust begins. Business entertaining typically happens at lunch or in the evening, and it's designed to get to know people first. Serious business discussion, if it comes at all, usually waits until the later stages of the meal. The gathering itself is the work. Showing up and engaging with interest demonstrates that the relationship matters to you.

Timing and pace are different from many task-focused cultures. Lunch, the main meal of the day, is eaten around 2pm and can stretch into the afternoon. Evening meals rarely start before 9 or 10pm and may continue late, especially if the evening includes drinks beforehand or a move to a bar afterwards. A colleague accustomed to early dinner or a quick business meal might find the extended rhythm hard to justify, while a Spanish counterpart may see arriving on time for a 7pm dinner as culturally misaligned or even awkward. The length of the meal reflects the importance of the conversation, not inefficiency.

Spain's regional food cultures are distinct and widely valued. Galicia, the Basque Country, Catalonia, and Andalusia each have their own culinary traditions, and hosts take pride in sharing them. Asking about local dishes or expressing genuine interest helps build rapport. Wine is common but never obligatory, and offering choice is courteous.

Conversation at meals is convivial, wide-ranging, and often informal. Heavy negotiation during the meal itself is rare. The meal is where people assess how you engage, not where they expect you to close a deal.
