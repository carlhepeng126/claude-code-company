# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Estonia are low-key and practical. Entertaining happens, but it's rarely seen as a necessary step to close a deal or build trust. Estonians don't rely on socializing to assess whether someone is reliable. They prefer to judge that through work itself. When meals do occur, they're usually scheduled after the working relationship has begun, and they're treated more as a courtesy than a threshold.

Restaurants in Tallinn or Tartu are the standard setting, typically chosen for convenience rather than atmosphere. The meal is brief, often lunch, and the tone stays professional. Long dinners are uncommon unless the relationship is well established or the visit involves senior figures from abroad. Estonians don't use meals to perform warmth or to soften negotiation. If business needs to be discussed, it will be, but just as directly as it would be in the office.

Alcohol is culturally present but not emphasized. Wine or beer may be offered at dinner, particularly on a Friday or if the occasion is more relaxed, but no one will press it. Declining is entirely normal and carries no social cost. Toasts are minimal and matter-of-fact. A colleague from a culture where toasting affirms group unity may find Estonian meals understated. From the Estonian side, elaborate ritual around drinking can feel performative or time-consuming when the relationship doesn't yet call for it.

Reciprocating is polite but not obligatory. If you've been invited, a return gesture is appreciated, though it doesn't need to be elaborate. A simple lunch works.
