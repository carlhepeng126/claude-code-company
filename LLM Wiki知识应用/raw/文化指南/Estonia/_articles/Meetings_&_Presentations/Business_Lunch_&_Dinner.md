# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Estonia are quieter and more task-focused than in many relationship-driven cultures. Estonians tend to view lunch or dinner as a working session with food, not primarily as a social occasion. Conversation often stays close to the business agenda, and there may be long pauses between topics. This reflects a broader Estonian preference for economy in communication and a discomfort with what might be seen as forced or unnecessary talk. Small talk for its own sake can feel insincere, so don't interpret silence as tension or disinterest.

Meals are typically short and efficient. Lunch meetings rarely last longer than an hour, and dinners, while more relaxed, still maintain a degree of formality and restraint. Estonians often eat quickly and don't linger over multiple courses. If you're used to multi-hour dinners where relationship-building unfolds slowly over wine and storytelling, expect a more contained rhythm here. Proposing a toast is acceptable, but keep it brief and genuine.

Alcohol may be offered, especially at dinner, and vodka or local craft beer are common choices. Estonians drink moderately in business settings and rarely to the point of visible intoxication. If you decline, a simple "no, thank you" is enough. Dietary preferences or restrictions should be mentioned in advance, as menus may not offer wide variety, particularly outside Tallinn.

Don't expect elaborate hosting gestures or emotional warmth at the table. Hospitality in Estonia is practical rather than demonstrative. Your counterpart may focus on ensuring logistics run smoothly rather than engaging in elaborate conversation. This reflects respect, not coldness.
