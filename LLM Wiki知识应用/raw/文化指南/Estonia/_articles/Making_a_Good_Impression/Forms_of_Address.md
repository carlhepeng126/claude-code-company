# Forms of Address

## Making a Good Impression

Estonians typically use first names in most workplace settings, including with senior colleagues and managers. This informality reflects the country's relatively flat social hierarchies and a cultural preference for directness over ceremony. The shift is also generational: younger Estonians and those working in international or tech-focused companies almost always use first names from the outset. Older professionals, particularly in more traditional sectors such as banking, education, or government, may expect surnames and titles initially, especially when meeting for the first time or in formal correspondence.

When in doubt, follow the lead of your Estonian counterpart. If they introduce themselves by first name only, reciprocate. If they use a title and surname, do the same until invited to switch. Estonians will not usually correct you if you get it wrong, but they will notice. A colleague from a culture where hierarchy is marked through language may find the rapid move to first names uncomfortable or presume a level of familiarity that has not yet been earned. Estonians, in turn, may read the continued use of titles as stiff or distancing once a working relationship is underway.

In written communication, especially emails to people you have not met, it is safer to open with "Mr" or "Ms" and the surname. Once a reply comes back signed with a first name, you can follow suit. The formality rarely lasts beyond the first exchange.
