# Managing Suppliers & Customers

## Managing Relationships

Reliability in Vietnam becomes visible through repeated delivery and social proof, not just contractual terms. Partners often evaluate you through networks, shared reference points, and steadily demonstrated competence, shaped by decades of reform-era transformation that rewarded calm adaptation over big promises. Start with small, clearly scoped commitments and build scope gradually as trust forms through consistent follow-through. Offer early notice when constraints shift, along with revised timelines and practical alternatives, rather than waiting for problems to surface.

Market realities differ sharply across Hà Nội, TP.HCM, and provincial centers, each with its own relational rhythms, logistics, and local authority presence. How you maintain the relationship often depends on where you are operating. Treat intermediaries and local gatekeepers with visible respect; they often carry real informational and relational value in their ecosystems, and working with them professionally strengthens your access and credibility.

Digital coordination tools, especially messaging platforms, frequently handle day-to-day updates and relational maintenance, while formal systems carry the record. Showing up in the channels counterparts actually use for coordination builds trust in your responsiveness and accessibility.

Relationship-building takes many forms. In some circles, shared meals or nhậu-style gatherings hold real weight; in others, they are secondary or avoided. Never assume one social style fits all. Offer multiple routes to continuity that maintain warmth without imposing a single format. When mistakes happen, handle them privately with calm accountability and practical repair, preserving dignity while addressing the issue directly.
