# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

In Vietnam, sharing a meal builds connection through shared presence, and the table functions as a space where respect codes and collective rhythm matter as much as the food itself. Communal ordering and shared dishes reinforce group cohesion, and the meal is designed to create ease rather than transactional efficiency. Business discussion may come up, but pushing contentious topics early can disrupt the relational function of the gathering. Trust builds through comfort first, and the conversation follows when the table feels right.

Seating reflects seniority. Who sits where, who begins eating, and who speaks first can all express respect, especially when older or senior colleagues are present. These signals are rarely announced but often noticed. Following the host's lead on ordering, pacing, and when to start eating helps maintain that comfort. Taking control of the table without invitation can come across as domineering, even if the intention is simply to help things move along.

Vietnamese food varies by region, and people often take affectionate pride in northern, central, or southern tastes. Food talk offers a safe and genuine way to connect without requiring personal disclosure. Toasting and drinking customs appear in some settings, but preferences differ. Flexible participation allows people to join in or step back without discomfort. Matching the social energy while leaving room for choice keeps the atmosphere inclusive.

If business does arise, keep it light and exploratory. The meal opens doors rather than forcing decisions. Staying calm, non-dominating, and attentive to hierarchy cues builds the kind of rapport that makes later collaboration smoother.
