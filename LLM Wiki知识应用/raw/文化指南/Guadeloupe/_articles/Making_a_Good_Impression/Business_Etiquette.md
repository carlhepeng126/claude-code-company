# Business Etiquette

## Making a Good Impression

Business etiquette in Guadeloupe reflects the island's dual French and Caribbean inheritance. Formality stems from French administrative culture, while warmth and relationship-building draw from Creole tradition. The result is a professional environment where courtesy and connection matter equally, and where getting the balance right requires attention to both structure and tone.

Greetings are formal but friendly. A handshake is standard in business settings, often accompanied by "Bonjour" and the appropriate title: Monsieur, Madame, or Mademoiselle. First names come later, once rapport has developed. Rushing into casual familiarity too soon can feel presumptuous. Colleagues from cultures where informality signals approachability may find this reserve stiff, while Guadeloupeans working with teams that skip formalities altogether may interpret it as a lack of respect or professionalism.

Punctuality expectations vary by setting. Formal meetings, especially those involving French companies or government agencies, run on strict time. Social or informal professional gatherings allow more flexibility. Arriving slightly late to a relaxed event is understood, but being late to a structured meeting without notice is read as carelessness. This split reflects the island's dual rhythm: metropolitan discipline in official contexts, and a more Caribbean sense of time in relational ones.

Dress codes lean conservative in professional environments. Men wear suits or tailored shirts; women opt for dresses, skirts, or smart trousers. Presenting yourself well is a sign you take the interaction seriously. Casual dress is reserved for after-hours or less formal settings, and dressing too informally for a first meeting can undermine credibility before a word is spoken.
