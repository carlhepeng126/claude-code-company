# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Guadeloupe blend French dining traditions with Caribbean rhythm and local warmth. Expect a leisurely pace and multiple courses, often featuring accras (salt cod fritters), colombo (a curry-influenced stew), or fresh seafood. Meals are a chance to deepen relationships, not just discuss work. Launching straight into business talk before people have settled in can feel abrupt and overly functional. Allow time for conversation to unfold naturally. Colleagues from cultures where meals are shorter and more task-focused may read this as inefficient, while Guadeloupeans may find a rushed meal transactional and at odds with building genuine trust.

Wine or rum, especially local *rhum agricole* (rum made directly from fresh sugarcane juice), often accompanies the meal. Sharing a drink is a gesture of inclusion, and declining without explanation may create distance. If you prefer not to drink alcohol, a brief reason smooths the moment without offense.

Meals often last two hours or longer, particularly at lunch. This isn't indulgence but part of how business culture works. Trying to wrap up early or checking the time visibly can suggest the relationship doesn't matter. Let your host guide the timing. The conversation may circle back to work naturally, but don't force it. What feels like a long detour is often where rapport gets built and decisions take shape later on.
