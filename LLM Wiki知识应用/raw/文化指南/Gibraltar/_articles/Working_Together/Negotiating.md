# Negotiating

## Working Together

Negotiations in Gibraltar move quickly once the groundwork is clear. People appreciate directness and expect proposals to be realistic from the start. There is little appetite for posturing or circling around what is actually on the table. This reflects a small, interconnected professional community where reputations travel fast and time is rarely wasted on theatrics. If you open with an inflated figure or hold back your real terms, you may be seen as playing games rather than negotiating seriously.

That said, the pace can slow if the relationship is new or if your counterpart is unsure about your credibility. Gibraltar's business culture is relationship-aware, shaped by its size and history of cross-border trade. Trust is earned through consistency, not assumed from a first meeting. If you are from a context where decisions are made quickly once terms are agreed, the pause may feel unnecessary. From a Gibraltar perspective, a colleague who pushes for closure before rapport is established may come across as transactional or unreliable.

Discussions tend to be pragmatic and solution-focused. People will flag concerns directly rather than soften them with diplomatic language. This can feel blunt to those from cultures where disagreement is expressed more indirectly, but it is intended to clarify, not to confront. Respond with the same clarity. If you hedge or avoid a direct answer, you risk being seen as unclear or unprepared, which can stall progress more than any substantive disagreement would.
