# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Gibraltar tend to be relaxed and informal, shaped by the small size of the territory and the way personal and professional relationships overlap. In a place where most people know each other, dining is less about formal etiquette and more about continuing the conversation in a setting where familiarity can deepen. The line between socializing and doing business is blurred, and meals reflect that fluidity.

Lunch is the more common choice for business dining, often held at local pubs, tapas bars, or restaurants near the office. The meal is typically brief, especially midweek, as most people need to return to work. Topics may move between business and personal life without ceremony. Dinner is reserved for more established relationships or for visiting colleagues, and while still relaxed in tone, it tends to last longer and allows for broader conversation.

Gibraltar's culinary scene reflects its cultural mix. British pub food sits alongside Spanish tapas, and it's common to find Mediterranean flavours paired with British-style service. Don't be surprised if the table orders a few plates to share in the Spanish style, or if someone suggests fish and chips. The variety reflects Gibraltar's dual influences, and familiarity with both British and Spanish dining customs is generally assumed.

Alcohol is commonly offered and accepted, though moderation is the norm. If you decline, it's unlikely to draw comment. What does register is the effort to participate in the social side of the meal. Asking questions, showing interest in Gibraltar's particularities, and staying engaged in conversation all contribute more to the relationship than any choice on the menu.
