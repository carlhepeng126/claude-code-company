# Business Etiquette

## Making a Good Impression

Gibraltar's business etiquette reflects its British heritage layered with distinctly Mediterranean warmth. The result is a professional environment that values courtesy and structure but remains relaxed in tone. Meetings may begin with a few minutes of friendly conversation before turning to business, and small talk about local events or mutual acquaintances helps establish rapport. This blend of formality and familiarity reflects Gibraltar's size and connectedness. In a territory of just over 30,000 people, professional and personal worlds overlap frequently, so relationship quality affects how smoothly things move forward.

Dress codes tend toward the conservative, especially in finance and legal sectors. A suit or smart business attire signals respect for the setting, though styles may relax slightly in summer heat or in smaller, less formal firms. Punctuality is expected and noticed. Arriving late without explanation can be read as dismissive, while giving advance notice of a delay is appreciated and reciprocated.

Communication is generally direct but delivered politely. Gibraltarians tend to get to the point without unnecessary preamble, a style shaped by British influence, but the delivery remains courteous rather than blunt. Colleagues from cultures where indirectness is the norm may find this refreshingly clear, while Gibraltarians working with more reserved counterparts may interpret hesitation or vagueness as a lack of transparency. Understanding that directness here is not meant to challenge or offend helps smooth cross-cultural exchanges.

Business cards are exchanged casually, usually at the start or end of a meeting. There is no particular ceremony attached to the gesture, though offering one signals openness to future contact. English is standard, and a simple handshake accompanies the exchange.
