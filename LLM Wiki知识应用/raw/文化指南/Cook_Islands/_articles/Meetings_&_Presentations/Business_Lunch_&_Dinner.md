# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in the Cook Islands tend to be informal and relationship-focused rather than strictly transactional. Sharing food reflects the broader cultural value of generosity and connection, rooted in communal island life where reciprocity and personal ties have long shaped how people interact. A meal is an opportunity to build trust and show genuine interest in the person, not just the project. Conversations may begin with family, the weather, or island news before touching on work. Rushing straight into business talk or treating the meal as a quick checkpoint can feel impersonal and may slow the development of the relationship you are hoping to strengthen.

Venues are usually relaxed. Meals might take place at a beachside café, a local restaurant, or even at someone's home if the relationship has developed. The setting reinforces the informal tone. Expect fresh fish, taro, coconut-based dishes, and tropical fruit. Portions are often generous, and declining food outright can seem abrupt. If you cannot eat something, a brief explanation is usually enough.

Timing is flexible. Meals may start a little later than scheduled, and the pace is unhurried. Leaving immediately after eating can seem dismissive. Staying for a while after the meal ends, even if just for conversation, shows respect for the time someone has made for you. Business outcomes are more likely to follow when the relationship feels balanced and the interaction feels genuinely two-sided, not simply efficient.
