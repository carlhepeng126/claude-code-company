# Making Contacts & Scheduling Appointments

## Making a Good Impression

Building business relationships in Cabo Verde begins with personal connection, a reflection of the islands' small-scale society where reputations travel quickly and trust is built through repeated, reliable interaction. Cold emails or formal requests to people you've never met can land flat. An introduction from someone known to both parties opens doors far more effectively. This might be a colleague arranging an informal meeting over coffee or mentioning your name to a contact before you reach out directly. The introduction signals that you're already part of a wider network of trust.

Regional and generational differences shape how contacts are made. In the capital, Praia, and in sectors with strong international links such as tourism or development, younger professionals often respond well to direct outreach and move to business discussion more quickly. On smaller islands or in family-run enterprises, introductions still tend to come through trusted intermediaries, and the first conversation may focus more on background and shared connections than on the work itself.

When scheduling appointments, book at least a week ahead and confirm the day before. Leave time between meetings. Discussions can stretch beyond their planned length, or other people may join partway through. A colleague from a culture where schedules are tightly sequenced may read this as disorganization. From a Cabo Verdean perspective, the flexibility reflects the value placed on being present and responsive to whoever is in front of you, rather than treating time as a series of fixed blocks.

Appointments are generally respected once set, but unforeseen personal or family matters can shift plans. Showing understanding rather than frustration when this happens strengthens the relationship and signals that you recognize the broader context in which business takes place.
