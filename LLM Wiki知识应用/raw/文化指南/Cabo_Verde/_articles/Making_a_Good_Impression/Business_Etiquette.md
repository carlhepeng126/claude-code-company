# Business Etiquette

## Making a Good Impression

Cabo Verdean business etiquette draws on the archipelago's blend of African warmth and Portuguese formality, with a strong undercurrent of relationship-first professionalism. Greetings tend to be cordial and unhurried. A handshake is standard, often accompanied by direct eye contact and a genuine exchange of pleasantries. Rushing this opening can feel transactional. Taking a few moments to ask after someone's well-being or acknowledge a shared contact helps set the tone for what follows. The relational foundation comes before the agenda.

Dress leans toward the formal, especially in Praia and Mindelo, where business culture follows more cosmopolitan norms. Men typically wear suits or tailored shirts and trousers; women opt for dresses, skirts, or smart separates. The climate is warm year-round, but professional presentation still holds weight. In smaller towns or more informal sectors, expectations may soften slightly, but erring on the side of formality shows respect and seriousness of intent.

Communication in meetings tends to be courteous and measured. Cabo Verdeans generally avoid bluntness or open confrontation, preferring to soften disagreement or steer around conflict to preserve rapport. A colleague from a more direct culture might read this as evasiveness, while a Cabo Verdean counterpart might perceive rapid contradiction as dismissive or disrespectful. Listening carefully to tone and context will often reveal hesitations that aren't stated outright.

Punctuality expectations vary. International meetings or formal settings call for timeliness, but slight delays are not uncommon and rarely treated as serious breaches. Flexibility around scheduling reflects broader cultural rhythms where relationships and unforeseen circumstances can take precedence over rigid timetables.
