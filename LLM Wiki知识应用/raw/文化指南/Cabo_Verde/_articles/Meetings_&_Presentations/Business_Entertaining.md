# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Cabo Verde tend to be informal and unhurried, serving as much to build personal connection as to discuss work. Lunch or dinner invitations often come once initial rapport has been established, and the setting is usually relaxed. The archipelago's size and blend of African and Portuguese cultural influences mean that entertaining styles can vary slightly from island to island, though everywhere the emphasis leans toward warmth rather than formality.

Conversation over meals is usually wide-ranging and may touch lightly on business, but the agenda is rarely fixed. Hosts tend to guide the flow, and raising work topics too directly or early can come across as transactional. The meal itself is treated as part of the relationship-building process, a chance to see how people interact outside structured meetings. For colleagues from cultures where dinners include detailed project discussion or implicit expectations around outcomes, this open-ended approach can feel ambiguous. From a Cabo Verdean perspective, a meal spent talking only about contracts or timelines can seem narrow or overly functional.

If alcohol is served, it is usually moderate and unobtrusive. Not everyone drinks, and assumptions should not be made. Following the host's lead is the safest approach. Reciprocating an invitation, whether during the same visit or later, is seen as courteous and helps cement the relationship over time.
