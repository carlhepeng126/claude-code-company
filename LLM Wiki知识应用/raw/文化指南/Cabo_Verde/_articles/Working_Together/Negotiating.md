# Negotiating

## Working Together

Negotiations in Cabo Verde move at a relational pace. Deals are built through repeated contact and personal familiarity, which means early meetings may feel more exploratory than transactional. The focus is often on getting to know each other, understanding intentions, and building a foundation of mutual respect before terms are discussed in detail. Rushing to lock in agreement or presenting a rigid proposal at the outset can close off the very flexibility that makes the process work. A counterpart from a culture where speed and efficiency are prized may read this slower rhythm as avoidance or hesitation, while a Cabo Verdean negotiator may view a fast-moving, contract-first approach as impersonal or even untrustworthy.

The process rarely follows a straight line. Points that seemed settled may resurface later, or the conversation may shift to something unrelated before returning to the original topic. This circularity allows both sides to test flexibility, revisit assumptions, and ensure nothing has been overlooked. It also reflects the way decisions are often made in Cabo Verde: through consultation, informal soundings, and input from people who may not be in the room. Allow time for this back-and-forth rather than treating it as a delay.

Bargaining is expected, and counteroffers are a sign of engagement rather than rejection. Presenting your best terms immediately leaves no room to adjust and can make the negotiation feel one-sided. Cabo Verdean negotiators tend to leave space for movement, and they expect the same in return.
