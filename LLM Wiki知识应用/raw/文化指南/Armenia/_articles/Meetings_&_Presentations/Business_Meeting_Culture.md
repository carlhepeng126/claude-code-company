# Business Meeting Culture

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meetings in Armenia are scheduled with care, and timing often depends on the relationship. A first meeting with a new partner may be arranged fairly quickly, but clarity about your purpose and role ahead of time helps ensure the right people are in the room. Hierarchy shapes attendance, and decisions typically rest with senior figures, so invitations should reflect that structure. If you send a meeting request to a mid-level contact, expect them to check upward before confirming. This reflects both respect for authority and a desire to avoid wasting anyone's time.

The meeting itself usually begins with tea or coffee and a few minutes of conversation that may touch on family, shared connections, or recent events. This opening is not filler. It allows participants to assess character and intent before moving into substance. Once the agenda begins, expect a shift toward directness. Armenians tend to state what they want clearly and appreciate the same in return. Overstatement or vague promises undermine trust quickly, so consistency between what you say and what you can deliver matters more than enthusiasm.

Formality is strongest in early encounters, especially when hierarchy is visible. Silence may follow a question or proposal, and filling it too quickly can come across as discomfort or impatience. If the relationship develops, later meetings often become warmer, with humor and personal stories woven in. Decisions are rarely made on the spot, even when discussion has been thorough. The senior person may need time to consult, reflect, or confirm details internally, and pressing for closure in the room is usually counterproductive.

After the meeting, follow-up is expected. A written summary or next steps, sent promptly, reinforces seriousness. If the conversation went well, you may be invited to continue over a meal. Accepting signals that you see the relationship as more than transactional.
