# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business entertaining in Singapore is essential for relationship building, but it is carefully calibrated rather than freeform. Invitations might range from a quick coffee catch-up to dinner or even a weekend golf trip to Malaysia. Accepting shows you value the relationship and want it to move forward. Declining repeatedly, or showing up distracted, suggests the opposite.

The setting of an invitation communicates something distinct. Being taken to a rooftop bar in the CBD or a formal international restaurant is corporate hospitality. Being invited to a favourite hawker stall or coffee shop in the heartlands is different: it means you are being shown someone's real Singapore, the places they go with family or old friends. Arriving open-minded rather than squeamish, and showing genuine interest in the food and setting, will be noticed. If you reciprocate, check preferences first. Singaporeans appreciate being asked whether they prefer something casual, traditional, or more formal, rather than having a choice imposed on them.

Drinking happens, but it is never assumed. Some circles, particularly in finance or certain expat networks, drink regularly. Many colleagues abstain for religious, health, or personal reasons. Pushing alcohol on non-drinkers or treating sobriety as awkward will damage your standing. If you do drink, control your intake. Being visibly drunk in a culture known for self-regulation and social discipline does lasting reputational harm.

Keep weekday gatherings time-bounded. Most people, especially those with family or National Service fitness obligations, prefer evenings that end by 9 or 10pm rather than stretching into the early hours. Stating a clear timeframe in the invitation allows people to plan and attend without anxiety.
