# Teamwork

## Working Together

Portuguese teams cohere through repeated, low-key contact rather than upfront intensity. The term *convívio* (the gradual thickening of familiarity through shared time) describes how trust accumulates: coffee pauses, overlapping lunch breaks, small repeated encounters that build social warmth without forced disclosure. Colleagues often become part of an extended social circle, but that closeness develops slowly and stabilises over time. For a manager accustomed to jump-starting collaboration through team-building events or rapid-fire workshops, this slower rhythm can feel elusive. From the Portuguese perspective, a push for instant closeness can feel performative or shallow, skipping the groundwork that makes working relationships durable.

Public composure and private candor coexist. The Estado Novo period (1933–1974) reinforced a habit of restraint in formal settings, and many Portuguese professionals still distinguish between "public correctness" and "private comfort." In group meetings, colleagues may stay composed, deferring visibly to hierarchy and avoiding overt challenge. Trust deepens in smaller conversations where people relax into frankness. Singling someone out for praise or criticism in a team setting can disrupt the social atmosphere; feedback works better delivered privately, where dignity stays intact and the relationship remains workable.

*Desenrascanço* (the culturally admired ability to improvise solutions when plans meet reality) shapes how teams handle disruption. Rather than panic or assign blame when timelines shift or resources fall short, Portuguese colleagues often respond with calm adaptability and social practicality. For a collaborator from a culture that values strict adherence to plans, this flexibility can read as unstructured. From a Portuguese viewpoint, rigid insistence on the original timeline when circumstances have changed can seem impractical or disconnected from lived constraints. Show *desenrascanço* energy by staying solution-oriented and composed when adjustments are needed, and by recognising improvisation as a group competence rather than a workaround.
