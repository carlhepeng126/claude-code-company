# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Fiji play a central role in relationship building, particularly once initial contact has been made. Lunches and dinners offer a chance to extend the connection beyond the formal setting, and invitations are often a sign that the relationship is progressing. These gatherings are less about advancing the deal and more about understanding the person across the table. In a culture where trust is built through personal familiarity and shared time, refusing an invitation can feel like a refusal of the relationship itself.

The tone of these occasions is relaxed and unhurried. Conversation may range widely, touching on family, sport, or community life, and business topics usually emerge late or not at all. The host typically sets the pace. A guest who steers the conversation toward contracts or timelines too early risks appearing transactional or impatient. For someone from a culture where meals are used to advance specific outcomes, this can feel inefficient. From a Fijian perspective, the meal is doing exactly what it should: deepening the relationship on which future collaboration depends.

Venues are often restaurants, especially in larger centers like Suva or Nadi, and may include both local and international cuisine. Alcohol is generally available and socially acceptable in business settings, though not universal. It's sensible to follow the host's lead rather than ordering ahead of the group. Reciprocating the invitation, either during your visit or on a return trip, is valued and expected.
