# Being a Host & Guest

## Meetings & Presentations

Most business hosting in Hong Kong happens in restaurants, not homes. Flats are often small, multi-generational and treated as intensely private, so extending an invitation to the living room requires a close, trusted relationship. Hosting skill is shown by booking the right venue, ideally somewhere stylish or a private room in a well-regarded hotel or restaurant, and then guiding the meal gracefully. A colleague from a culture where home hospitality is a standard gesture of warmth may read this as coolness or formality, while a Hong Kong host may see a casual suggestion to "come over" as presumptuous before the relationship has deepened.

Gifts should be portable, consumable and easy to store. Good tea, specialty snacks, high-quality sweets, small bottles of wine or whisky, or elegant office items work well. Large ornaments or anything with overt political symbolism are awkward in a city where space is limited and symbols can be loaded. Present the gift with both hands to acknowledge the gesture's weight.

Seating and ordering follow quiet protocols. Senior hosts often sit facing the door and may select dishes for the table, while guests are offered positions with better views or central placement. Letting the host lead on these details, rather than insisting on equal input, respects the choreography of care that underpins the meal. Showing visible appreciation for the effort and choices reinforces the relationship the meal is meant to build.
