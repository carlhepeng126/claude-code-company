# Business Meeting Culture

## Meetings & Presentations

Meetings in Hong Kong are built for speed. The expectation is that you read the material beforehand, so the session itself becomes a place to clarify, decide and assign next steps, not to walk through slides line by line. Agendas are punchy and people move fast. If you arrive expecting a slow build toward consensus, you will notice impatience quickly. Send documents in advance and use the room for decisions, not reading.

In mixed-language settings, the official conversation may run in English, but quick asides in Cantonese reveal the real temperature. A brief exchange such as "冇可能啦" (no way) or "要同上面講先" (need to clear with upstairs) tells colleagues what will not fly or what still requires senior approval. If you do not speak Cantonese, ask a trusted local colleague afterward what surfaced off-mic. Silence in English does not mean silence in the room.

Hierarchy shapes who speaks when. Juniors rarely contradict a partner, managing director or general manager in front of others. Instead, they will debrief immediately afterward in a one-on-one chat, a WhatsApp group or the office pantry. Build in post-meeting touchpoints where people can voice reservations safely. The decision may look settled in the room, but the real conversation often continues outside it.

Hybrid etiquette is still taking shape. Many meetings now happen over Teams or Zoom from small flats, sometimes with family audible in the background. Cameras off may reflect bandwidth constraints or privacy, not disengagement. Focus on clear contributions and solid minutes rather than policing video norms.
