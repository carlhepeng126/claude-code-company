# Sharing Information

## Working Together

Information flow in Hong Kong runs across several parallel tracks, and understanding which channel holds what matters in practice. Email and shared drives serve as formal records, while day-to-day decisions and nuance often live in WhatsApp or WeChat chats. Some context never makes it into the official thread. Key decisions may be shaped in a side conversation and only documented later, if at all. If you're working across offices or joining a project midstream, clarify early what needs to be captured in formal channels and make sure those decisions are actually written down where others can find them.

Since the National Security Law, many people are more cautious with written words, especially on anything that might be read as political, sensitive, or critical. Tricky topics may move to phone calls or in-person chats on purpose. This isn't universal, but it's widespread enough that you may notice colleagues steering certain discussions offline. Keep work records factual and neutral, and take sensitive analysis out of email.

Language also shapes who sees what. Some information streams run mostly in English (global clients, expats), others in Chinese (local regulators, certain customer segments). Those fluent in both often act as informal gatekeepers. Don't over-rely on one bilingual colleague to translate or bridge. Distribute those tasks so knowledge isn't trapped with one person, and so you have visibility across both channels.
