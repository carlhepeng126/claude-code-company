# Business Entertaining

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Ecuador are a natural extension of workplace relationships, used to deepen trust and build rapport outside the formality of the office. Lunch is the most common setting for professional entertaining, often at restaurants that serve traditional coastal or highland dishes depending on the region. Dinner may also be offered, especially if a visiting colleague has more time or a stronger relationship has already been established. These gatherings are less about the venue and more about creating the conditions for open, personal conversation.

The meal itself is rarely rushed. Ecuadorians tend to regard time spent together over food as a way to understand character and intent, and business topics often take a back seat. Conversations may stay personal, covering family, travel, or the day's events, and switching to work-related matters too quickly can feel transactional. Hosts will usually indicate when business discussion is welcome. A colleague from a culture where meals are tightly scheduled and task-focused may read this slower pace as inefficient, while an Ecuadorian counterpart may see the foreign visitor's directness as lacking warmth or depth.

Reciprocating an invitation is expected and appreciated, though it does not need to happen immediately. Offering to host a meal later in the trip, or on a return visit, shows respect for the relationship and an understanding that professional ties in Ecuador are mutual and ongoing.
