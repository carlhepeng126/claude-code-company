# Business Lunch & Dinner

## Meetings & Presentations

Business meals in Ecuador are primarily social occasions. The focus is on building personal trust and warmth before advancing professional goals. Ecuadorians tend to separate relationship-building from business discussion, and lunch or dinner provides the setting where connection happens. Colleagues from cultures where meals are used to discuss strategy or close deals may feel the conversation stays too general or informal for too long. From the Ecuadorian side, introducing business too early can feel transactional and may signal that the relationship itself holds less value than the outcome.

Lunch is the more common option for a business meal, often starting around one or two in the afternoon and lasting two hours or more. Multi-course meals are typical, and the pace is unhurried. Trying to move things along or checking the time repeatedly can come across as disinterest in the person across the table. If you are hosting or being hosted, expect the business topic to emerge naturally later in the meal, if at all. The meal itself is the work.

Coastal cities like Guayaquil tend to offer lighter, seafood-based dishes, while the highlands favor heartier fare. Restaurants in larger cities are accustomed to international guests, but it is still worth confirming dietary needs ahead of time. Alcohol may be offered, though not always. If you are unsure, follow your host's lead rather than ordering independently.
