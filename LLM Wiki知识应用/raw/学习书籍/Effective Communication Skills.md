Introduction book 1 Having poor communication skills can keep you from satisfying your core needs. The ability to express yourself and listen attentively is a much underestimated skill. As human beings we have needs, and for the most part, we rely on other human beings to fulfill these needs. So, it is important that we know how to communicate with other humans so that we can satisfy these needs. Since the dawn of mankind we have always been about teaming up, or in other words, forming relationships. And the success of a relationship is in large measure down to our communication skills. We have to be excellent at communicating interpersonally through both verbal and nonverbal means. This book details how people ought to approach communication so that they can create successful marriages. Communication skill is not ingrained in our DNA. Thus, any motivated person can acquire communication skills and improve the quality of their dating life.

Chapter 1: Effective communication techniques for couples Research shows that poor communication is one of the leading causes of divorce, and that’s really awful considering that mastering communication is one of the stress-free exercises. It is upon the couple to understand each other and ensure that everyone’s message is received just as intended. Sadly, most couples who are unable to communicate effectively tend to shift blame on their partner for failing to get them. And in the midst of that blame game, their relationship comes crashing down. These are some of the vital communication techniques that couples ought to observe: Talk your issues out at the opportune time Unless it is an extremely pressing matter, there’s no reason why you shouldn’t wait for the appropriate time to bring it up. Most couples tackle issues as they crop up, and it breeds combativeness between one another. The best way is to always mention an issue and then explain that you will both work out the issue at a certain time. This shows that you respect your partner and it compels them to cooperate. On the other hand, if you have a tendency of erupting and putting your partner in tight spots, they will get defensive, and start to resent you. And at the end of the day you won’t make any progress. Obviously, the “right time” is subjective. There are many factors to consider when choosing the opportune moment to discuss your problems, but it comes down to what makes your partner comfortable. Imaginative couples may go out on dates, talk their issues out over a meal and drinks, and the presence of other people will be an active reminder of their need to be civil. Other couples may settle for talking either before or after bed. Selecting the best time to “talk” is influenced by among other factors, personality and philosophy. Ensure that you get your partner’s perspective The most practical way of ensuring you understand your partner is to pay attention especially when their words or actions stir you a lot. Hold yourself until it’s your turn to speak, but more importantly, listen to understand, rather than merely to give a response. Put your focus on the conversation and resist the “Me VS them” mentality. You should view the conversation as just two subjective arguments. To ensure that you understand your partner’s perspective, eliminate the doubts. If there’s something you want further clarification on, just ask about it. Of course it is no mean task to concentrate on a conversation that triggers your defensiveness and still be objective. Learn to recognize when you are caving in and take a short break. Every person has a breaking point. If you become overwhelmed by emotions you might be unable to follow through on your sober plan. But taking a break will help you stabilize your emotions and be able to act in a rational manner. When you understand where your partner is coming from, it doesn’t matter whether they are right or wrong, but it improves your chances of finding a common ground. Mind your tone and language Realize that everyone has an ego and they are bound to fight back when you put them on blast. You may be objectively correct, but then fail to get your way simply because your language and tone is wanting. Thus, avoid using an accusatory tone whenever you are talking thing out with your partner. For instance, if your partner has been spending what you feel is a lot of money on their grooming; don’t start with “You are such a wasteful person! You used all our money on clothes and shoes” It hits at their ego and causes them to want to retaliate. But you could say something along the lines of, “I appreciate that you’ve always wanted to groom yourself like royalty, but in these hard economic times, we have got to be a little keener on how we spend money, right?” When you avoid name-calling and at the same time use a pleasant voice it shows that you respect and value your partner. Ensure that you don’t belittle your partner and be aware of your biases. Such tendencies will bring out the best in your partner when resolving issues. Think before you speak You certainly don’t want to be impulsive. It’s so easy to say something hurtful or stupid in a heartbeat and cause a rift in your talks. Ensure that you weigh every word that rolls out of your mouth. This promotes a civil conversation that will see you achieve your objective. Don’t be in a hurry to say the first thing that comes to mind. When you are careless with the first word you are likely to put yourself in a hard spot and subsequent words will only worsen the situation. It is better to say nothing at all than to speak regretful words, but when you say something you shouldn’t have, offer a concise apology. Have facts If the issues revolve around one or both partners doing something they shouldn’t have, then the offended party must have proof. For instance, if you suspect your partner of straying from marriage and sleeping with another person (or people), ensure that you have sufficient evidence to show that they actually strayed. Accusing your partner of infidelity when they are actually innocent could put a strain in your marriage that might be impossible to get over. In order to be the person that gathers evidence before bringing up an issue, you have to have some patience. But when you are conducting your investigations ensure that you are mature enough to keep a good attitude. Having facts and talking in a sincere and respectful fashion will grant you a productive conversation. On the other hand, coming at your partner with no facts, plus using derogatory terms, will only make the situation worse. Body language They say that body language accounts for upwards of 60% of communication. This means people can tell more about you by just observing you than hearing your words. When you are communicating with your partner, it is extremely important that you keep the right body language. For instance, if you mean to tell your spouse that they look nice in their outfit, you have to face them, with your eyes lit up, and then say it. Don’t just catch their reflection on a thermos’ and then mouth off a compliment without so much as turning your neck; it would come off as insincere. Some of the body language tips to keep in mind include: 1. Eye contact: maintain a substantial amount of eye contact throughout your conversation. It shows that you are sincere and respectful and it compels your partner to act in an appropriate manner. 2. Tone: your voice is every bit as important. Use a pleasant but firm tone when outlining an issue. If the situation gets tense, don’t raise your voice. Using a commanding voice is likely to put your partner on the defensive and frustrate your objectives. 3. Posture: anything for which you have set time apart to discuss with your partner is important enough to warrant a good posture. Ensure that you are either sitting or standing upright with your head looking forward. Don’t slouch or drape your limbs over the items around you.

Chapter 2: Frequent communication errors couples make Open and honest communication is the keystone of every successful relationship. At the same time it can be a difficult thing to achieve and couples are well aware of this fact. There are various mistakes that couples do – consciously and unconsciously – that frustrate their communication efforts. Assumptions If your partner is a naturally outgoing person, and then suddenly they clam up and limit their interaction with you, it’s a sign that something is not well. This should spur you into seeking a remedy. Of course merely asking your partner whether they are all right is likely to be met with affirmative answers. You have to schedule a meeting and talk at length about precisely what’s ailing them. But sadly most people have a tendency of assuming that everything is all right even when it clearly isn’t. Being a distraction Let’s say that your partner works as a feature journalist with a national newspaper. And this particular day they come home and rush into their study room, complaining that their deadline is fast approaching and they haven’t touched the assignment yet. It automatically means that your partner is busy with the assignment and would appreciate that you don’t disturb. Sadly, there are insensitive people who would still go ahead and bring up less important issues with their partner, perhaps even shouting through the door from outside. Such behavior can invite resentment from your significant other. Negative overgeneralizations If you’re overgeneralizing in a positive direction, that’s welcome. But if you are always accusing your partner of the bad things that they do or not do, you are going to make them resent you. Negative overgeneralizations make a person think that you look down upon them or have taken a dislike to them, making them want to fight back. Excessive criticism Your partner is just like everyone else: they want to be accepted for who they are. Criticism is a form of disapproval, but if it’s expressed the right way, criticism can do more good than harm. The problem comes up when it is done excessively and in a destructive manner. For instance, if your every move is met with criticism from your partner, it can get to a point and frustrate you, break your relationship, even. So a couple ought to trend carefully where criticism is concerned. Interrupting When you butt in while your partner is still speaking they may take it that you consider yourself more important than them. You don’t want them to take that route. What’s hard about waiting for your turn to speak? Okay, sometimes it’s hard, particularly when your partner is saying something that triggers your emotions, but you have to learn to be patient for your relationship’s sake. Speaking in turns will create an ideal atmosphere for an objective conversation to take place. Lecturing The actions or words of your partner might make you get beside yourself with rage, but don’t be tempted to lecture them. They are not your kids. Lecturing your partner is a form of degradation. They will come from it with major resentment. It doesn’t matter how bothered or hurt you are, seek remedy through courteous means. It is very important that your partner understands you respect them. Being insensitive There are common things that people can be insensitive about. For instance, weight, health, and finances. If your partner is overweight, you have to be careful how you handle that topic. If your partner is no doing well financially, you still have to be careful how you talk about finances with them. And if your partner has a health issues you also have to mind your words about that topic. Being a sensitive person is mostly about being a critical thinker. Life is about drifting from one set of circumstances to another, meaning that there will always be new experiences to test how sensitive you are. Ensure that you have your partner’s respect. Passive aggressiveness A passive aggressive person exhibits sneaky behaviors and they are not clear about what they want and yet they are uncooperative. Nothing indicates childishness in a person more than passive aggressiveness. When someone starts behaving passive aggressively, they encourage the other person to lose trust in them. Passive aggressive people are unpredictable and in the long run they cause tremendous pain. Talking over each other Talking over each other means that no one is listening to the other. It halts progress. You should learn to wait for your turn to speak. Otherwise, when you talk while your partner is still talking is not only unhelpful, but also disrespectful. In the same breath, a partner should not talk for longer than necessary. They should allow their partner to chip in. This habit of not letting the other person speak is what accelerates conflict and things could very easily become ugly. Giving advice even when you’re not asked Another communication mistake that couples are prone to make is giving unsolicited advice. Not every conversation that a couple has is about resolving conflict. Sometimes, the other party might just be looking to get therapeutic benefits of talking to someone. They are not looking for your advice. When you develop the habit of dispensing random advice to your partner, they might think that you consider them incapable of handling themselves, thus triggering resentment. Avoiding tough topics Running away from your problems won’t help matters. You will still come back to these problems. So, you have to develop a can-do attitude and tackle these hard topics. Pushing a tough topic back only worsens the situation and invites strife in your relationship. On the other hand, working together through difficulties will actually strengthen your bond. Tough topics must be handled with a lot of care, and they require lots of attitude-adjustment, or else the relationship might implode. Tough situations not only improve your communication but also make you a stronger couple on the whole. Attacking the person instead of the issue When negative emotions take over, modesty is tossed out the window; it’s what leads people to attack their partners instead of articulating their concerns. Obviously this kind of behavior causes the offended party to be resentful of their partner. It is easier to attack the person instead of studying your situation. For instance, if you tell your spouse to stop by the groceries store when they are coming back from work, and yet they somehow forget to do it, it can be tempting to shout an insult, and remind them that they have always been unreliable. The much more effective way of handling this scenario would be to calmly explain why repeated inability to buy groceries causes them distress. Inability to appreciate your partner Appreciating your partner is about using words that make your partner feel warm inside. Validation coming from a significant other is always welcome. When you make your partner feel nice about themselves, they are likely to cooperate with you, and agree to your propositions. But when you make them feel small, disrespected, and crazy, you are likely to trigger their hatred as they fight back.

Chapter 3: How to improve communication between couples The most important thing when it comes to communication between couples is that they understand each other. These are some of the ways one may boost communication with their partners: Planning The excuse that most of us give when asked why we are not spending enough time with our loved ones is that we are busy. One might think we are involved in such big projects that earth’s survival depends on our success. But the truth is that most of us allow distractions to steal our time, and the biggest of all is social media. If we are serious about improving our communication we should plan about it. We have to draft schedules for spending time together in order to talk out our issues. By being deliberate, as opposed to being a chance-taker, your partner can see that you mean well for them. Planning not only improves communication between couples but also makes their relationship stable. “I” statements as opposed to “You” statements When an issue comes up, understand that your partner will be keen on your word selection. It’s perhaps an unconscious survival tactic for they want to clear themselves of any wrong doing. Thus, avoid using phrases that stir your partner’s defensive spirit, but use statements that present the conflict in neutral terms. This means, you have to use more “I” statements as opposed to “You” statements. “I” statements indicate that you are expressing an issue from how it’s affecting you and not necessarily attacking your partner. For instance, if there’s an issue with the way money is being spent, you might say, “I think it’s time we have a discussion about our spending habits. I think we can minimize our expenses without lowering our quality of life.” the issue is presented in such a neutral, nonthreatening way that would easily invite their partner’s cooperation. On the other hand, “You” statements can be incredibly accusatory. For instance: “You’re such a wasteful person! You spend more money than there’s need for! Tame your impulsivity before you ruin us!” Such statements would put your partner in war mode. Bring up issues in a specific manner Nothing is as frustrating as having to deal with a person that hardly expresses them straightforwardly. For instance, if they are well aware that you received a bonus check at your place of work, and they are in need of some money, they might say something along the lines of, “I really want to buy a new set of seats for my restaurant but I have no cash. Where can I get a loan?” They are well aware that you could help out but instead of phrasing the sentence appropriately, they expect you to catch up and give an answer that they approve of. If such a partner is with someone who couldn’t care less, they might have a hard time getting their way, until they are specific about what they mean. Don’t mind-read You can never tell what your partner is thinking at any time, so avoid imagining that you understand their thoughts. When you mind-read your partner, chances are you will make the wrong conclusions, and acting upon such information worsens the situation. For instance, if you have not done something that is expected of you, and in the evening your partner appears sullen, you might be tempted to “mind-read” that they are obviously mad at you, when it is not necessarily the case. Perhaps they had a difficult day in the office! Or they heard about something distressing. So, always ask your partner what’s going on behind their eyes before you rush into a conclusion. Avoid using insults Some people are so uncouth that they use insults when communicating with their partners. And you might find them justifying their behavior, expecting their partners to put up with such abuse, but anyone with sufficient self-esteem wouldn’t be okay with that. Using insults is a clear indication that you have no respect for your partner. The end results can only be ugly. It doesn’t matter what your spouse has done, but you must always be dignified, if at all you are interested in seeking remedy. Insults can be very hurtful. And they wreck egos. If your partner is the unforgiving type, they can nurse vengeful thoughts way long after an insult. To ensure that you don’t insult your partner always think through what you are about to say and also learn to express criticism in a sensitive, yet constructive manner. Stop being defensive Don’t make it seem as if your partner is out to get you every minute. Resist the urge of retorting to every word that your partner utters. Be comfortable with letting them talk out what’s bothering them without feeling the need to butt in and defend ourselves. The thing is, if you are an insecure person, you would look at a normal story and twist it in a manner to support your warped beliefs. Being ultra-defensive is a sign of deep insecurities, and if they don’t seek help when there’s still time, they ultimately suffer defeat. Affirm your partner The problem with most couples is that they are always ready to highlight problems and don’t celebrate each other enough. In order to notice the great qualities of your partner you have to stop taking them for granted. If your partner does something nice, praise them. If they are dressed to the nines, praise them. Marriage counselors rightfully say that the affection in a marriage is like the deposits in a bank account. It is upon the couple to constantly deposit affection into their account so that they’ll always be happy with one another. Of course someone shouldn’t place their self-worth on another person’s validation; but being validated by your partner feels damn great. Listen actively When your partner is talking, resist the urge of thinking up your “comeback.” You are not against your spouse. You’re playing as a team. So, ensure that you are listening actively when your partner is speaking. The general rules of active listening are: 1. Face them: this allows you to get the message pretty well and leave no chance for misinterpretation. 2. Eye contact: looking your partner in the eyes as they speak shows that you value their input. They feel appreciated. 3. Tone: when it’s your turn to speak, it is important to use the right tone just as it is important to use the right words. The perfect tone will not only disarm your partner but also make them appreciate your gentleness. Join in on the other person’s fun Couples can strengthen their communication game when they are happy around each other. Generally, each person will have their hobbies that are unlike. Instead of castigating your partner for being different, you could take an interest in their hobbies, and have fun together. Such activities bring couples close together. And it makes communication much easier. Be calm No matter what goes down, never lose your calm. Obviously, it is not as easy as it sounds. You might find yourself in the middle of a high-intensity drama and all sorts of emotions would be boiling inside of you. Take a time-out and wait for your emotions to become stable again. When you are calm you can always have a productive conversation.

Chapter 4: How to listen to your partner To have a strong marriage, a couple must be good at listening to one another. If one is not careful, you may not listen to your partner as attentively as you should, for there are many distractions, tasks, and people vying for your attention. Being a good listener promotes cooperation and strengthens a marriage. The following tips are aimed at boosting our capacity to listen effectively to our partners. Have empathy Don’t just before your partner and listen for the sake of listening. Try to put yourself in their shoes and see where they are coming from. This helps you get the perspective of your partner. When you listen with empathy it won’t be hard to resolve conflict since you already your partner’s perspective. Empathy allows us to become better individuals and act in a manner that promotes unity. Allow your partner to express their emotions When your partner has had a tough day, they may not be looking for a solution, but just a shoulder to lean on. This is especially the case with females. So, allow your woman to vent their frustrations and don’t for a moment imagine that they are weak. Once they have cleared away their emotions, they will return to a state of happiness, but suppressing emotions can only worsen their circumstances. So, ensure that you are a pleasant listener who lets their spouse understand that it’s okay to speak about anything with them. Be aware of your biases When you have a matter between yourselves and everyone takes a different side, the issue could very easily become ugly. You might have biases that inform your decisions and drive a wedge between you and your partner. Thus, you have to be acutely aware of these biases that influence your decisions. When your partner is speaking, detach yourself from these biases for long enough to grasp what your partner is really saying. Being aware of your biases shows that you are, to a certain extent, introspective, and this puts you in a better position to resolve conflict as opposed to when you are biased and yet closed off. It is a strong indication that you are willing to agree to a compromise in order to end the conflict. Show that you care Understand that listening is not merely sitting before your partner with droopy eyes. Show them that you care by giving appropriate responses to whatever they are saying. For instance, if your partner is speaking about something horrendous, you have to act shocked. But don’t do it like an actor. If you invest in active listening it will come out naturally. Your partner’s words will be readily evident in your reactions. When your partner realizes that you are indeed an active listener, it gives them a great impression of you, and compels them to be more understanding and cooperative. Don’t judge Sometimes it can be hard to listen to your spouse without being judgmental. Depending on the matter at hand, we will always weigh their actions or words by our own standards. For instance, if your spouse has started a business, and it seems they are running into problems, don’t be so keen on happily pointing out the areas that your spouse took wrong moves. When you become judgmental it can cause your partner to resent you. And this would ruin any chance of making up and strengthening your relationship. Apologize When your partner talks about your misbehavior, you must apologize, or at the very least give an apology. People with big egos have a hard time apologizing because they think it makes them look small. But that’s not the case at all. Seeking an apology is in fact a demonstration of strength. But the most important thing is that you actually reform, otherwise you are theoretically going to spend your life apologizing. A sincere apology should be complimented with a genuine effort to change for the better. Some hardcore behaviors can be hard to get rid of, but all it takes is patience and practice. Avoid interrupting Interrupting your partner while they are speaking is not only rude but it actually keeps you from understanding – or remembering – what they just said. You want to be the person who expresses calmness and can contain their emotions for quite long. If you have something to say, wait until they pause, or it’s your turn to speak. Some issues can arouse strong feelings in you and it can be hard to communicate with your partner without these feelings overpowering you. In such cases, you may take a break, or actually look for alternative means of communication. You could write your spouse a long email detailing every last concern of yours. Don’t walk out on them Sometimes, the conversation can be so frustrating that you feel like walking out and leaving them talking to themselves. If you give in to that thought just realize that you have made it even worse. Walking out on someone amidst a conversation invites resentment as they see you as an enemy. It breaks any hope of resolving a conflict. Ego can be a dangerous little thing and it can cause us to go to extreme lengths just to prove a point. Eliminate distractions For the average person, there are so many things vying for their attention e.g. phones, computers, and their own wild imagination. It’s important to shield yourself both mentally and physically against these things, for they would keep you from listening attentively. When you give your partner full attention it shows that you value and respect them. But if you are “listening” to them and at the same time watching match highlights on your smartphone, that’s a sign of disrespect. Eliminating distractions is about cultivating discipline and improving the person that you are. Realize that it’s a two-way affair Effective listening makes for a great conversation. You have to be careful that the conversation is not one-sided. When your partner is speaking, ensure that you are fully attentive, and when they stop speaking, say something appropriate, so that it’s your partner’s turn to be an active listener. Don’t let your partner do all the talking while you just sit there and listen for forever. Make eye contact When you establishing eye contact, it boosts your concentration into what your partner is talking about, but then you want to be careful that it doesn’t come across as an intimidation tactic. Long, unblinking stares make you look bad. If you are uncomfortable about looking right into another person’s eyes, you can shift your gaze between their eyes. Apart from showing that you appreciate your partner, eye contact communicates sincerity, and when it’s absent it makes people distrust each other. Be patient Not everyone is gifted at communicating, and more specifically, not everyone can express themselves pretty quickly. If the challenge of slow communication wasn’t much of a big deal to stop you from welcoming that person into your life, then you should play along. Avoid pressuring your partner. And give them time to form their opinions and arguments and to express them satisfactorily.

Chapter 5: Tools that improve communication in marriage Understanding that communication is vital for the success of a marriage is one thing but actually communicating effectively is another thing. If you’d like inspiration as to how to go about it, use the following communication tools. The first response rule People don’t wake up one day and start fighting. It’s usually a slow process, sometimes fast, of back and forth comments. If your spouse utters a conflict-ridden phrase, desist from launching a counter-attack, for that’s precisely what contributes to full blown conflict. The person who responds first has the power to nip the conflict in the bud. Obviously this requires a lot of discipline in order to pull off. It’s not easy to keep your cool when someone else launches an open attack against your ego. The physical touch rule Giving someone a gentle touch evokes a sense of connection between you and them, which eliminates tension, and discourages fighting. But it may not go as expected when you have already started an argument. This tool works when executed at the start of a conversation; particularly when you sit down to discuss a sensitive matter. Just touch your partner both when you are speaking and when you are listening. It will make both of you feel nice and it will discourage the temptation to fight. The numbers technique This technique is most helpful when you haven’t quite understood what your partner just said. Most people are at pains framing precisely what they intend to be clarified and it can take away from their happiness. Using the numbers technique you have the chance to elaborate precisely what you’d want. For instance, if you come up with an idea, and your partner says that they don’t want to do that, what do they really mean? Is it that they hate the idea but are willing to talk about it anyway? Is it that they don’t care at all about the idea? Is it that in an ideal world they’d love the idea but now circumstances keep them from liking it? Or is it that they had past terrible experiences with the idea. The idea is to generate as many as possible variations of a statement or claim. Knowing precisely what your partner means is critical in steering a conversation forward. The pencil tactic Sometimes you might be having a difficult issue before you but fail to understand how to approach it or when you try to pay listen to one another but you keep failing. In other words, the issue could be overwhelming. Having the wrong approach could bring undesirable results and this causes delay. This technique requires that the person holding a pen for a particular time to start off. When the person gets started, they should be given ample time to express what bother them, and if there’s something that the other party cannot understand, they are to seek clarification, but not go further than that. The speaker should use a lot more “I” statements. For instance, they can say, “I feel that this relationship could improve if I’m less stressed…” Once they are done speaking, they may pass over the pencil to their partner for a response. The quarter-hour rule This technique is most appropriate for couples who are prone to talking over each other. Some couples find themselves having such wild conversations that it becomes hard for each of them to wait their turn. Obviously, that’s a risky situation, as things could escalate pretty quickly. This rule advocates for giving one person fifteen minutes to talk after which the other party has fifteen minutes to respond. One must not speak when the other one is speaking. This technique helps couples to work their differences out without going for each other’s throat. It also gives sufficient room for elaboration of viewpoints, thus ensuring that the couple fully understands one another. The timing factor Your partner won’t be happy all the time. And they won’t be sad all the time. To an extent, the success of a conversation depends on the time you choose to bring up an issue. Obviously, bringing up an issue when your partner is distressed is likely to have ugly results. So, you must avoid bringing up important issues when one of you is experiencing one of these conditions: 1. Hunger: bringing up an important conversation when your partner is battling hunger pangs is likely to result in conflict. When someone is hungry means that their brain is scrimping on resources which can hinder objectivity. So, wait until they are full. 2. Angry: you would have to be pretty unwise to bring up a conversation when your partner is mad at someone or the world. Anger causes us to be bling. And this can constrict our critical thinking abilities. 3. Lonely: when your partner is feeling alone, they are not keen on dissecting another one of life’s challenges, but the one thing that they are really in need of is your companionship. They need you take them in their arms and assure them that things will be fine. 4. Tired: are you the kind of person that brings up issues immediately after your partner walks into the house after a difficult day at work? You are needlessly jeopardizing your relationship. Such behavior is not only bothersome but disrespectful as hell. Always wait for the opportune moment to bring up an issue, especially if it is a sensitive matter. The “Three and three” decree Some couples experience difficulties listening to each other. They are perpetually caught up in fights. And this makes it hard for them to make any progress. This tool can be effective in helping such couples overcome their challenge of not paying attention to one another. It starts with the flip of a coin. The winner gets to talk – uninterrupted – for three minutes. Then the other person takes three minutes to issue a reply. Such an arrangement ensures that both partners are heard. The S.T.O.P. rule This tool is aimed at avoiding conflict and reconciling couples at the onset of a fight. For instance, if a couple is engaged in a sensitive topic, and the temperature of the conversation is steadily rising, they have to STOP the conversation. Then they have to take a break, each one hanging out in a different area for between thirty to sixty minutes to ensure they are totally calm. Chapter 1: Effective workplace communication: what it means Workplace communication is basically the exchange of ideas and information, either verbally or non-verbally, between a person or group and another person or group in the workplace. It involves making calls, sending texts and emails, writing notes, and word of mouth. Effective communication promotes good professional ties, increased productivity, and helps the company accomplish its objectives. It is critical that both employees and employers improve their effectiveness at communicating in the workplace. The following are some of the factors that promote this skill. The art of timing It doesn’t matter how valuable your message is, but if you are not sensitive to the timing, the reception will be poor. For instance, if you approach a co-worker who’s racing against time to beat a deadline, and bring up some non-urgent issues, you risk annoying them, or burdening them unnecessarily. Thus, ensure that you bring up issues at the opportune moment. It is a key factor in appearing charismatic to other people. Professionalism In the workplace, you should always be professional. First off, ensure that you are dressed in a presentable manner. People make quick judgments upon seeing you. Thus, it is upon you to ensure that they have a pleasant impression of you. You also have to be respectful of others, regardless of their position in the command chain. Some ill-meaning people, especially those who have managerial position, have a tendency of treating their juniors disrespectfully, thus putting a strain on their relationship. Being respectful ensures that your message is taken in good faith. But when you are disrespectful, people will conceive you as an enemy, and they’ll start to wish that you go down. You must also avoid whining. It’s true that some workplaces are filled with challenges that disrupt the normal functioning. But look for solutions without lamenting endlessly to your juniors or superiors. The ability to resist whining is a trait of a great leader. Every company has their preferred way of doing things. It doesn’t matter what you think should be done instead. Ensure that you respect the system. You must also be seen to have basic manners. Learn to say “thank you” and “please” when the situation calls for it. Understand different personalities At your workplace, most of the personality types are possibly represented. This means that employees won’t have a uniform character. Some employees are naturally talkative whereas others prefer being silent. When you understand people’s personality, you’ll determine how best to approach them. For instance, if you realize that person X really likes talking to people. You might want to walk up to them and disclose your message. And if you realize that person Y isn’t open, that they like keeping to themselves, you might want to send them an email or text, and in that way you won’t make them feel uncomfortable. Generally, it is advisable for all workers to be outgoing. This promotes a culture of inclusion. But then personalities are practically unalterable. Someone might mask their introversion and start acting like an extrovert, but suppressing their real nature might cause them emotional distress. If you are in a managerial position, it really helps to distinguish your juniors by their personalities. Once they recognize that you are sensitive to their needs, they will take you for a considerate and charismatic leader, and this will increase their morale and productivity. Active listening Effective communication is not one-sided. Both parties must participate. If information or ideas are passed down from management, it is the duty of employees to listen very well. Some people might cheapen the need of practicing active listening, and then they are unable to grasp the message accurately, either by virtue of not hearing precisely, or misinterpreting the information. Active listening is about being fully attentive. First off, you have to establish eye contact with the speaker. This is an expression of both trust and confidence and it encourages the speaker. Active listening allows you to become aware of the nonverbal cues. There are hints, conveyed by the speaker, that are not verbal, but they still add to the message. For instance, the tone of the speaker can point to the weight of the matter at hand. One must also not interrupt the speaker. This may alter their train of thought and ruin the delivery of their message. If you have an issue, you had better note it down, and bring it up afterward. Also, avoid jumping into conclusions, or making quick judgments before the speaker is done. Ensure that you stay focused throughout the entire process. And once the questions are welcome, be sure to ask away your concerns. Positive mindset Attitude is everything. It doesn’t matter how important the ideas or information might be, but when you have a negative attitude, you’ll hardly make any progress. A positive mindset is basically looking on the bright side of things and having a can-do attitude. There are a number of things you must do in order to maintain a positive mindset: 1. Spend more time with positive individuals: we end up becoming just like the people we associate ourselves with. Thus, we ought to associate with positive people in order to take on their positivity. An individual with a positive mindset will always speak with self-belief and act with determination. Stay away from negative colleagues and surround yourself with positive colleagues. 2. Fill up your mind with positive things: there are so many things out there vying for your attention, especially when you consider the Internet. If you are not careful, you would easily get sucked into the bottomless pit of non-helpful content. Once you fill up your mind with bad content you’ll start to ooze negativity. The good thing about content proliferation is that there’s the good content too, although it doesn’t get marketed around. Ensure that you are filling your mind up with positive things. For this will allow you to send out positive energy. 3. Watch your language: always ensure that you are aware of what you are saying. Don’t be the person that talks for the sake of talking. Always think through your sentences before you utter them. This enables you to communicate sense and also sound positive. When you let your mouth run loose, you are at risk of letting out unworthy words that may taint your image. 4. Follow a routine: having a routine allows you to make the best use of your time. Sadly, most of us tend to drift from one task to the next until the day is over, which is what puts us at risk of becoming negative individuals. When you have a routine, you are aware of what you should be doing at any given time. Come up with a realistic routine and stick to it. 5. Be kind to others: we tend to feel great about ourselves when we extend our kindness to those that need it. Being kind is not about donating millions to a charity. It’s about helping in the way that you can. Thus, it doesn’t matter your background, but you can always help out. Improve message clarity There are many instances we would think that we have communicated our intended message when the opposite is true. Thus, it is prudent that we craft our message in such a manner that people won’t have any doubts as to what we mean. The following are some of the factors to consider in boosting clarity in your message: 1. Use descriptive words: for people to understand what you are saying, you have to express your exact meaning, and this can only be achieved by ensuring that you use accurate words. Thus, you will have to invest more time in crafting your message, to weed out the imperfect words and faulty sentences, and capture precisely what you mean. One of the ways to increase your capacity to use descriptive words is by reading voraciously. 2. Avoid complex vocabulary: there are some people who are interested in showing off their impressive vocabulary, which drives them to use heavy words. There’s nothing wrong with using complex words, but ask yourself whether the people you are talking to can understand what you are saying. If they can, then go on throwing those difficult words. But to be safe, you had better use simple, descriptive words to convey your message. 3. Be concise: we live in an era of tight schedules. There’s so much going on in our lives. There are so many things demanding our attention almost simultaneously. Thus, we cannot afford to spend more time than necessary on a single task. For that reason, communication should be short and simple. You have to figure out a way of passing your message with the least amount of words as possible. 4. Choose the right medium: if you have an urgent message, you would reach more people texting them, as opposed to say, sending an email. This is because people are wired to check their phones when it buzzes but they are not necessarily checking their emails every thirty minutes. So, ensure that you match the nature and urgency of your message with the appropriate medium.

Chapter 2: Significance of effective workplace communication Communication in the workplace cannot be avoided. There aren’t many jobs that you can do alone, and even if you find one, you will probably have to report to a senior officer. As an employee or employer, communication, whether official or nonofficial, is pretty much unavoidable. For a business to thrive there has to be effective communication both vertically and horizontally. The following are some of the benefits of communicating effectively in the workplace. Increased productivity among workers Research shows that companies with an effective communication structure tend to make the most use of their resources. Consequently, productive employees are likely to be skilled in communication. High productivity means that workers are utilizing company resources to the maximum, which leads to an increase in revenue. Effective communication, especially from the superiors to the juniors, is critical in promoting productivity. On the other hand, poor communication leads to poor performance. For instance, if the bosses and the employees have a cavalier attitude about work, then anything goes, and such an attitude opens the gates for errors. Managers hold a lot of power in terms of pushing their employees to be productive, and one of the critical factors is effective communication. They have to pass down clear and concise information consistently. It promotes teamwork You’ll rarely find jobs that require your input only. At most companies, it’s all about everyone doing their part. There can be a serious problem if people are not excited about what they are doing. They’d be simply acting like robots on command. But one of the factors that promote team cohesion is effective communication. When employees are at liberty to consult each other, and discuss their progress, then you have a team that enjoys collaborating. Such an environment promotes job satisfaction and pushes people to bring out their A game so as to not disappoint their colleagues. When everyone is kept in the loop, and secrecy is shunned, workers gain morale. Effective communication not only brings a team together but also improves their mindset. And when you have a strong team working toward a common goal, you can be sure that the results will be nothing short of stellar. It promotes growth Excellent communication in the workplace is vital in eliminating errors. Thus, when everything falls into place, by virtue of error-free decisions, the business is bound to expand. Every business owner will tell you that growth is one of their major goals. Great communication ensures that employees are making the best use of available resources, which drives revenues up. When the workplace communication is effective, it allows the management to come up with a clear vision for the company. And obviously, a great vision is necessary for business growth. Great communication structure allows the managers to identify talented individuals. In as much as companies rely on teamwork to get results, you can’t neglect the great input of talented employees. Managers ought to keep an eye out for people who can become a good fit for their companies. Effective communication is vital for purposes of using tools. Depending on the industry, there’ll always be tools to drive your company forward, but sadly not every employee may catch on fast on how to use these tools. One of the reasons is that the communication is poor. But when communication is great, the employees are in a position to utilize the tools pretty well, something that drives the company forward. It promotes innovation Innovation is the process of introducing better ideas and deriving greater value from resources. Some businesses try to “save themselves” by cutting costs when they should really focus on innovating. Effective communication is vital for a business to be innovative. For instance, if employees resist sharing their ideas for fear of rejection, then that company might stagnant. But when there’s a great system that promotes free flow of information, then great ideas will hardly go down the drain. Another thing about innovation is that it takes team work. People may be independently talented and have great ideas, but they will have to come together and fuse their ideas to form something magical. Thanks to great communication, employees can form strong teams, and by extension, boost innovation. Considering that effective communication ensures complete allotment of responsibilities, everyone is aware of their role, which ultimately promotes the spirit of innovation. For a company to be innovative there has to be some level of integration i.e. everyone must feel as though they belong. It takes effective communication strategies to ensure that employees integrate. It boosts efficiency Poor communication leads to poor performance. For if instructions aren’t clear, or not given at all, employees are bound to muddle things up. On the other hand, great communication ensures message clarity, thus one is less likely to make an error. When employees are free to consult each other, maybe seeking help, it results in increased efficiency. It is a no-brainer that every company needs skilled workers. Most companies actually retrain their new employees as a way of assimilating them into their culture. But it takes a proper communication culture for workers to acquire these skills and become efficient at their roles. For a company to register growth there has to be efficient utilization of resources. Effective communication empowers the managers to delegate tasks appropriately, which leads to effective use of resources. Without an efficient communication system, managers would probably mismatch tasks with skills, which would cause a massive negative effect on productivity. Company goals empower the employees to stay focused and diligent. But it takes proper communication for employees to internalize these goals and work toward realizing them. It boosts loyalty If you were employed by Company X, and then Company Z came along and offered you both a higher pay and a higher position, would you go? If your answer is a bold “No”, then you are loyal to your company. Sadly, it cannot be said of most employees. One of the factors that promote employee loyalty is a strong communication culture that makes everyone feel valuable. In order to increase employee trust, you have to give them more control. If there’s efficient communication, it is much easier to give employees control, which promises greater efficiency and increased productivity. An efficient communication platform allows employees to contact their superiors with no hassle. For instance, an employee may contact their superiors to offer key business suggestions and referrals, and seeing their ideas implemented, they become emotionally invested in the company, which makes it harder for them to leave for the proverbial “greener grass”. It aids in resolving conflict It’s the ugly side to human beings, but it has to be confronted nonetheless. Human beings rarely coexist without having issues with one another. And this causes conflict. But it is necessary to resolve conflict before everything goes to hell. In the workplace there are conflicts too that stem from all manner of things like politics, gossip, performance, and cold hatred. Colleagues can become vicious to one another. But it takes efficient communication to put workplace wars to an end. First off, the warring parties can be talked to, and when sense is finally drilled into the offending party, the conflict is over. Some other times, workers squabble so as to create some attention and have someone listen to their grievances. So, it helps that there is someone to listen to them. Efficient communication also ensures that resolutions have been made, and going forward, the warring parties are required to honor these resolutions. If workplace conflict is ignored, it could slowly, but surely, degenerate into a cataclysmic, full-out war that could spell the end of an individual’s career. It promotes company reputation Nothing is as valuable to a company as its reputation. The customers and employees of a company are either its ambassadors or adversaries. In this age of the Internet, it is especially critical that companies respect their clients and employees, for an unhappy customer or employee could easily tarnish the company’s name. One of the ways for a company to avoid losing its reputation is through investing in proper communication models. This ensures that information flows to the concerned parties. During a crisis, and every company goes through one, the management must send out appropriate information, both to customers and employees, so as to defend their reputation. It promotes transparency Some managers think that locking out employees from participating in the making of key decisions is beneficial to the company, when in fact it’s not. Obviously it comes down to the niche, but generally it’s always great to have your employees invested in the progress of the company. This not only increases their loyalty but also their morale and productivity. When a company is transparent, employees are very much aware of their roles, are self-driven and diligent, and this is what drives the company forward.

Chapter 3: Things that ruin effective workplace communication Anything that involves human beings must be flawed. It’s in our nature to mess up. When it comes to communication in the workplace, it doesn’t usually go without a glitch. There are many factors that ruin effective communication. Poor communication Poor communicators mislead the audience. Poor communication comes about as a result of not preparing. For instance, if you have an employee workshop coming up, you have to check and countercheck the dates, thee speakers, and precisely the employees that must be in attendance. But if the communicator doesn’t bother to verify the accuracy of their information, they might leave gaps, which lead to miscommunication. If the message is written, it has to be error free, especially grammatically and semantically. If it’s communication by word of mouth, the message has to be clear, and expressed with simple, descriptive words. The communicator must also avoid making personal attacks and stick to issues. Failure to listen Some people treat communication as a competitive sport whereby whoever speaks most is the winner. How wrong they are! Effective communication in the workplace, and virtually everywhere else, is about listening just as it is about speaking. Actually, the person that speaks less and listens longer enjoys the most benefits. These are some of the advantages of listening more than you speak: 1. You will think through your message: when you think through everything you intend to say, you sound clear and intelligent, as you are likely to leave out the unnecessary parts you might have said had you not thought through. 2. You will understand the other individual’s point of view: communication is about more than speaking yourself hoarse. It is also about listening to what the other person is saying. Once you understand them, it becomes easier to reach a common ground. 3. You’ll be prepared with facts: nothing makes an argument more compelling than having facts. But it takes quiet consideration to separate facts from delusions. And when you start rolling the facts out one by one, your audience will trust you. 4. Improved relationships: by listening to others, you have the chance to learn new things about them and understand what they are about at a deeper level. 5. Appreciation: by having the patience to listen to others, especially when you have a top managerial position, it will boost your charisma, and both employees and colleagues will view you in a positive light. Ego This is the biggest factor. If someone has an ego the size of a mountain, and won’t stop coming at people, they needlessly frustrate the team, and cause people to become ungovernable. A manager who has a big ego will antagonize his juniors and incite rebellion in the form of ignoring or paying partial attention to communication from the top. Key employees, who might probably be seduced by other companies, will bolt out at the first chance, and soon the company will have no talent. An ego also spills out into the public and customers take you for a person that means them harm. Thus, it is important to always check your ego, especially if you take a managerial position. Rivalry Rivalry, or negative competition, takes place when competing parties each want to succeed at the expense of the other, thus forcing them to be vicious to one another. When this takes place, there’s a falling out, and parties align themselves according to their selfish needs, leading to withholding of key information and even deliberately misleading others. Once you notice that a colleague is interested in starting a negative competition against you, handle the situation by doing the following: 1. Enlighten them: sometimes, you have to be bold in order to get things straight. Approach your fellow worker and have them know what their actions imply. Try to form a positive relationship with them so that they no longer see you as a threat. 2. Stay focused: it is so easy to become frustrated by the negative competition and lose focus on your work. But you must stay fixed to the completion of your goals. 3. Talk to management: if the situation frustrates you, then approach your boss and have them know what’s going on. Micromanagement One of the goals of effective communication in the workplace is to grant workers more control. Workers want to feel that they are running the show, and not as if they are dispensable. Thus, when a higher authority starts micromanaging employees, they react by resenting the authority. Micromanagement is an indication that the management doesn’t trust the workers to perform. Also, micromanagement might be an indication that the management suspects workers of ill motives. Thus, workers won’t be comfortable when they are micromanaged, and this causes problems in the flow of information. Instead of micromanaging employees, the management ought to delegate tasks to employees, and then demand regular updates on the progress. Endless criticism Another factor that ruins effective workplace communication is constant criticism. This is whereby the managers feel as though their employees are not good enough. It is even worse when managers criticize failure but they don’t praise success. The workers feel unappreciated. Constant criticism has a negative effect on the workers morale and confidence and it usually results in declining performance. In worst case scenarios, it causes employees to become depressed. Now, when you are the source of so much pain, employees will try as much as they can to avoid you, and also resist your ideas, thus frustrating your communication efforts. Unreasonable expectations Unreasonable expectations are another factor that ruins effective communication in the workplace. It shows that you are not thinking realistically. If workers realize that you are not realistic, they will give less thought to any information originating from you, for they know that they can’t fulfill your expectations. And this of course leads to friction down the road. For instance, if you come up with a complex idea, and ask your employees to make something of it within a very short window, you will cause them to resent you. Once resentment sets in, respect is the next casualty, and it becomes for workers to cooperate with you again. Stubbornness It’s always a nice thing to stand for what you believe in. But when you gain a reputation as a stubborn person, then it will become a bit hard for you to survive in a teamwork environment, for everyone will resist you even before they have heard your proposals. If you are not self-aware, you might be stubborn and not even realize it. The following are some of the instances that indicate you are needlessly being stubborn: 1. You stick to an idea even when evidence shows you are wrong: although defending your ideas can be a good thing, when taken to extremes it comes across as narrow-mindedness. Also, when you fail to admit you are wrong when the evidence is clear shows that you are uncooperative, which can be an impediment to success in both business and personal life. 2. Doing something against your group’s wish or judgment: when you go against the grain, understand that you will meet resistance. Even if you succeed with your plans, people will still be resentful of you and try to shun you. 3. Criticizing every idea: it’s okay to find fault in one or two of your groups ideas. But when you criticize every last idea, you will start to seem like an enemy, and this will keep you from making any progress. So, ensure that you are a supportive team member. 4. You get mad when others try to correct you: this smacks of arrogance and a giant ego, and it is a sure way of not only frustrating communication, but breeding hatred. If people realize that you are misled, and they try to help out, only to be met with insults, they will certainly view you in a bad light. 5. You agree to do something when you know you will do something else: it is a form of disrespect, but it shows others what you are: a dishonest, cowardly, and untrustworthy person. Making assumptions Effective communication is about passing information accurately. But for some odd reason someone might not get the original meaning and end up having doubts, but instead of following up with a question to clear doubt, they may make assumptions, that usually mislead them, thus defeating the purpose of communication. Always be sure that you have gotten the precise message and if you have any doubts then it’s upon you to seek clarity. Distractions Effective communication takes place in an environment with little to no stimulation. When something else takes place it is bound to distract people, and keep them from getting the message precisely. So, it is upon the involved parties to ensure that there are no distractions during communication. Some of the common distractions include: 1. Usage of mobile phones 2. Interrupting the speaker by e.g. launching a counter-argument 3. Paying attention to other things e.g. chatting or clipping fingernails

Chapter 4: Body language tips Our body language says more than words or text ever could. It’s all that the world needs to see to form an opinion of you. But sadly, we are so poor at body language that we end up giving our insecurities away. Having the right body language is indicative of self-confidence, and people will always react positively toward a confident person. Self-confidence is critical for communicating effectively. The following are some of the things you might want to do for the sake of your body language. Smile more often When you put that smile on your face, you send the message to your brain that you are having a pleasant moment, and the brain in turn makes you feel genuinely happy. It doesn’t matter what you are going through, but a smile is an instant mood-booster. When you give out information with a smile on your face people are likely to believe you and also cooperate with you. This is because people are naturally attracted toward people that demonstrate positivity. A smile is the biggest communicator of positive energy. Thus, putting on a smile sends out the message that you have a positive mindset and people are drawn to you. If you are not used to smiling, it can be a bit challenging to start off. You only have to practice on the regular, say, every morning, until it becomes second nature. Keep an erect posture Nothing gives away your confidence levels more than your posture. If you are slouching it means that you are low in confidence. And if you stand upright it means that you have healthy self-confidence. Assuming a poor posture might send out the wrong signal and cause your audience to tune out. Having an erect posture is primarily about shifting your weight to the balls of your feet, keeping your feet slightly apart, holding your arms by the sides of your torso, and standing straight with shoulders held back. Research shows that assuming the right posture leads to an increase in energy levels, as well as lung capacity, and thus your voice comes out firmer, and you appear confident in your abilities. Give strong handshakes Obviously, not all communication opportunities will require that you meet people, let alone shake their hands. But if you intend to share information with a tight circle in the workplace, you may have to approach them physically. One of the most important things to keep in mind is to have a strong handshake. Nothing says “low energy” or “low confidence” more than a limp handshake. But you want people to think of you as a confident person so that they can respect your time and contribution. Giving a strong handshake doesn’t mean that you have to try to be macho. You should actually establish eye contact and smile while shaking their hand. Improve your fashion sense They say, “Never judge a book by its cover,” but that doesn’t apply to us humans, for we most certainly judge people by their looks. If someone is poorly dressed, we form a low opinion of them, and if someone is dressed to impress, we think them high status. Having a fashion sense isn’t necessarily about wearing the latest clothes. It’s about being aware of the clothes that compliment your body type, and dressing for an event. Generally, impressive clothes are nicely tailored and fitting. Sometimes it’s okay to stretch the limits, but then it matters how you handle yourself: always keep your head high. Appear comfortable in whatever you are wearing by having a smile ready. Break the habit of fidgeting If you have a habit of fidgeting, people will think that you are not sure of your abilities. Splaying your fingers, playing with your hair, and biting your nails all indicate that you are trying to suppress your shock and anxiety. Fidgeting is a distraction, for it takes the attention away from the actual conversation. If you realize that you fidget a lot while speaking, there are a number of things you could to fight away this habit: 1. Speak with your hands: as you speak, try to make subtle motions with your hands, and you won’t have to fidget anymore. 2. Fold or clasp your hands: you may also fold your hands and hold them against your body. Or clasp your hands together every time you are speaking. Remind yourself constantly to undertake this exercise until the habit sticks. 3. Take heavy breaths: obviously, anxiety is the major trigger behind fidgeting. So, how about treating the root case? Taking deep breaths has long been shown to be an effective method of fighting away anxiety. It calms you down, helping you stop fidgeting. Establish eye contact Learn to look at people direct in their eyes. It means that you are both sincere and confident. It also increases your intimacy, causing your message to hit home. If you are not used to looking people in the eyes, it can be pretty challenging at first, but with constant practice, it should come naturally. If you are uncomfortable looking at the eyeballs of another person, you can just look at the area between their eyes. But then don’t become a creep or try to establish dominance. Look away from time to time, and do it slowly. Establishing eye contact also makes you appear more charismatic. Take up space Some people have this hideous habit of shrinking themselves when they are on the spotlight. But rarely anyone takes them seriously because they look ridiculous. In order to be taken seriously, you must project competence, which is still confidence, and one of the ways of doing that is taking up space. So, spread out your hands, stand tall, put your shoulders at ease and from time to time adjust and readjust your limbs. It sends out the message that you are unafraid. But more importantly, it makes people comfortable, and causes them to treat you respectfully, which means they are bound to listen actively. Nod when someone else is speaking As we said, communication is two-way. You have to be willing to listen actively just as much as you are willing to speak. One of the methods of demonstrating active listening is through constant nods. By nodding regularly while the other person speaks, it is an indication that you are super attentive, which is a great encouragement to the speaker. But then, in as much as nodding is cool, please don’t overdo it. Know when to stop. Lean subtly If the speaker delves into something that really piques your interest, you might want to lean forward slightly. But be sure not to lean in too much lest you appear desperate for approval. If you want to appear cool, you can always lean back slightly and give the occasional nod. Mirror their actions Psychology teaches us that when we are greatly impressed by someone, we tend to mirror their actions, albeit subconsciously. If you find the speaker interesting, you don’t have to wait for your subconscious mind to catch up. You may very well begin to mirror the actions of the other person. If they lean in, then do so, and if they put their hands on their thighs, wait for a moment, and then follow suit. It strengthens your ties with the speaker and causes them to give their all. Use the right tone Your voice is an excellent indicator of your confidence and energy levels. A weak voice points to someone with low self-esteem, and a strong voice indicates high confidence. Obviously, there are people with small voices and high superior confidence levels, but those are an exception. In order to sound great, you don’t particularly have to thunder, or shout yourself hoarse, but reserve the right tone for everything that you are saying. For instance, if you are talking about something fun, use an upbeat voice, and if you are talking of a regretful or sad event, use a calm tone. Appear focused You could be having a wonderful posture and appear not intimidated at all, but then have a wandering mind, which spoils it all. Ensure that you are not throwing your eyes to every corner every few seconds. It can actually make people wary. Like, what are you up to? In order to seem focused, ensure that your eyes are fixed at the speaker, or your face is at least facing their direction. Your facial expression should reflect what the speaker is talking about. For instance, if he’s trudging his way through a complex concept, you should appear thoughtful, and if he’s telling a joke, you should have a light facial expression, as if ready to laugh out at the punch line. Don’t invade other people’s space When you are talking to people, there are some simple rules that you can easily forget. For instance, you can forget to stay within your boundaries, and decide to encroach on other people’s space. It is a bit creepy when you come too close to another person. So always ensure that there’s a healthy space between you and the other people (or person)

Chapter 5: Improving conversation skills Sending emails, and texts, and making calls are all methods of communication. But it is only through conversations that we can connect with other people in a sincere way. But the problem is that not many of us are really good at making conversation. We fumble through sentences and find ourselves not making any sense, which makes us appear uncharismatic. The following are some tips to help you become better at conversations: Speak more slowly It is difficult to make any sense when you are speaking very fast and yet you lack the mental alertness of what you are saying. This puts you at risk of making errors and sounding fake. If you are trying to boost your charisma and conversation skills, start by learning to speak in a slow fashion. Apart from having the time to think through what you are saying, speaking slowly makes you seem like a calm and confident person, which is the ultimate projection of confidence. Chapter 13: Practice active listenin g We go through countless meetings in our workplace on a weekly basis. The purpose of those of these meetings is obviously to pass some message across. But the big question is whether or not this message actually reaches its intended audience. It takes active listening to be able to capture the message just as it was intended, thus there are no misunderstandings, and no potential conflict. Here are some of the methods of engaging in active listening: Face the speaker You can hardly be an active listener when you are facing away. You have to look at the speaker directly so that you are able to take in every word and nuance. There are very many nonverbal messages that a speaker sends out and unless your eyes are on them, you would miss out. Ensure that you look at the speaker in the eyes. But be careful that you don’t overdo it. Excessive eye contact can seem intimidating. Establishing eye contact makes sure that you get the whole message and also it makes the speaker feel appreciated. Nothing would heartbreak a speaker faster than talking to people who were looking elsewhere but him. Assume an erect posture First off, your posture says a lot about you. If you have a poor posture then it means you are low in confidence and the vice versa is true. An erect posture makes it easy for you to listen actively, for your eyes can now face forward. Avoid crossing arms over your chest or locking legs. It is a gesture that signifies that you don’t want to be disturbed, which is an unfriendly thing to do in a public place. To show the speaker that you are indeed following their every word, you might want to lean in forward slightly, or tilt your head a bit as you support it with a palm. Apart from boosting your self-confidence and helping you practice active listening, an erect posture is beneficial for health purposes. It will help with back problems and also boost blood flow. Never interrupt Interrupting the speaker is virtually the rudest thing you can do. It shows that you have no time for what they are saying, or that you are more important than everybody else, assuming there are other people listening. Interrupting the speaker might upset their train of thought and thus ruin their message. You want to ensure that you are cooperating with the speaker and giving them respect. Some issues might be too pressing and you feel as though you can’t wait to raise them. The appropriate way of raising a concern is to make a note of all the issues and then waiting for the speaker to be done. This shows that you respect them and you are more likely to have a great relationship with them. Avoid jumping into conclusions or judging People frustrate their efforts of listening actively by constantly jumping into conclusions. This habit can lead to miscommunication and give rise to potential conflict. Ensure that you listen to the speaker until they are done. Jumping into conclusions indicates lack of patience as well as immaturity on the part of the listener. Equally as bad is the habit of judging others. A speaker might decide to tell a story that touches on their lives, but the listener might decide to start weighing the story in morality scales, trying to determine the moral conscience of the speaker. This habit not only takes away from an individual’s capacity to practice active listening but also reduces them into a quasi-morality preacher. Avoid preparing a counter-attack Some people cannot listen actively because they don’t listen to understand but they listen to merely give a response. For such people, communication is like a sport whereby the winner takes it all, and they want to be the winner and revel in that glory. When you are planning to launch a counter-attack as soon as the speaker is done, it means that you didn’t have sufficient concentration when the speaker was talking. Chances are you didn’t get the whole facts or you heard them in a skewed manner, thus opening up gaps for conflict. Active listening is about being attentive until the speaker is done. Then you can start to say whatever is on your mind. Encourage the speaker Nothing bothers a speaker more than having to talk to someone or a group that is totally unreceptive. It makes the speaker lose morale and stop putting enough effort into passing their message across. There are a number of things you can do to encourage the speaker. Some of these things include: 1. Nodding: this is a classic way of showing the speaker that you are on the same page as them. From time to time, and when you lock eyes, give them a slight nod. But you want to be sure that you are economical with the nods lest it appears as though you are trying too hard to impress the speaker. 2. Smiling: this is a way of showing the speaker friendliness. It makes them comfortable. Think about how you’d feel if you started speaking with someone and or a group and then they wore a hostile face? You’d feel unwelcome, right? But if they had a smile you’d feel welcome. So, learn to smile regularly at the speaker as it shows him that you are their friend, and it inspires them to keep on. 3. Say “Yes” or “Uh-uh” and other affirming sounds: mouthing off such sounds makes the speaker proud of himself for attracting people’s attention, and more importantly, their full engagement. 4. Don’t look at the watch every thirty seconds: we tend to frantically look at our watches when we are stuck at a place and just can’t seem to wait to leave. When the speaker realizes that people are looking at their watches quiet frequently, they can assume that they are boring, which hurts their feelings. It is enough to look at the watch just once and keep reminding yourself not look again for it’s too soon. 5. Avoid fidgeting: why would you fidget? It’s the speaker doing the talking, not you. Does your fidgeting stem from your nervous energy? But why is that? On the part of the speaker, such behavior is as much disturbing as it is discouraging. Don’t offer solutions unless asked to If a colleague comes up to you and sobs about their challenges, your main function ought to be holding them steady and listening to them keenly. You must not attempt to say, “Oh, do this and that -” The rules of active listening demand that you only give guidance and solutions upon request. When you start giving away unsolicited advice you ignore the wishes of the imagined victim. If you stop wasting time trying to come up with a solution for the numerous troubles of the speaker, you would have sufficient time to listen to actually what’s being said, to understand the message, regardless of its pain. Be focused Another critical facet of active listening is staying focused throughout. There are many things, both external and internal, that might try to grab your attention, but shun them off and stick to listening. Don’t expend your mental resources fighting automatic thoughts (the thoughts that seem to overpower your will) but just take on the role of the observer, for eventually these thoughts will fade away. When you are focused at what the speaker is saying you have a decent chance of hearing the entire message as was intended. This means communication has been carried out in a flawless manner. Be curious and creative When you practice active listening, you are bound to capture the message accurately, as opposed to the person who’s distracted, or who allowed himself to be distracted. But then if you are the curious type, or the creative type, you should see more than one perspective to whatever the speaker says, and thus, you should have a question. Such curious behavior goes a long way in starting and cementing ties between the two parties. Be open minded This green orange – hung in space – whose name is Earth is quite a large, large thing. In it there lives all manner of people and creatures. Human beings are different. There is so much variety like you wouldn’t believe. For that reason, it would be unfair for any culture, or distinct group to declare themselves as more worthy than any other culture or group. Thus, when you are listening to a speaker you should have an open mind. Since you are probably unfamiliar with their background, you might not be aware of their deep convictions and philosophies, so keep an open mind and be accepting of whatever they say as long as it is within boundaries of respect. Actually, this attitude is great for creativity. It helps you have a multifaceted conception of ideas and things.

Chapter 14: How to resolve workplace conflicts There’s no workplace without its fair share of conflict. As long as workers have different expectations – plus throw in the moods – conflict is a guarantee. But this doesn’t mean that conflict should ground a company to a halt. If anything, conflict should help colleagues know more about each other, and develop strong between one another, so that in the long run they become a tight family that cares about each other. The following are critical steps for resolving conflict: Take action before it’s too late Most workplace conflicts don’t just come about at the snap of a finger. It is usually a culmination of squabbles, incremental resentment, simmering hatred, and other nasty feelings that combine and burst out at the slightest trigger. Most people ignore the buildup to the real conflict and survive that phase by being mostly passive aggressive. But you don’t want to be one of these people. Once you realize that you have started to make the conditions right for a storm in your workplace, take action before you run out of time. Look at your role in starting the conflict and the role of the other person too and then invite them to bury the hatchet. You could easily go from the worst enemies to the best friends ever. Talk with your “enemy” Unless you are the type that avoids the person that caused you pain, but you should consider talking to the source of your troubles, for there may be a small chance that the situation could be salvaged. Maybe there was an omission or maybe you misunderstood one another. And in the event that the conflict still rages on, with no remedy in sight, still talking with your adversary might trigger a reconciliatory mood. The conflicting parties might be taken aback by how determined they are to ruin one another and take a joint decision of salvaging their condition. This may cause them, for instance, to forgive one another and promise to get rid of their expectations or standards and to work together. Such reconciliations tend to be powerful and sincere. But you cannot get there until you are first willing to talk to the other person. Look for a mediator In a worst-case scenario, you could be dealing with an adversary that is pretty ruthless and uncooperative. There are no proposals you would make that could calm their hunger for a fight. It seems all they want is a conflict. In such a situation, you should look for a new hand to handle the situation. Invite a mediator to talk between you and your adversary. The mediator can be your colleague or even a professional. But their sole task is to bring peace between you and your relentless adversary. Cut out the threats Some people unwittingly escalate a conflict by issuing threats, thinking that threats would deter their adversary from starting a conflict, but then they find out that their adversary just became even nastier. Let’s say you are a team and have been tasked with developing a software upgrade by a certain date. The conflict arises from the fact that you are doing most of the work as your teammate sits around. You want him to work. Or else you might want to have his name scraped and have the project credited to you. Threat: “Dude, you only have 48 hours! I want you to reach the set milestones on our assignment! If you fail to do that I will make sure that your name is removed from the project!” You might find that such a threat would make your adversary even more determined in resisting your wishes. This is because such a threat hits out at their ego. And they are desperate to protect their fragile ego. Obviously it’s unfair that your adversary shuns responsibility and leaves it up to you to finish the project and yet he wants to claim 50% credit. Focus on the trigger, and don’t attack personalities Once a conflict is ongoing, effort has to be made to not only resolve the conflict but keep it from degenerating into a worse state. This usually takes place when one conflicting party is unwilling to compromise or “still keeps coming” i.e. looking for trouble. In order to avoid making the conflict worse, ensure that you focus on the issue that sparked your conflict. For instance, if the source if your conflict had been an assignment, focus on the assignment and how everybody handled it as opposed to antagonizing your “enemy” and shining light at their character flaws. This would hurt their ego and make them less willing to cooperate and resolve their conflict. Highlight the areas where you agree on It is easier to resolve a conflict by highlighting areas that you both agree on than focusing on what sets you apart. This takes a great deal of self-confidence and maturity. Let your adversary see that you can agree on things. And try to lead him into seeing the sense behind your reasoning. Of course you won’t go anywhere if they are the unbreakable type. But in the same breath, you should too be willing to change your stand if the other party supplies overwhelming evidence. Highlighting the things that you both agree on is a major step in resolving conflict for it firstly gets the warring parties to speak to one another and this step also gives everybody a chance to be sure that they know what they are standing for. Compromise The major problem is that both you and your adversary want to be right. It cannot happen. The fastest and most effective method of overcoming your differences is through reaching a compromise. This requires that you first ditch the need to be right. And then you have to rethink your expectations. If the other party is cooperative, they will be willing to cut back on their expectations as well, so that both of you can meet halfway. Then both of you have to be willing to change, and more specifically, you have to adjust your attitudes. Once the conflict has been resolved, the previously warring parties ought to share between one another about their beliefs and philosophies and the major events that led them to adopt those beliefs. This goes a long way in understanding how the mind of the other person operates. You could be surprised to empathize with your former adversary when you hear their circumstances. Moving on, the former adversaries must make time to nurture their partnership, and they must not avoid each other out of awkwardness or shame. Nothing shows maturity more than the ability to reach a compromise. Apologize So, if you have been at the center of a conflict, and have resolved your differences, but the consensus was that you were at fault, you still have to apologize to everybody else for being such a drama queen or drama king. So, wear your sheepish smile and look at your colleagues, moving from one person to the next, and express how sorry you are to have put them through such drama. If you can’t bring yourself to apologize in person, which is most sincere, then you can write them an email, although it is likely to be read with scorn. For your apology to hit home, there are some critical things you must include. 1. Express remorse: you have to start like someone who’s done something to be ashamed of or something totally unnecessary. Thus, you have to start with phrases like, ‘I’m sorry,” or “I apologize”. If you start out with a casual toe you might make it seem like you are not sorry at all, and your colleagues will think you are not serious. 2. Admit responsibility: don’t try to start weaseling your way out of being responsible for the ugly scene that your colleague just witnessed. Don’t try to pass yourself off as a victim. Instead, admit that you are responsible for the ugly things that went down. 3. Show that you have made amends: explain how you have resolved the conflict and that now everything is as it should be. 4. Promise not to repeat the mistake: you must show colleagues that being the mastermind of a conflict is not exactly the greatest achievement of your life, thus you would never want to relieve it. It is important that you have a remorseful facial expression so as to make your colleagues believe that you are sorry. Celebrate progress If a colleague who’s notorious for behaving badly finally shows considerable character improvement, then they are worthy of being celebrated. The thing in our workplaces we are diverse people. And we come from varied backgrounds. We tend to imagine that just because we grew up with a loving dad and mom and two brothers in a sweet safe townhouse… that the story is the same for everybody else. Wrong! Some of your colleagues were probably abused as kids and they have endured all sorts of pain and their repulsive character might be in part a shout out to the abuse they have gone through. These people have talents and multiple skills and it is upon us to include them, not shun them. But make no mistake; this is not to say that anyone who ever underwent abuse during their childhood has the right to upset their workplace; by no means! Actually, they better not! Because punishment awaits them! And we will only feel sorry for them once they have drunk of the bitter cup of the consequences of illicit behavior!

Chapter 15: Bonding with colleagues Can you imagine what life in the workplace would be life if everybody was hostile to everybody else? Nothing would go on, right? Thus, it is essential for colleagues to form tight professional relationships. Once colleagues have a favorable attitude toward one another, it becomes far easier to reach milestones, and accomplish company objectives. The following are some of the practices that strengthen the bond between colleagues: Know your colleagues It may sound obvious, but not many people actually follow observe the tip. It’s common to find workers ignoring each other and burying themselves into their tasks, with a “Don’t disturb” attitude written all over. But this is usually the first step toward establishing tight bonds. Workers ought to approach other workers and want to know about them. It is in no way a violation of their privacy but just an effort to get to know their coworkers. This problem appears to be common in this generation. People of long ago, when technology was at the infant stage, didn’t have gadgets to offer them an escape window from reality, and they had no choice but practice their social skills. But in the current world, it is unsurprising to find a whole adult with zero communication and social skills. But back to the point, people ought to be active in wanting to know about their colleagues, and creating close ties. Do you share common interests? When two people share a common interest they are likely to become too fond of one another. It’s a biological thing. We are wired to react positively toward the people that share our traits. Thus, if you want to capitalize on this factor, take the risk and go asking around what they like doing. Should you come upon someone that shares your interests, it means you have a bigger potential of establishing close ties with them. But be sure that you don’t sound like a spy. It has to be subtle, and if they resist, then back off, only to ask again some other day. Some of the subtle ways to ask a colleague about their interests include: noticing their recent wardrobe addition, their recent shoes, smile, and energy levels. Develop trust Every rewarding relationship is founded on trust. A professional relationship ought not to be different. You should start out by showing other people that you trust them. For instance, you can leave money and other possessions in an area that your fellow colleagues can see. Do they tell you about it or do they just keep mum. Obviously this is a corrupted way of finding out who’s to be trusted or not. But it is very important to have a close-knight group of colleagues where everyone feels equal. Relationships are work and it obviously takes a lot of sacrifice to make an office group tight and dependable. Be positive When you are preparing your work relationships, you have to be a person that constantly spits out positivity. People need to have a positive mindset in order for a relationship to flourish. Being positive is simply focusing on the bright side of things. Positive people are aware that they hold power and they don’t indulge in self-inhibiting tendencies. But they act with confidence, as they go for their important life goals. Positivity is like a muscle. You have to replenish it on the regular lest you slip. Engage in activities that build your positive energy and make better utilization of your free time. It is relatively easy to have everyone’s cooperation when doing something for the company, but when it comes to supporting an individual’s personal goals, there’s nearly no enthusiasm on the part of colleagues. Of course it reflects badly on workers. Workers should be their own biggest supporters! If one of you has a project, find a way of giving it a boost, and invest in your colleague’s success story. Obviously this is a tall order because most people are selfish and want to engage in things that directly benefit them. Share In order to strengthen your professional relationship with other colleagues, you must get into the habit of sharing. You have to share ideas, plans, and even stories. This habit promotes integration and the more you spend time together the tighter your relationship becomes. Obviously, it is important that you focus on sharing about positive things, so that people can draw inspiration from that and improve their lives. In this age of advanced technology, you can share messages in various formats like videos and even put categories to these messages. Ensure that you label them for easy retrieval. Work on your social skills This is an area that people at the workplace need to really invest in. human beings are social animals. We tend to respect those who can fit in within our social structures. But then there’s so much nervous energy floating around whenever people do a simple meet up. Does it mean that most of us lack social skills? Certainly! But the good thing is that social skills aren’t inborn traits. Anyone can acquire these skills as long as they practice.
