**How to Deliver Effective Presentations?**

  

Delivering presentations is an everyday art form that anyone can master. To
capture your audience's attention, present your information with ease and
confidence. Act as if you are in a conversation with your audience, and they
will pay attention to you. To get this level of fluency, write an engaging
narrative, use more visuals than text in your slides, and practice, practice,
practice.

译文：进行演讲是一种日常艺术，任何人都可以掌握。要吸引观众的注意力，您需要轻松自如地展示信息，充满自信。表现得像是在与观众对话一样，他们自然会专注于您。要达到这种流畅度，首先要写一个引人入胜的叙述，幻灯片中使用比文字更多的视觉元素，并不断练习、练习、再练习。

**1、Effective-有效**

Give it the "bar" test. The "bar" test ensures that your idea is clear and
your storytelling is effective. Before you write your presentation, make notes
sketching out your message. Think of it as a story, with a beginning, middle,
and end. Then ask a colleague or friend who knows nothing about your project
to listen to your summary.

·Deliver your summary to them in friendly, direct language, as if you were
telling the story to a friend in a bar.

·In fact, you can tell the story to a friend in a bar. However, telling a
colleague over coffee can work just as well.

·Get them to tell you what their takeaway was. If they can summarize your
message accurately, that's a good sign.

  

译文：进行“酒吧”测试。“酒吧”测试能确保你的想法清晰，并且你的故事讲述有效。在编写演讲之前，先做一些笔记，勾勒出你的信息。把它看作一个故事，有开始、中间和结尾。然后请一个对你的项目一无所知的同事或朋友听你的总结。

以友好、直接的语言向他们传达你的总结，就像在酒吧里给朋友讲故事一样。

实际上，你可以在酒吧里给朋友讲这个故事。不过，在喝咖啡时向同事讲述也同样有效。

让他们告诉你他们的收获是什么。如果他们能准确总结你的信息，那就是一个好的信号。

**2、Practice-练习**

  

P  ractice your speech in front of a colleague while you're still developing
it. Find a friend, colleague, or mentor who you trust to give you useful
feedback. Rather than waiting until your presentation is "finished," deliver
it to them while it's still a draft. That way, they can give you tips not just
on your delivery, but on the organization of your content as well.

·Ask them to be your coach.

·Give them your presentation once or twice and let them ask you questions and
give feedback.

·Ask them to point out moments that are dull or confusing.

译文：
在演讲还在准备阶段时，在同事面前练习演讲。找一个你信任的朋友、同事或导师，他们可以给你有用的反馈。不要等到演讲“完成”后再给他们展示，而是在还处于草稿阶段时进行。这种方式不仅能让他们对你的表达方式提出建议，还可以帮助你优化内容的组织。

· 请他们成为你的教练。

· 把演讲给他们讲一到两次，让他们提问并给予反馈。

· 请他们指出哪些部分显得沉闷或令人困惑。

**3、Prepare-准备**

  

Prepare for nerves. It's important to rehearse in front of people because it
will make you less nervous when the time comes. If you tend to get nervous
when you give presentations, you can go further during your rehearsals in
order to reduce your nerves.

·Write down what you're afraid of. What exactly worries you when you give a
speech? Looking foolish? Being asked a hard question? Write down your exact
fears, and then consider them each individually.

·Think about what you will do in each situation. For instance, if your fear
is, "I'll forget what I'm saying," you can prepare a plan like, "If I forget
what I'm saying, I'll pause, scan my notes, and find the next important point
I need to make."

·Catch your negative thoughts, and calm them. If you think, "I'm going to get
nervous and sweaty," replace it with, "I have important information to deliver
and everyone is going to pay attention to that.

译文：为紧张做好准备。在人们面前排练很重要，因为这会让你在  真正演讲时减少紧张。如果你在演讲时容易紧张，可以在排练时进一步努力，来减轻焦虑。

写下你害怕的东西。你在演讲时到底担心什么？是害怕显得愚蠢？还是害怕被问到难题？把你的具体恐惧写下来，然后逐一考虑它们。

想想你在每种情况下该怎么做。例如，如果你的恐惧是“我会忘记我要说什么”，你可以准备一个计划，比如“如果我忘记我要说的内容，我会暂停一下，翻阅我的笔记，找到我需要讲的下一个重要点”。

抓住你的负面想法，并平复它们。如果你想的是“我会紧张出汗”，就把它换成“我有重要的信息要传达，大家都会专注于这一点”。

**4、Time-计时**

  

Time yourself carefully. You don't want to go over your time frame and get cut
off, or wear on the patience of your audience. Time your rehearsals,
especially those you give in front of others, to see that you are staying
within the time you have.[4]Give yourself extra time if you plan to take
questions, or if you anticipate lots of digressions.

译文：仔细计时。你不想超出时间范围被打断，也不想消耗观众的耐心。给自己排练计时，尤其是在别人面前的排练，确保你在规定时间内完成演讲。

如果你打算接受提问，或者预期会有很多偏离话题的情况，给自己预留一些额外的时间。

**5、Repeat-重复**

  

Practice repeatedly. Try practicing in front of your dog, your mirror, or your
family. Practice speaking with an engaging tone. Gesture and emote as if you
were addressing your audience. Know your presentation backward and
forward.This doesn't mean sticking to a strict script every time. Instead,
when you rehearse, improvise freely. Deliver your main points, but include
quips and anecdotes that occur to you as you go. You'll remember the best ones
when you actually deliver the presentation.

译文：反复练习。试着在你的狗、镜子或家人面前练习。用富有感染力的语调说话。做出手势并表达情感，就像你在面对观众一样。要对你的演讲内容了然于胸。

这并不意味着每次都要严格遵守稿子。相反，在排练时，尽量自由发挥。传达你的主要观点，同时加入你想到的妙语和故事。当你正式演讲时，你会记住那些最好的内容。
